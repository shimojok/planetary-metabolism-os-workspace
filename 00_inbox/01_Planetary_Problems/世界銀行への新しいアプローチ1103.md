Over the past three years, I have researched and exchanged opinions on food assistance and agricultural development investments in Africa conducted by the World Bank, FAO, agricultural and environmental foundations, and agricultural research institutions.

IFAD's agricultural development projects and the World Bank's agricultural development initiatives in Sierra Leone, Ghana, and Senegal have achieved remarkable results, but there are still numerous challenges across vast Africa. In particular, addressing fundamental issues behind food and nutrition challenges in recent years, as well as rising food prices in countries like Nigeria, is essential.

The FAO and many aid organizations promote agroforestry and no-till farming, but these approaches will not bring about improvement. These methods address only partial aspects of fundamental ecological processes, and it seems they do not understand the mechanisms that need to be implemented.

The AGRIX Project I am planning seeks to tackle degraded soil, food issues in developing countries, food systems under climate change, and global warming through technologies backed by ecological processes and mechanisms for global environmental conservation. The AGRIX platform utilizes advanced data analysis to enhance the functionality of the MBT55 microbial consortium. Through deployment by local agricultural corporations, the MBT Sustainable Cycle will transform processed food waste, livestock excrement, plant residues, and marine waste into compost and organic carbon in 24 hours, stabilizing food prices by improving food production and enabling domestic production of fertilizers.

The key to realizing the AGRIX Project is collaboration with Amazon. In addition to the AGRIX Project, I plan to transfer the HealthBook project in healthcare and the MBT Herbal Probiotics business concept, which will become Amazon's fourth pillar, and secure funding. This would enable Amazon to expand into the food business, integrated healthcare platforms, life insurance with survival benefits, and personalized probiotics, broadening its customer base and significantly increasing its corporate value.

Furthermore, the AGRIX Project aims not only at agricultural development in Africa but also at building food systems under climate change and establishing multilateral food security, which will move Amazon’s food business to a new stage.

Additionally, I am working toward building multilateral food security bases and climate-adaptive food production bases called AquaTowers in countries like Nigeria, Togo, Gambia, Zambia, and Kenya. This framework will also establish a multipurpose fair-trade-based food system with Japan, where the food self-sufficiency rate is low. I anticipate that the Japanese government will be involved in this framework, with support from J-Credit.

The World Bank and FAO/IFAD should be highly interested in this framework.
