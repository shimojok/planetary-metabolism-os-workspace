Policy Proposal Title

“Redefining Regenerative Agriculture: Implementing an Accelerated Circular Bio-Geo-Chemical System through MBT55 & MBT Sustainable Cycle”


---

Executive Summary

A brief overview highlighting the limitations of current regenerative agriculture frameworks, introducing MBT55 as an Ecological Hypercycle Engine, and proposing MBT Sustainable Cycle as a scalable and scientific approach to food security, environmental restoration, and resource circulation.


---

1. The Current Misinterpretation of Regenerative Agriculture

Lack of systems-level biological and ecological understanding

Overreliance on passive soil recovery methods

Low productivity, high transition costs, and limited scalability



---

2. The Bio-Geo-Chemical Reality of Earth’s Ecosystem

Explanation of Earth as a circular, zero-waste biochemical system

Role of microbial metabolic pathways and symbiotic plant interactions

The necessity of hypercycle models for modern agroecology



---

3. MBT55: The Ecological Hypercycle Engine

High-temperature aerobic fermentation (80–100°C) in 24 hours

Complete decomposition of organic waste

Production of stable humic substances (critical for carbon sequestration and nutrient cycling)



---

4. MBT Sustainable Cycle: A Holistic Agricultural Regeneration Platform

Transformation of food/agricultural waste into functional humus

Closed-loop model integrating soil enhancement, waste reduction, and microbial biodiversity

Demonstrated improvements in yield, quality, and shelf life with cost reduction



---

5. Why the Global South Needs This Model

Unsuitability of current regenerative models for African food production

Empowering smallholder farmers as revenue-generating food producers

Cost-effective, locally producible, scalable solution with measurable ecological gains



---

6. Policy Recommendations for FAO, UNEP, and World Bank

Redefine regenerative agriculture with scientific and ecological criteria

Pilot and support MBT55-based systems in priority regions (Africa, Asia, Latin America)

Integrate MBT Sustainable Cycle into climate finance, carbon credit systems, and SDGs



---

7. Conclusion: A Path Forward for Regenerative Food Systems

From slogans to systems science

Bridging microbial ecology and agro-economics

Toward an accelerated, circular, and resilient food production paradigm
