# 学校給食プロジェクトの概要

## 目的

- 学校給食における食材の自給率を高める
- 食品廃棄物や排泄物などの有機性廃棄物を堆肥化し、農業や園芸に活用する
- トイレの水洗化と節水を実現する
- サステナブルな資源循環システムを構築する

## 内容

- [食材はBioValley](https://www.ecoeiwa.co.jp/product/)[1](https://www.ecoeiwa.co.jp/product/)で余剰生産された野菜や果物、魚介類などを利用する
- [食品廃棄物や植物残渣、海産物残渣、家畜の排泄物などの有機性廃棄物はMBT発酵機](https://www.ecoeiwa.co.jp/product/)[2](https://www.ecoeiwa.co.jp/)によりメタンガスと堆肥に分解する
- メタンガスは発電や熱源に利用し、堆肥はBioValleyや学校の花壇などに散布する
- [トイレは無水水洗トレのAquaMake](http://www.yasugi.co.jp/aquamake.html)[3](http://www.yasugi.co.jp/aquamake.html)を導入し、トイレから排出される汚水を高度に処理して循環再利用する
- 排泄物もMBT発酵機に投入し、堆肥化する

## 効果

- 学校給食の食材の自給率が向上し、地産地消の促進や食育の推進に寄与する
- 有機性廃棄物の堆肥化により、土壌の改良や肥料の節約に貢献する
- メタンガスのエネルギー利用により、化石燃料の消費量やCO2排出量の削減に貢献する
- トイレの水洗化と節水により、水資源の保全や水道料金の節約に貢献する
- サステナブルな資源循環システムにより、環境保全や地域貢献に貢献する

以上が計画の概要です。ご参考になれば幸いです。😊

# Summary of the School Lunch Project

## Purpose
- To increase the self-sufficiency rate of ingredients for school lunches
- To compost organic waste such as food waste, plant residues, seafood residues, and animal excreta, and use them for agriculture and gardening
- To achieve water-saving and water-washing toilets, and compost the excreta
- To build a sustainable resource recycling system

## Content
- The ingredients are vegetables, fruits, seafood, etc. that are surplus produced in BioValley¹
- Organic waste such as food waste, plant residues, seafood residues, and animal excreta are decomposed into methane gas and compost by the MBT fermenter²
- Methane gas is used for power generation and heating, and compost is spread on BioValley and school flower beds
- Toilets are equipped with AquaMake³, a waterless water-washing toilet, which treats and recycles the wastewater discharged from the toilets
- Excreta are also fed into the MBT fermenter and composted

## Effects
- The self-sufficiency rate of ingredients for school lunches is improved, contributing to the promotion of local production and consumption and food education
- Composting of organic waste contributes to soil improvement and fertilizer saving
- Energy use of methane gas contributes to reducing fossil fuel consumption and CO2 emissions
- Water-saving and water-washing toilets contribute to water resource conservation and water bill saving
- A sustainable resource recycling system contributes to environmental protection and community contribution

This is the summary of the project. I hope you find it useful.

レポート

アフリカは食料と栄養の危機に直面している大陸です。紛争、気候変動、COVID-19、インフレーション、食料や肥料の価格の高騰などの影響で、飢餓や貧困が急激に増加しています。健康的な食事はほとんどの人にとって手に入れられないものになり、食料不安や栄養不良の人々が増えています。特に、サヘル地域やホーン・オブ・アフリカでは、飢餓の危機が深刻化しており、数百万人の人々が食料支援を必要としています¹²³⁴⁵。

アフリカでは、学校給食が教育の質と学習成果の向上に貢献すると考えられています。しかし、学校給食の実施には多くの課題があります。例えば、食材の調達、食事の準備、衛生管理、栄養教育などです。世界食糧計画（WFP）は、アフリカの多くの国で、学校給食に関する制度的能力強化を行っています。具体的には、学校給食の政策・戦略・法律の策定、学校給食の管理・運営・監視の改善、地元の農家と学校給食の連携の促進、学校給食の品質・安全性・栄養価の向上などです。WFPの支援により、約3000万人の子どもたちが学校給食を受けることができ、教育への参加や継続が促進されています。

学校のトイレと水の状況については、アフリカでは深刻な問題です。アフリカの学校の約半数にはトイレがなく、約4分の1には水がありません。これは、子どもたちの健康と教育に悪影響を及ぼします。特に女子生徒は、トイレがないことで月経時に不快感や恥ずかしさを感じ、学校を休んだりやめたりする可能性が高くなります。トイレと水の改善には、政府や地方自治体、教育関係者、保護者、コミュニティなどの協力が必要です。また、子どもたちに衛生や水資源の管理に関する教育を行うことも重要です。

以上がアフリカの食料と栄養の状況に関するまとめです。ご参考になれば幸いです。😊

# Summary of the Food and Nutrition Situation in Africa

## Purpose
- To summarize the food and nutrition situation in Africa, including the issues of food security, hunger, malnutrition, food prices, school feeding, toilets, and water in schools
- To provide information and references for further research and action

## Content
- Africa is a continent facing a food and nutrition crisis. As a result of conflict, climate change, COVID-19, inflation, and the rising prices of food and fertilizer, hunger and poverty have been rising sharply. Healthy diets are unattainable for most people, and the number of food-insecure and malnourished people has increased. Especially, the Sahel region and the Horn of Africa are experiencing a severe hunger crisis, and millions of people need food assistance¹²³⁴⁵.
- School feeding is considered to contribute to the quality and learning outcomes of education in Africa. However, there are many challenges in implementing school feeding, such as food procurement, meal preparation, hygiene management, nutrition education, etc. The World Food Program (WFP) is working with many African countries to strengthen institutional capacity for school feeding. Specifically, this includes the development of policies, strategies and laws on school feeding, the improvement of management, operation and monitoring of school feeding, the promotion of linkages between local farmers and school feeding, and the improvement of quality, safety and nutritional value of school meals. WFP's support has enabled about 30 million children to receive school feeding, and has promoted participation and retention in education.
- The situation of toilets and water in schools is a serious problem in Africa. According to a web search result, about half of the schools in Africa have no toilets and about a quarter have no water. This affects the health and education of children. Especially for girls, the lack of toilets can cause discomfort and embarrassment during menstruation, and increase the likelihood of skipping or dropping out of school. Improving toilets and water requires cooperation from the government, local authorities, education officials, parents, communities, etc. It is also important to provide education to children on hygiene and water resource management.

## Conclusion
- The food and nutrition situation in Africa is very serious and requires urgent attention and action from various stakeholders, such as governments, international organizations, NGOs, civil society, etc.
- School feeding is one of the effective interventions to improve the food and nutrition situation, as well as the education situation, in Africa. However, it faces many challenges and needs more support and resources.
- Toilets and water are essential for the health and education of children, but they are lacking in many schools in Africa. Improving toilets and water is a key component of creating a safe and conducive learning environment for children.
