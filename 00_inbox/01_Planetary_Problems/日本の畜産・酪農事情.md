### 日本の畜産・酪農事情  

60年代の農業基本法の発足以降、日本はタンパク質の摂取を米から牛乳・牛肉へシフトし、畜産・酪農業の拡大のため、配合飼料や牧草の調達を海外に求めるようになりました。当時米国は、トウモロコシの余剰在庫分の輸出先を探しており、日本へトウモロコシ輸入の圧力をかけました。
2014年には、雪印のスキャンダルによる需要低迷と酪農家の離農により、一時的に生乳不足に見舞われました。その後持ち直したのですが、昨今の気候変動の農業・畜産・酪農への影響は予想以上に厳しくなっています。
日本における、コロナ禍による需要の低迷と、長引く円安は、肥料・飼料・牧草価格の高騰をまねき、多くの酪農・畜産農家は事業が成り立たなくなりました。  
酪農家の戸数は1963年の42万戸から2000年には3万戸にまで大きく減少し、今では12,000戸に減少しています。さらに、昨年４月～今年９月までに、1,100の酪農家が離農し、56,000頭以上が減少しました。  
北海道の酪農家は、輸入飼料を削減し、放牧へのシフトも見られますが、牧草地が必要になります。 
世界の農地は世界人口を養う余裕がないとも言われますが、多くの農業放棄地も存在します。  

気候変動の影響により、米国では干ばつや水不足も頻発し、今世紀末までに、トウモロコシの生産量が最大で24%まで減少すると見られています。  
その上、全米のトウモロコシの40%がバイオエタノールに向けられ、ある大規模トウモロコシ農家では95%がバイオエタノール向けとなっています。  
世界は、食料問題と食料安全保障に注目していますが、世界のトウモロコシは、食料、資料、エネルギーによる綱引きの状態にあるのです。  
インドでは、タンパク質の供給を牛乳に頼る傾向にあり、生乳需要は日本の生産量の25倍にもありました。これは、世界の酪農の需要が高まっている証拠です。

### The situation of livestock and dairy farming in Japan

In 2014, Japan faced a shortage of raw milk due to the decline in demand and the abandonment of farming caused by the Snow Brand scandal, but it recovered later. However, the impact of climate change on agriculture, livestock, and dairy farming has become more severe than expected. In Japan, the depreciation of the yen also raised the prices of fertilizers and forage, hurting livestock farmers.
The number of dairy farms decreased from 420,000 in 1963 to 30,000 in 2000, and continued to decline, reaching 12,000 today. In addition, from April last year to September this year, 1,100 dairy farms left the industry, and more than 56,000 heads were reduced.

Due to the Agricultural Basic Law in the 1960s, Japan shifted its protein intake from rice to milk and beef, and began to rely on overseas sources for compound feed and forage for the expanding livestock and dairy farming. In particular, at that time, the export of corn, which was overproduced in the United States, became a proposition for the United States, and there was also pressure from the United States to Japan.
And, the decline in demand due to the corona disaster and the high price of feed due to the depreciation of the yen caused many farmers to leave the industry.

Due to the impact of climate change, the United States is experiencing frequent droughts and water shortages, and it is estimated that by the end of this century, corn production will decrease by up to 24%. Moreover, 40% of the corn in the United States is directed to bioethanol, and in some large-scale corn farms, 95% is for bioethanol. The world is paying attention to food issues and food security, but the world's corn is in a tug-of-war between food, materials, and energy.

Now, the world's farmland is limited, but there are many abandoned lands.
Dairy farmers in Hokkaido are reducing imported feed and shifting to grazing, but they need pasture land.

I have a proposal to solve these problems.

The challenge is the ability to respond to demand.
In Japan, the high prices of feed and forage are a problem.
In Africa, even the opportunity for production is limited. Africa has farmland.

With the MBT Sustainable Cycle, we will realize the domestic production of fertilizer in Africa and dramatically improve the productivity of grains. We will encourage surplus production, produce low-cost MBT functional feed, and supply it to Japan. We will also encourage grazing in Africa, and develop the livestock industry with grass grown with MBT functional compost and MBT feed. At the same time, we will produce surplus forage and export it to Japan.

Japan will transfer all kinds of technology to Africa, and open the way to food security among multiple poles of East Asia, including Japan, China, Korea, and Taiwan, and Africa. Japan will also launch CDM projects with Africa, and consider sending organic waste to Africa as an option.
In India, there is a tendency to rely on milk for protein supply, and the demand for raw milk is 25 times that of Japan's production. This is evidence that the demand for dairy farming is increasing in the world.

The MBT circular model in agriculture should be one that considers the demand for products and the challenges of raw materials from the level of farmland and resources, stabilizes the income and food procurement of all related people, and develops mutually. The MBT circular model also contributes to the production and distribution of coffee beans in Ghana, which has collapsed economically.
