Functional clusters and blend hypotheses

- Anti-diarrhea barrier blend
  - Components: Inulin + Scutellaria + Fucoidan
  - Mechanisms: SCFAs for energy/repair; baicalein for inflammation control; sulfated oligosaccharides for mucin shielding
  - Readouts: TEER recovery ≥20%, IL-8 reduction ≥30%, pathogen adhesion ↓

- Inflammation-resilience blend
  - Components: Blueberry peel + Citrus peel + Rice bran
  - Mechanisms: Phenolic metabolites dampen NF-κB; aglycones stabilize tight junctions; ferulates provide antioxidant tone
  - Readouts: NF-κB activity ↓≥40%, Occludin/ZO-1 intensity ↑, intracellular ROS ↓

- Maternal-immune support blend
  - Components: Ganoderma + Seaweed (fucoidan) + Inulin
  - Mechanisms: Triterpenes modulate cytokine balance; sulfated polysaccharides enhance mucosal defenses; SCFAs maintain barrier
  - Readouts: IL-10/IL-6 ratio ↑, macrophage function ↑, mucin gene expression ↑

Proposal backbone: MBT Food & Herbal Probiotics for Maternal and Child Health

Objectives

- Primary: Develop MBT55-fermented probiotic extracts that prevent infectious diarrhea, reduce gut inflammation, and improve nutrient absorption in mothers and children.
- Secondary: Establish local, low-cost manufacturing via MBT-EcoGrid; integrate monitoring through HealthBook for outcome tracking.

---

Development and screening plan

1. Fermentation and analytics

- Substrate panels: Inulin, blueberry peel, citrus peel, Scutellaria, Ganoderma, seaweed powder, okara, rice bran.
- Fermentation regimes: 
  - Phase 1 (aerobic): 48 h at 45–55°C for glycoside hydrolysis and enzyme induction.
  - Phase 2 (microaerophilic): 72 h at 40–45°C for phenolic transformation and SCFA production.
- Analytics:
  - SCFAs: GC–MS (acetate, propionate, butyrate).
  - Phenolics/flavonoids/triterpenes: LC–MS/MS; targeted MRM panels.
  - Polysaccharide profiling: MW distribution, sulfation (NMR), monosaccharide composition.

2. Bioactivity assays

- Barrier integrity: TEER in human intestinal epithelial monolayers; tight junction staining (ZO-1, Occludin).
- Anti-inflammatory: NF-κB reporter assays; cytokines (IL-8, TNF-α, IL-10).
- Antimicrobial/anti-adhesion: MIC against prevalent enteropathogens; adhesion to mucus/epithelium assays.
- Nutrient absorption proxies: Iron/zinc uptake in Caco-2; transporter expression (DMT1, ZIP4).

3. Maternal–child models and safety

- Organoid models: Infant intestinal organoids for mucosal repair and pathogen challenge.
- Immunomodulation: PBMC cytokine profiling for maternal samples; non-stimulatory baseline verification.
- Safety gates: Residual solvents = 0; endotoxin thresholds; allergen screening (soy/citrus), pregnancy-safe excipient review.

4. Formulation and delivery

- Formats: Shelf-stable sachets, syrups for pediatrics, fortified porridges.
- Stability: Accelerated aging (40°C/75% RH); active content retention ≥80% at 6 months.
- Dosing bands: Age-appropriate portions defined by active metabolite ranges (non-pharmacologic).

5. Local manufacturing (MBT-EcoGrid)

- Micro-facilities: Modular MBT units near clinics/schools using local by-products (peels, bran, okara).
- QA/QC: Batch analytics via portable MS/GC kits; barcoded lot tracking to HealthBook.
- Training: Standard SOPs for operators; hygiene and contamination control modules.

---

Pilot implementation and evaluation

- Sites: 3 regions (East Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia) with differing diets and pathogen profiles.
- Partners: Local health ministries, NGOs, Gates/Rockefeller for funding; Yara for agronomic integration (substrate supply chains).
- Metrics:
  - Clinical: Diarrhea incidence and duration; growth markers; anemia and zinc deficiency rates.
  - Biological: Stool biomarkers (SCFAs, calprotectin); barrier function proxies.
  - Operational: Cost per beneficiary, local sourcing %, batch pass rate, stability compliance.

---

Data integration and governance

- HealthBook linkage: Individual-level intake logs, outcomes, and microbiome snapshots.
- Privacy and ethics: Consent workflows; de-identified analytics dashboards for funders and public health teams.
- Adaptive optimization: Feedback loops to adjust blends to local microbiome/diet patterns.

---

Timeline and milestones

1. Months 0–3: Fermentation screens, analytics validation, bioassay pipeline setup.
2. Months 4–6: Lead blend selection; stability and safety profiling; formulation prototypes.
3. Months 7–12: Pilot manufacturing (EcoGrid) and Phase I/II community pilots; interim efficacy readouts.
4. Months 13–18: Scale-up planning; regional customization; cost optimization.

---

Budget and scaling rationale

- Cost drivers: Substrate procurement, analytical kits, QA, training.
- Deflationary levers: Local by-product sourcing, modular MBT units, standardized SOPs.
- Scale targets: ≥100,000 mother–child dyads in year 2 across pilot regions.

MBT Food & Herbal Probiotics：母子健康のための提案骨子

目的
- 主目的：MBT55発酵由来のプロバイオティクス抽出物を開発し、感染性下痢の予防、腸炎の軽減、母子の栄養吸収改善を実現する。  
- 副目的：MBT-EcoGridによる地域製造を確立し、HealthBookと連携して成果を追跡する。

---

開発とスクリーニング計画

1. 発酵と分析
- 素材パネル：イヌリン、ブルーベリー果皮、柑橘果皮、黄芩、霊芝、海藻粉末、オカラ、米ぬか  
- 発酵プロセス：  
  - 第1フェーズ（好気）：45〜55℃で48時間 → グリコシド分解と酵素誘導  
  - 第2フェーズ（微好気）：40〜45℃で72時間 → ポリフェノール変換と短鎖脂肪酸生成  
- 分析方法：  
  - GC–MSで短鎖脂肪酸測定  
  - LC–MS/MSでポリフェノール・フラボノイド・トリテルペン分析  
  - NMRで多糖類の硫酸化度や分子量分布を確認

2. バイオアッセイ
- バリア機能：腸上皮細胞でのTEER測定、タイトジャンクション染色（ZO-1, Occludin）  
- 抗炎症作用：NF-κBレポーターアッセイ、IL-8/TNF-α/IL-10測定  
- 抗菌・抗付着作用：病原菌に対するMIC試験、粘膜付着阻害試験  
- 栄養吸収指標：鉄・亜鉛吸収試験（Caco-2細胞）、トランスポーター発現解析

3. 母子モデルと安全性
- 乳児腸管オルガノイドで粘膜修復効果を検証  
- 母体PBMCで免疫調整作用を確認  
- 安全性確認：残留溶媒ゼロ、エンドトキシン基準内、アレルゲンチェック（大豆・柑橘）、妊婦安全性レビュー

4. 製剤と投与形態
- 形態：常温保存可能なサシェ、乳児用シロップ、強化粥  
- 安定性：加速試験（40℃/75%RH）で6か月後も活性成分保持率80%以上  
- 投与量：年齢別に代謝産物濃度に基づき設定（薬理的ではなく栄養的範囲）

5. 地域製造（MBT-EcoGrid）
- 小規模製造施設：診療所や学校近くに設置、果皮や副産物を原料化  
- 品質管理：携帯型GC/MSでバッチ分析、HealthBookと連携したロット追跡  
- 教育：現地オペレーター向けSOP、衛生・汚染防止トレーニング

  - 臨床：下痢発生率・持続時間、成長指標、貧血・亜鉛欠乏率  
  - 生物学的：便中バイオマーカー（SCFA、カルプロテクチン）、バリア機能指標  
  - 運用：1人当たりコスト、現地調達率、バッチ合格率、安定性遵守率

- HealthBook連携：摂取履歴、成果、腸内フローラ解析を統合
