## **Current World Bank Policies and Their Limitations**

The **World Bank’s current initiatives** primarily focus on **indirect environmental improvements** through **greenhouse gas emission reductions** and **the promotion of sustainable agricultural technologies**. However, these efforts are **mainly centered on monitoring and data collection**, rather than directly addressing **carbon sequestration and soil restoration** through fundamental ecological processes, such as the **"Ecological Hypercycle"** for ecosystem regeneration. This has led to criticism that current policies fail to achieve **substantial ecological restoration**.

### **Challenges in the World Bank’s Approach**

#### **Monitoring-Centric Approach**

The World Bank's current policies emphasize **monitoring agricultural and forest conditions** using **satellite data and drones** to identify areas that require intervention. While this provides **a foundation for policy planning and funding allocation**, the evaluation of **actual carbon sequestration and soil improvement** relies heavily on **secondary indicators** rather than **direct measurements of ecological impact**.

#### **Lack of Response to Rising Input Costs**

Additionally, **rising global input costs** pose a major challenge. However, the World Bank's current approach is **limited to optimizing supply chains and implementing subsidy policies**, rather than actively introducing **technologies and processes that enhance ecosystem resilience and natural resource cycling**. This **reactive** rather than **proactive** approach has been criticized as insufficient.
