
### 1. Executive Summary

This proposal outlines the implementation of the **MBT Sustainable Cycle (MSC)** across Nairobi (population: 4.4 million), transforming organic waste—food waste, livestock manure, agricultural residues, and flood-related disaster waste—into valuable resources, while simultaneously addressing five key challenges in Kenya: **food security, environmental degradation, healthcare costs, youth unemployment, and climate resilience**.

### 5. Strategic Relevance

MSC is more than a composting technology;  
It is a **planetary-scale circular platform** that integrates **food, energy, environment, health, and jobs**.

It directly aligns with the Rockefeller Foundation and Gates Foundation’s strategic vision for **climate-resilient, food-secure Africa** and can be scaled continent-wide as a **transformative infrastructure**.

## Note: Additional Benefits Not Included in the Core Economic Evaluation

The calculated economic impact presented in this proposal **does not account for** the following critical additional revenue streams and environmental co-benefits. Including these factors would dramatically increase the value proposition of the MSC Project in Nairobi and Kenya as a whole.

- **Increased crop yields from improved soil fertility**
- **Higher market value from improved quality and nutrient density of crops**
- **Reduction in food loss throughout the supply chain**
- **Cost savings from remediating chemically and heavy-metal-contaminated soils using MBT-derived materials**
- **Biodiversity enhancement through restored microbial ecosystems**
- **Wildfire prevention by reducing flammable biomass and restoring forest floor stability**
- **Boosted pasture growth and livestock productivity with MBT feed; reduction in pharmaceutical use in livestock**
- **Mitigation of marine and freshwater pollution by preventing nutrient runoff and eutrophication**
- **Environmental bioremediation of contaminated land and water bodies**
- **Increased production and quality in apiculture (beekeeping) and mushroom farming using MBT-based substrates**
