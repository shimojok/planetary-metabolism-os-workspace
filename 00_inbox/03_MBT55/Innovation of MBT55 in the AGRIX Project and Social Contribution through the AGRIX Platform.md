### 1. The Innovative Nature of the MBT55 Microbial Consortium as the Core of the MBT Sustainable Cycle

The **MBT Sustainable Cycle** is a groundbreaking system that, by combining **MBT55**, a dedicated fermenter, and additives like dried okara, can **decompose and ferment almost all organic waste to its limit at high temperatures of 80°C to 100°C within a short period of 24 hours**, excluding only moisture adjustment and pulverization. This high-speed processing capability fundamentally resolves challenges in traditional waste management such as inefficient land use and increased processing costs.

The **MBT55 microbial consortium**, truly embodying "Multi-Biotechnology," is a complex microbial group composed of a **delicate balance of 55% aerobic and 45% anaerobic microorganisms**. This far exceeds the scope of biological treatment encompassed by typical MBT systems, maximizing the **synergistic effects of various microorganisms with specific functionalities**. It comprehensively covers **four major areas of organic decomposition: starch-degrading bacteria, protein-degrading bacteria, lipid-degrading bacteria, and cellulose-degrading bacteria**, enabling the fermentation and decomposition of diverse organic wastes such as all organic matter in soil, all food residues, and livestock excrement.

The main functions performed by its diverse components are as follows:

- **Aerobic Bacteria (e.g., yeasts, cellulose-degrading bacteria, nitrogen-fixing bacteria):** Promote rapid decomposition and fermentation of organic matter, enhancing soil health and aiding nutrient supply to plants.
- **Anaerobic Bacteria (e.g., cellulose-degrading bacteria, nitrogen-fixing bacteria):** Responsible for organic matter decomposition under anaerobic conditions, contributing particularly to potential biogas production and nutrient cycling in low-oxygen environments.
- **Lactic Acid Bacteria Group:** Contribute to the stabilization and quality improvement of organic matter and help suppress pathogenic bacteria.
- **Filamentous Fungi (Aromatic Compound Degrading Fungi):** Contribute to the decomposition of recalcitrant organic matter like lignin, increasing the processing capacity for diverse organic wastes.
- **Actinomycetes (Chitin-Degrading Bacteria):** Promote the decomposition of chitin and may also contribute to suppressing soil-borne diseases.
- **Manganese-Reducing Bacteria (Black Mold Group - Basidiomycota) and Manganese-Oxidizing Bacteria (Organotrophic Bacteria):** Involved in the valence change of manganese, affecting the circulation and availability of trace elements in the soil.
- **Ammonia-Oxidizing Bacteria (Nitrosomonas) and Nitrifying Bacteria:** Play crucial roles in the nitrogen cycle, promoting the oxidation of ammonia to nitrate (nitrification). This supplies nitrogen in a form readily available to plants and suppresses excessive ammonia emissions.
- **Sulfur Bacteria (Bacteria utilizing hydrogen sulfide as a hydrogen donor, e.g., Green Sulfur Bacteria family) and Sulfate Bacteria:** Efficiently remove odor-causing substances like hydrogen sulfide, contributing to improved facility environments and public health protection.
- **Cellulose-Actinomycetes, Cellulose Filamentous Fungi:** Strongly promote cellulose decomposition, efficiently processing large amounts of organic matter such as plant residues.
- **Iron-Oxidizing Bacteria, Iron-Reducing Bacteria:** Involved in iron cycling, potentially contributing to the stabilization of heavy metals in soil and the adjustment of iron content in water.
- **Methane-Oxidizing Bacteria:** Efficiently decompose methane (CH4), a potent greenhouse gas generated during organic matter decomposition and from soil, into less potent carbon dioxide (CO2) and water. This suppresses direct greenhouse gas emissions from the waste sector.
- **Lignin-Degrading Bacteria:** Decompose lignin, considered one of the most difficult organic matters to break down, enabling the processing of woody biomass and driftwood.
- **Sulfate-Reducing Bacteria:** Play a role in reducing sulfates under anaerobic conditions, contributing to the sulfur cycle.
- **Propionate-Producing Bacteria:** Of particular note is the ability of these bacteria to promote propionate production in the rumen of ruminants, thereby **effectively reducing methane gas emissions from cattle rumen**. This could be a groundbreaking solution for climate change countermeasures in the livestock sector.

Through the collaborative action of these diverse microorganisms, MBT55 exhibits extremely high decomposition capabilities for all organic waste and complex environmental improvement effects. **This breaks through the processing limits of existing MBT technologies, offering a more efficient and multifunctional solution.**

### 3. Contribution to Climate Change Countermeasures and Superiority in Carbon Sequestration

The MBT Sustainable Cycle makes multifaceted and innovative contributions to climate change countermeasures.

- **Methane Emission Reduction:** Dramatically reduces organic matter destined for landfills, thereby suppressing the emission of methane, a potent greenhouse gas. Furthermore, the action of methane-oxidizing bacteria effectively suppresses methane generation from soil and residual organic matter.
- **Long-Term Carbon Sequestration:** The humus-like properties of MBT metabolic substances enable the **long-term fixation of carbon in the soil**. This is an extremely effective climate change mitigation measure that stores atmospheric CO2 in the soil, forming a core component of nature-based solutions (NbS) emphasized by the World Bank. Compared to conventional carbon sequestration technologies, its superiority is evident in its ability to simultaneously achieve waste treatment and improved agricultural productivity.
- **Livestock-Derived Methane Reduction:** The ability of propionate-producing bacteria to directly contribute to the reduction of rumen methane in cattle, a major source of greenhouse gas emissions in the livestock sector, distinguishes this system from any other existing waste treatment technology.
- **Nitrous Oxide (N2O) Emission Suppression:** Promoting a healthy nitrogen cycle through the action of nitrifying bacteria and other microorganisms may also contribute to suppressing N2O emissions from agricultural activities.
