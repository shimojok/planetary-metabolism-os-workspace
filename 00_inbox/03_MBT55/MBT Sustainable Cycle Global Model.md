### 1. Paradigm Shift from Conventional Waste Management

##### **Collection and Resource Recovery of Waste:**

Centralized collection of food waste and disaster debris (such as driftwood) avoids CO₂ emissions associated with traditional incineration and landfill disposal. This significantly reduces environmental burden and enables more sustainable waste management practices.
##### **Quality Transformation:**

By converting waste from mere “unwanted material” into high-value resources such as humic substances and feed, the model transcends the conventional cost structure of simple material loss and gains, enhancing both qualitative and economic value.
### 2. Innovative Mechanisms through Biotechnology

##### **Utilization of MBT55 Microbial Consortium:**

The core of the biotechnology, the MBT55 microbial consortium, accelerates the humification process of waste, contributing to soil improvement and carbon sequestration. Thus, traditional waste treatment is elevated into an environmental carbon fixation process.
##### **MBT Fermentation System (Reactor):**

The MBT fermenter efficiently processes food waste and driftwood, promoting resource conversion through microbial activity. This system enables fine-scale transformation processes that were difficult through chemical treatments, producing high-quality humic substances and feed.
### 3. Integration of Economic and Environmental Benefits

##### **Significant Reduction of Corporate Costs:**

The MBT Sustainable Cycle subscription model, by providing access to the MBT fermenter and MBT55 microbial consortium, enables companies to reduce their waste processing costs. Additionally, the processed materials are reused more cost-effectively, lowering resource procurement expenses.
##### **Revenue from Carbon Credits and Achieving Carbon Neutrality:**

The CO₂ reduction effects from waste resource recovery serve as a basis for companies to acquire carbon credits, greatly contributing to achieving carbon neutrality.
##### **Expectations for Forest Fire Prevention through Soil Improvement:**

MBT-derived humic substances increase the soil’s organic carbon content, improving fertility and structure. This enhances the soil’s water retention capacity, supports plant growth, and creates environments less prone to drying out. Moist soils help suppress the outbreak of fires.
##### **Optimization of Government Budgets:**

By reducing national waste management budgets and directly linking to environmental initiatives such as soil restoration and carbon sequestration, this model also promises enhanced efficiency in public fiscal operations.
### 4. Cross-Industry Applications and Social Ripple Effects

#### **Deployment Across Diverse Industries:**

##### **From Large Corporations to SMEs:**

Not only companies working on reducing plastic waste, like Starbucks, but also coffee ground processors, food waste handlers, fruit beverage manufacturers, processed food producers, food distributors, hotel chains, and restaurants can all benefit from resource conversion through the MBT fermentation system.
##### **Stabilization of Agriculture and Distribution:**

The supply of humic substances contributes to reducing agricultural production costs and increasing yields, ultimately helping stabilize fair food pricing, reducing food loss, and even lowering healthcare expenses.
##### **Transforming Waste into "Earning Resources":**

Waste, previously seen purely as a cost, becomes "earning waste" by generating economic value through processing and resource recovery. This leads to the establishment of an “earning forest model,” fundamentally transforming the conventional cost structures dependent on waste disposal.
### 5. Global Impact and Future Prospects

##### **Contribution to Global Warming Mitigation:**

This model enables not only the reduction of greenhouse gas emissions but also long-term countermeasures against climate change through soil restoration and carbon sequestration. It is a major contribution toward international environmental policies and the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
##### **New Development of the Circular Economy:**

By circulating waste as renewable resources rather than treating it as mere refuse, this model embodies the philosophy of the circular economy. It has the potential to become the new global standard aiming to balance environmental conservation and economic activity.
##### **Social Innovation through Quality Transformation:**

Beyond physical waste treatment, transforming the quality of waste achieves new value creation across environmental, economic, and social dimensions. As a result, traditional business models, public budgets, and approaches to corporate social responsibility are fundamentally redefined, providing a powerful driving force toward a sustainable society.
### Conclusion

The technological innovations of the **MBT Sustainable Cycle System** and **MBT55** go beyond waste treatment to simultaneously achieve environmental improvement and economic growth. By reducing traditional waste processing costs and creating revenue through resource recovery and carbon credits, they contribute to achieving corporate carbon neutrality. Furthermore, by reducing environmental burdens, restoring soils, and mitigating global warming, they deliver highly public benefits and have the potential to introduce new budget structures across industries and government agencies worldwide.  
Thanks to these multifaceted effects, this model is expected to become the symbol of a new era of **"earning waste, earning forests"**, and a vital solution that paves the way for the future of the circular economy.
