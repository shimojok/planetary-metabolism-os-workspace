**Title:**  
**“MBT55 and the Sustainable Cycle: From Disaster Recovery to Global Climate Resilience”**

**1. Background**  
The global system is facing a “metabolic crisis” due to the breakdown of carbon, nitrogen, and water cycles. Agriculture, waste, health, and education remain fragmented, driving greenhouse gas emissions.  

**2. MBT55’s Role**  
MBT55 reconnects carbon, nitrogen, phosphorus, and water cycles through microbial ecosystems. It achieves simultaneous “decomposition = generation,” turning waste into resources. This creates scientific, social, and economic reconnection.  

**3. MBT Sustainable Cycle (MSC)**  
Waste → Fermentation/Decomposition → Resource Conversion → Carbon Fixation → Economic Value.  
MSC integrates material, informational, and economic cycles into an “Eco-Hypercycle.”  
Simulation results: **CO₂ reduction 22–28%, agricultural income increase 17–22%.**  

**4. Case Study: Tohoku Earthquake & Tsunami (2011)**  
- In Iwanuma, Miyagi Prefecture, farmland was buried under 4–10 cm of seabed sludge.  
- MBT55 was applied on April 29, followed by rainfall/irrigation.  
- Within one week, sludge decomposed and transformed into nutrient-rich soil.  
- Rice transplanting was possible by May 20, and by June 18 MBT55-treated plots showed vigorous growth.  
- Untreated plots remained buried, with almost no growth.  
- Despite typhoon damage later, harvest yields exceeded pre-disaster levels.  
**→ MBT55 enabled rapid bioremediation, soil recovery, and sustainable farming in a disaster zone.**

**5. Impacts**  
- **Environmental:** SOC formation rate +116%, CO₂ absorption +100%, methane reduction 42–57%.  
- **Social:** Health improvement, job creation (~820 jobs per unit/year, 42% women & youth), education benefits.  
- **Economic:** Green premium +18–22%, GDP contribution +2.8–3.4%.  

**6. Numerical Model (Annual Effect Estimates)**
