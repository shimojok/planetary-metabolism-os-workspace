The 2011 off the Pacific coast of Tohoku Earthquake caused devastating damage to the coastal regions of the Tohoku area. The impact on farmland was particularly severe, with specific damages reported as follows:

#### Flooding and Contamination of Farmland:
- The tsunami flooded a wide area of coastal farmland, causing salt damage to many fields. Seawater intrusion increased soil salinity, making it unsuitable for crop growth.
- The tsunami also covered farmland with large amounts of seabed sludge, accumulating contaminants.

#### Effects of Salt Damage:
- The salt damage caused by seawater increased soil salinity, severely affecting crop growth. High salinity soils make it difficult for crop roots to absorb water, often leading to plant death.
- The effects of salt damage were long-term, requiring soil improvement and freshwater washing to remove the salt, which was time-consuming and expensive.

#### Soil Improvement:
- Efforts were made to improve the soil in affected areas. Freshwater was used to wash away the salt, and soil conditioners were applied to lower salt concentrations.
- Many farmers in the disaster-stricken areas were unable to deal with the salt damage and sludge, abandoning agriculture for several years. However, our MBT development team visited the affected areas immediately after the disaster. By using MBT materials, we were able to treat the salt damage, compost the sludge, and achieve higher yields than the previous year.

The MBT55 microbial group decomposed organic matter, providing plants with numerous minerals, vitamins, and amino acids, enhancing productivity, quality, nutritional value, and freshness retention. Moreover, many of the nutrients produced by microorganisms contributed to reducing the need for fertilizers. High-functional soil prevents continuous cropping disorders and suppresses the occurrence of pests and diseases.
