◇Lignin Humification Project Using MBT Biocontrol Function

This project is planned as part of the AGRIX Project. It also serves to encourage the actions of investors I am currently approaching, especially in the context of stalled climate change measures and the slow progress in restoring degraded soils in Africa.

Lignin is a vital component of all plant matter and exists in large quantities on Earth, including in waste such as driftwood. However, due to its nature as a high-molecular compound, lignin is difficult to break down and process, and much of it has been left untreated or incinerated.

MBT55 can decompose and ferment lignin in just 24 hours, producing humic substances rich in carbon. This increases the soil organic carbon (SOC) and can significantly contribute to enhanced food production and climate change mitigation. Solving the waste treatment problem also brings benefits to national governments.

Humification Project for Waste Using MBT Biocontrol Function

Key Points:

Due to frequent flooding, driftwood, thinned wood, pruned trees, and organic waste such as coffee grounds have mostly been incinerated or landfilled. However, by humifying these materials and applying them to soil, there is potential to achieve long-term carbon sequestration.

Generally, lignin is difficult to decompose, but MBT55, which contains lignin-degrading microorganisms, can produce humic substances through decomposition and fermentation in about 24 hours.

This enables the effective resource utilization of waste such as driftwood.

One of the reasons for the prolonged forest fires occurring frequently in recent years is the neglect of forest maintenance—underbrush and small vegetation are left unmanaged, and thinning is not performed, resulting in an abundance of fuel during wildfires.

Traditionally, forest conservation activities other than reforestation have been considered cost burdens. However, the humification of driftwood and other waste as raw material for humic substances is expected to be profitable. It also contributes to wildfire prevention.
