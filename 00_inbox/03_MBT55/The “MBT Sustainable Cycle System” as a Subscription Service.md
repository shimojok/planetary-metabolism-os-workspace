## **3. Special Focus on Lignin and Economic Evaluation**

Lignin, a primary component of woody waste, is difficult to process and expensive to treat. With MBT55’s enhanced biodegradation control, the following benefits are anticipated:

### 3.1 Improved Economic Outcomes

- **Lower treatment complexity:** Enhanced lignin degradation simplifies the process and reduces costs
- **High-value product potential:** Lignin can be used for bioplastics, adhesives, carbon fiber, pharmaceuticals
    
**Example:**

If traditional lignin treatment costs 10,000–15,000 yen/ton, MBT55 could cut this by 50%, lowering it to under 5,000 yen/ton.  
Post-treatment lignin products may sell with a premium (5,000–10,000 yen/ton).

### 3.2 Key Takeaways

- **Technological innovation:** MBT55’s lignin processing can lead to licensing or co-development projects
- **Environmental impact:** Effective lignin utilization supports circular economy and waste reduction
- **Market potential:** Expanding lignin use creates new biomass markets with long-term revenue prospects

- **Data analytics:** Measure CO₂ sequestration, soil improvement, and visualize benefits
