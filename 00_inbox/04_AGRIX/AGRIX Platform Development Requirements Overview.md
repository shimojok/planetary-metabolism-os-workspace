### Overview

The AGRIX Platform is a multi-layered integrated digital infrastructure designed to comprehensively support agriculture, the environment, and health. This platform consists of multiple modular components (e.g., AgriWare, SafetyChain, AgriChain) and utilizes a microservices architecture and API gateway to flexibly and scalably manage complex lower-layer processes.

In the mid-to-upper layers, ANE (Agricultural Net Ecosystem) and ACAIN (Agro-Circular AI Network) work in tandem to leverage the data accumulated within AGRIX and construct a service layer oriented toward a circular economy. This includes the MBT sustainable cycle-based subscription investment model, carbon sequestration monitoring and credit issuance management, and the provision of production risk assessment indicators for the insurance and financial sectors—thereby expanding value through API integration with external stakeholders.

As core technologies, the platform incorporates blockchain to ensure transaction traceability and reliability, IoT sensing of soil ecosystem control data via the MBT55 microbial consortium, high-precision phenotyping analysis, screening of plant secondary metabolites such as polyphenols and flavonoids, and AI-driven functionality prediction algorithms for these compounds.

The AGRIX Platform aims to become a globally sustainable technology infrastructure by serving as an edge-cloud integrated digital ecosystem that bridges the domains of agritech, the bioeconomy, and climate tech.

### AGRIX Platform Development Requirements Overview (BioNexus Holdings)

The "AGRIX Platform" being developed by BioNexus Holdings is a modular enterprise platform that serves as the core of a decentralized agricultural ecosystem. This platform is composed of three functional modules—**AgriWare, SafetyChain, and AgriChain**—and employs a scalable architecture that accommodates a wide range of stakeholders, from end-users to supply chains and policy authorities.

### Technical Structure and Functional Layers

AGRIX features a multi-layered architecture, built with the following key technologies and components:

- **Lower-Level Modules (AgriWare, SafetyChain, AgriChain)**  
    These modules collect multi-layered big data including sensor inputs, image analysis, phenotyping, environmental, growth, and nutritional data. Real-time analysis is performed via the AgriWare API using algorithms for component analysis, growth prediction, soil MBT analysis, nutrition and freshness management, and safety evaluation.
    
- **Mid-to-Upper-Level Modules (ANE/ACAIN Layer)**  
    The agronomic data generated by AGRIX is connected to the design of a circular economy zone, providing the following capabilities:
    
    - **MBT Sustainable Cycle Integration**: Resource circulation management through the fermentation of soil and animal/plant residues centered on MBT55
    - **Subscription-Based Investment Model**: Design of ESG capital flow for sustainable agriculture
    - **Carbon Sequestration Index & Credit Management**: Visualization of soil carbon dynamics and tokenization of carbon credits on a blockchain basis
    - **Agricultural Risk Index for Insurance**: Provision of APIs for crop yield risk assessment models using phenotyping, extreme weather, and pest/disease data
        
### Platform Technologies

- **AI-Integrated Processing**: Implementation of AI to screen plant secondary metabolites such as polyphenols and flavonoids, and to predict metabolic transformations by MBT55
- **Blockchain Compatibility**: Ensures data authenticity and traceability
- **API Economy Development**: Deployment of custom APIs for institutions such as insurance, finance, distribution, and consumers
    
### Use Cases and End-User Value

- **Agricultural Operators / Food Companies**: Yield and quality enhancement, supply stability, and improved traceability
- **Governments / Institutional Investors**: Agricultural policy support, climate change mitigation, and portfolio diversification
- **Insurance Companies**: Development of new products and risk assessment, design of health-promoting insurance models
- **International Organizations**: Food security, SDGs alignment, and support for technology transfer
