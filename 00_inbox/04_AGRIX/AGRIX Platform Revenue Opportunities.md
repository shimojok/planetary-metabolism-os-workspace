**2. New insurance business linked to carbon credits**

Carbon credits are emissions allowances that are earned by reducing or absorbing greenhouse gas emissions. Carbon credits are traded on emissions trading systems or voluntary offset markets.

Carbon credits have a variety of risks. For example, the following risks are possible:

- **Policy risk:** The demand or price of carbon credits may fluctuate as international frameworks and domestic regulations on climate change are changed.
- **Technical risk:** The quantity or quality of carbon credits may decrease as the technologies used for measuring and verifying carbon reduction or absorption are inaccurate or unstable.
- **Market risk:** The price of carbon credits may fluctuate as the supply and demand or liquidity of carbon credits fluctuates.
- **Environmental risk:** The effectiveness of carbon reduction or absorption may decrease due to climate change or natural disasters.

To address these risks, new insurance products linked to carbon credits are being developed. For example, the following insurance products are possible:

- **Hedging insurance against price fluctuations of carbon credits:** This insurance compensates for losses due to price fluctuations in the trading of carbon credits.
- **Guarantee insurance against non-performance of carbon credits:** This insurance compensates buyers for the case where the seller fails to deliver the promised quantity or quality of carbon credits.
- **Environmental risk insurance against carbon credits:** This insurance compensates creators of carbon credits for the case where the effectiveness of carbon reduction or absorption decreases due to climate change or natural disasters.

AGRIX Platform can create carbon credits through MBT compost and Ferrihydrite/Humus for carbon sequestration, and through AgriWare® for carbon reduction. SafetyChain can also ensure the quantity and quality of carbon credits through quality control and production management. By linking these services with the above insurance products, the risk of carbon credits can be reduced and their reliability and value can be increased.
