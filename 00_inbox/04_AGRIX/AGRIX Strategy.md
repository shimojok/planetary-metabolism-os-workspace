Now, while both IPCC and COP have held numerous sessions, and vast amounts of funds have been poured into developing countries like those in Africa, many food aid projects have been implemented. However, hunger in Africa continues to rise, and the global food situation is deteriorating. Climate action remains stagnant, leaving us watching the growing frequency of floods, droughts, and wildfires.

On the other hand, recent years have seen growing support for regenerative agriculture in food production.

The World Bank and FAO are said to play a key role in promoting regenerative agriculture from the perspective of sustainability and food security. Further examples include:

- The Rockefeller Foundation announced $11 million in grants at COP27 to expand regenerative and indigenous agriculture globally. ([source](https://www.rockefellerfoundation.org/news/the-rockefeller-foundation-announces-ten-grants-at-cop27-to-scale-indigenous-and-regenerative-agriculture-practices-globally/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
- The Foundation also collaborated with Pollination and TIFS to publish a catalog of financing instruments for regenerative agriculture, noting $4.5 trillion in new investment is needed annually, calling for the mobilization of large-scale capital such as pension funds and insurance companies. ([source](https://www.rockefellerfoundation.org/news/pollination-tifs-rockefeller-foundation-release-catalogue-of-regenerative-agriculture-financing-instruments/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
- Nestlé aims to source 50% of its raw materials from regenerative agriculture by 2030, providing technical support and incentives to farmers.
- Walmart and PepsiCo announced a $120 million, 7-year investment to support regenerative agriculture across over 2 million acres of farmland in North America, aiming to improve soil health, water quality, and reduce greenhouse gases. ([source](https://corporate.walmart.com/news/2023/07/26/pepsico-and-walmart-aim-to-support-regenerative-agriculture-across-more-than-2-million-acres-of-farmland/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
- Patagonia is expanding the use of regenerative organic-certified crops and supports sustainable agriculture by forming direct partnerships with farmers across its supply chain.
    
However, there are also challenges with regenerative agriculture:

- Scientific evidence for its benefits is still limited, especially regarding carbon sequestration, which some claim is overestimated. The carbon absorption capacity of soil has limits, and more research is needed to ensure long-term sustainability.
- Initial investment and technical learning costs can be high, potentially reducing short-term profitability, especially for small-scale farmers for whom economic burdens are a major obstacle. ([source](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/land-use-biodiversity/rewarding-farmers-regenerative-agriculture-is-critical-decarbonising-food-sector-2024-06-10/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
- Some studies indicate a potential drop in yields during the transition to regenerative agriculture, raising concerns about food supply stability and farmers' incomes.
    
FAO provides policy advice and technical support to promote sustainable agricultural development, but it does not possess the technologies required to resolve Africa’s food crisis. FAO has stated that it will develop such technologies by 2030.
