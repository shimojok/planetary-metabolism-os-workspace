### Evaluation Points for Agricultural Products in SafetyChain

The quality of agricultural products involves assessing various characteristics and functions. To measure crop quality, it's necessary to check items such as:

1. **Appearance:** Observable features like shape, color, size, weight, damage, dirt, pest damage, etc. Appearance influences consumer purchasing desire and impressions. Quality can be assessed by grading or ranking based on standards.

2. **Taste:** Features experienced when consuming crops, including flavor, aroma, and texture. Taste affects consumer satisfaction and repeat rates. Quality assessment methods include sensory tasting by humans and measuring physical and chemical indicators like soluble solids and sweetness.

3. **Nutritional Value:** Components contributing to human health, such as vitamins, minerals, dietary fiber, and pigments. Nutritional value impacts consumer health awareness and needs. Analytical methods, including chemical analysis and near-infrared spectroscopy, quantify components.

4. **Safety:** Substances potentially harmful to human health, like pesticides or harmful microorganisms on crops. Safety influences consumer trust and confidence. Measurement methods include pesticide residue and microbiological testing, along with presenting certificates for pesticide-free or organic cultivation.

5. **Functionality:** Special effects on human health, like antioxidant properties or immune system enhancement. Functionality influences consumer added value and differentiation. Quality assessment involves analyzing bioactive components and providing scientific evidence through animal experiments or clinical trials.

6. **Freshness:** The extent and speed of changes in crops after harvest. Freshness impacts consumer vividness and tastiness. Quality measurement involves physiological indicators like moisture and respiration, along with managing environmental conditions such as temperature and humidity.

These are the key points to consider when evaluating the quality of agricultural products in SafetyChain.

### Conditions to Improve Crop Quality

1. **Appearance:** To enhance the appearance of crops, it is crucial to implement proper cultivation management, harvest timing, harvesting methods, and post-processing such as sorting and packaging. Additionally, preventing and controlling injuries and pest infestations are necessary.

2. **Taste:** Improving the taste of crops involves selecting suitable varieties, adjusting cultivation environments, optimizing harvest timing and methods, and post-processing through storage and processing. Avoiding excessive fertilization and irrigation is important as they can degrade taste.

3. **Nutritional Value:** Increasing the nutritional value of crops requires choosing appropriate varieties, optimizing cultivation environments, determining the right harvest timing and methods, and implementing post-processing through storage and processing. Moreover, technologies promoting the expression and activation of genes related to nutrient absorption and metabolism have been developed.

4. **Safety:** Ensuring the safety of crops involves managing to prevent residue and contamination of pesticides and harmful microorganisms. Employing safe production methods such as pesticide-free or organic cultivation, along with reliable distribution methods like safety inspections and issuing certificates, is crucial.

5. **Functionality:** Enhancing the functionality of crops requires selecting suitable varieties, adjusting cultivation environments, optimizing harvest timing and methods, and post-processing through storage and processing. Technologies such as genetic manipulation and chemical treatments have also been developed to increase the content of functional components and physiological activity.

6. **Freshness:** Preserving the freshness of crops necessitates immediately subjecting them to appropriate storage conditions, such as low temperatures and high humidity, after harvest. Utilizing technologies like plastic films and cushion packaging is also important for maintaining freshness.

### Biomarkers in Evaluation Points for Crop Growth, Quality, Nutritional Value, Freshness, and Functionality

Biomarkers in the context of evaluating crops refer to substances or phenomena that serve as indicators for measuring the characteristics and functions of crops. These biomarkers encompass crop elements such as genes, proteins, metabolites, bioactive substances, and photosynthetic parameters. Here are examples of biomarkers for each evaluation point:

**Growth:** Biomarkers for measuring the growth rate and biomass of crops include gene expression, protein quantity, chlorophyll content, and photosynthetic efficiency.

**Quality:** Biomarkers for assessing the appearance and taste of crops include pigments, sugar content, acidity, aroma components, and hardness.

**Nutritional Value:** Biomarkers for measuring nutrients and functional components in crops include vitamins, minerals, dietary fiber, antioxidants, and phytochemicals.

**Freshness:** Biomarkers for evaluating crop freshness and shelf life include moisture content, respiration rate, ethylene production, enzyme activity, and microbial count.

**Functionality:** Biomarkers for measuring physiological activity and health effects of crops include bioactive substances, physiological activity indicators, and methods such as animal experiments and clinical trials.

### Biomarkers and Phenotyping

Phenotyping is the measurement of the phenotype, or observable characteristics, of plants. The phenotype encompasses various attributes and functionalities of plants, including shape, color, size, weight, taste, aroma, texture, nutritional value, safety, functionality, freshness, and more.

Therefore, all six points mentioned above are relevant to phenotyping. Phenotyping involves not only visually assessable characteristics but also the measurement of physical and chemical indicators, as well as bioactive components.

Phenotyping plays a crucial role in breeding and cultivation management. In recent years, research has advanced the use of technologies such as sensors, drones, and machine learning to expedite and automate phenotyping. This advancement aims to more accurately evaluate plant characteristics and functionalities, contributing to the creation of superior varieties and increased productivity.
