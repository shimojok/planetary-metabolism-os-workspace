### 世界銀行、FAO、アフリカの農業団体によるアフリカ農業開発と食料問題対策

世界銀行、FAO（国際連合食糧農業機関）、そしてアフリカの農業団体は、それぞれ異なる役割と強みを持ちながら、アフリカの農業開発と食料問題対策に取り組んでいます。以下にそれぞれの具体的な取り組みをまとめます。

#### 1. 世界銀行

世界銀行は、融資、無償資金協力、技術支援、知識共有などを通じて、アフリカの農業セクターの変革を支援しています。主な取り組みは以下の通りです。
 * 戦略的投資: 「Feed Africa」戦略の下、2015年から10年間でアフリカの農業に240億ドルの投資を目指しています。これは、生産性の向上、サプライチェーンの強化、市場へのアクセス改善などを目的としています。
 * 気候変動対策: 気候変動に強い農業（Climate-Smart Agriculture）を推進し、干ばつや洪水などの異常気象に対する農家の resilience（強靭性）を高めるための支援を行っています。これには、持続可能な土地管理、効率的な水管理、気候変動に適応した作物の導入などが含まれます。
 * デジタル農業の推進: データに基づいたデジタル農業の導入を支援し、農家の意思決定能力向上や効率化を図っています。これには、土壌マッピング、精密農業技術、市場情報の提供などが含まれます。
 * 食料安全保障と栄養: 食料と栄養の安全保障を強化するため、情報共有、迅速な資源提供、そして長期的な改革に必要な国の制度設計を支援しています。
 * 民間セクターの動員: 農業・食料分野への民間投資を促進するため、革新的な官民連携（PPP）の設計、パイロットプロジェクトの支援、リスク軽減策の導入などを行っています。
 * 土壌の健康と肥料へのアクセス: OCPグループとのパートナーシップを通じて、西アフリカとサヘル地域の500万人の農家を対象に、カスタマイズされた肥料と持続可能な農業慣行の利用を促進し、土壌の健康と農業生産性を向上させる取り組みを行っています。

具体的なプロジェクト例:
 * 地域農業フラッグシッププログラム（Regional Agriculture Flagship Programs）: 特定の地域における主要な農業課題に対応するため、複数の国が連携して取り組むプログラムを支援しています。
 * 小規模農家支援プロジェクト: 小規模農家に対して、融資へのアクセス、技術指導、市場との連携などを支援し、収入向上と食料安全保障の改善を図っています。

#### 2. FAO（国際連合食糧農業機関）

FAOは、食料と農業に関する国際的な専門機関として、アフリカ各国政府や地域機関、その他のパートナーと協力して、食料安全保障の向上と持続可能な農業の実現を目指しています。主な取り組みは以下の通りです。
 * 政策支援と技術支援: 各国の農業政策策定や実施を支援し、持続可能な農業技術や慣行の普及を促進しています。これには、土壌管理、水資源管理、病害虫管理、ポストハーベストロス削減などが含まれます。
 * 食料安全保障情報の提供: 食料需給のモニタリングや早期警戒システムの構築を支援し、食料危機への対応能力強化を図っています。
 * 緊急支援: 自然災害や紛争などによる食料危機が発生した際には、緊急食料支援や農業再建支援を提供しています。
 * 「一つの村、一つの産品（One Country One Priority Product: OCOP）」イニシアチブ: 各国が優先的に取り組むべき主要な農産物の生産、加工、マーケティングの促進を支援し、持続可能な「グリーン」バリューチェーンを通じて経済成長と食料安全保障に貢献することを目指しています。
 * 栄養改善: 栄養政策の強化、健康的な食生活の推進、食料へのアクセス改善などを通じて、アフリカの栄養状況改善を支援しています。
 * 気候変動への適応と緩和: 気候変動が農業に与える影響を軽減するため、適応策の導入や温室効果ガス排出量の削減に向けた取り組みを支援しています。

具体的な取り組み例:
 * サヘル地域における気候変動関連の家畜損失と生計の課題への取り組み: IGAD（政府間開発機構）と協力し、気候変動の影響を受ける東アフリカの牧畜民や農牧民への緊急支援プロジェクトを実施しています。
 * 栄養政策の強化支援: 各国の栄養政策と計画策定を支援し、栄養、健康的な食事、適切な食料への権利に関する政策手段の強化を図っています。

#### 3. アフリカの農業団体

アフリカには、地域レベル、国レベル、そして農家レベルの様々な農業団体が存在し、それぞれの役割を果たしています。
 * 地域農業団体:
   * 東アフリカ農業連盟（Eastern Africa Farmers Federation: EAFF）: 東アフリカ地域の農家の声を代表し、政策提言や情報共有、能力開発などを行っています。
   * 西アフリカ農民・生産者組織（ROPPA）: 西アフリカ地域の農民組織をネットワーク化し、政策対話や市場アクセス改善などを推進しています。
 * 国レベルの農業団体: 各国の農家組合や農業協同組合などが、農家の権利擁護、技術指導、市場アクセス支援、資材の共同購入などを実施しています。
 * テーマ別の農業団体: 特定の作物や畜産、有機農業、女性農業者などを支援する団体が存在し、専門的な知識や技術を提供しています。
具体的な取り組み例:
 * Sasakawa Africa Association (SAA): 日本財団の支援を受け、アフリカ17カ国で35年以上にわたり農業支援を実施。現在はエチオピア、マリ、ナイジェリア、ウガンダの4カ国に注力し、小規模農家の食料、栄養、収入の安全保障を支援しています。具体的には、農業普及サービスの提供、収穫後損失の削減、環境保全型農業の推進、高栄養作物の普及、アグリビジネスの育成などを行っています。
 * One Acre Fund: ケニア、ルワンダ、ブルンジなどで活動するNGOで、小規模農家に融資、研修、高品質の種子を提供し、収量増加と収入向上を支援しています。
 * African Development Foundation (ADF): ガーナなどでキャッサバ生産の改善プロジェクトを実施し、農家の収入向上と地域経済の発展に貢献しています。
連携と協調
これらの機関や団体は、単独で活動するだけでなく、互いに連携・協調しながら、より効果的な農業開発と食料問題対策に取り組んでいます。例えば、世界銀行とFAOは共同で調査・分析を行ったり、合同でプロジェクトを実施したりすることがあります。また、国際機関と地域・国の農業団体が連携して、現場レベルでの活動を展開することも重要です。
アフリカの農業開発と食料問題対策は、多くの関係者の協力と継続的な努力が必要な複雑な課題です。それぞれの機関や団体の強みを活かし、連携を強化することで、より大きな成果が期待されます。

### African agricultural development and food security measures by the World Bank, FAO and African agricultural organizations

Efforts by the World Bank, FAO, and African Agricultural Organizations for African Agricultural Development and Food Security Measures
The World Bank, the FAO (Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations), and African agricultural organizations each play different roles and have distinct strengths as they work to address agricultural development and food security issues in Africa. Here is a summary of their specific efforts:

#### 1. The World Bank

The World Bank supports the transformation of Africa's agricultural sector through loans, grants, technical assistance, and knowledge sharing. Key initiatives include:
 * Strategic Investment: Under the "Feed Africa" strategy, the World Bank aims to invest $24 billion in African agriculture over ten years, starting in 2015. This is intended to improve productivity, strengthen supply chains, and enhance market access.
 * Climate Change Measures: Promoting Climate-Smart Agriculture to enhance the resilience of farmers against extreme weather events such as droughts and floods. This includes sustainable land management, efficient water management, and the introduction of climate-adapted crops.
 * Promotion of Digital Agriculture: Supporting the adoption of data-driven digital agriculture to improve farmers' decision-making abilities and efficiency. This includes soil mapping, precision agriculture technologies, and the provision of market information.
 * Food Security and Nutrition: Strengthening food and nutrition security by supporting information sharing, rapid resource provision, and the design of long-term reforms needed for resilient food and nutrition systems.
 * Mobilizing the Private Sector: Encouraging private investment in the agriculture and food sectors by supporting the design, piloting, and de-risking of innovative public-private partnerships (PPPs).
 * Soil Health and Access to Fertilizers: Partnering with the OCP Group to support five million farmers in West Africa and the Sahel by promoting the use of customized fertilizers and sustainable farming practices to improve soil health and agricultural productivity.

**Specific Project Examples:**
 * Regional Agriculture Flagship Programs: Supporting programs where multiple countries collaborate to address key agricultural challenges in specific regions.
 * Smallholder Farmer Support Projects: Providing smallholder farmers with access to finance, technical guidance, and market linkages to improve their income and food security.

#### 2. FAO (Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations)

As a specialized international agency for food and agriculture, FAO collaborates with African governments, regional bodies, and other partners to improve food security and achieve sustainable agriculture. Key initiatives include:
 * Policy and Technical Assistance: Supporting the formulation and implementation of national agricultural policies and promoting the adoption of sustainable agricultural technologies and practices. This includes soil management, water resource management, pest and disease management, and reduction of post-harvest losses.
 * Provision of Food Security Information: Supporting the monitoring of food supply and demand and the development of early warning systems to enhance the capacity to respond to food crises.
 * Emergency Assistance: Providing emergency food assistance and agricultural rehabilitation support in the event of food crises caused by natural disasters or conflicts.
 * "One Country One Priority Product (OCOP)" Initiative: Supporting countries to formalize and boost the production, processing, and marketing of their chosen priority agricultural products through more sustainable 'green' value chains to contribute to economic growth and food security.
 * Nutrition Improvement: Supporting the improvement of nutrition in Africa through the strengthening of nutrition policies, the promotion of healthy diets, and the improvement of access to food.
 * Adaptation to and Mitigation of Climate Change: Supporting efforts to reduce the impact of climate change on agriculture by introducing adaptation measures and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

**Specific Examples of Initiatives:**
 * Addressing climate-related livestock losses and livelihood strains in the Sahel region: Working with the Intergovernmental Authority on Development (IGAD) to implement emergency support projects for pastoralists and agro-pastoralists affected by climate change in East Africa.
 * Supporting the strengthening of nutrition policies: Assisting countries in enhancing policy and planning instruments for nutrition, healthy diets, and the right to adequate food.

#### 3. African Agricultural Organizations

Various agricultural organizations exist in Africa at regional, national, and farmer levels, each playing its respective role.
 * Regional Agricultural Organizations:
   * Eastern Africa Farmers Federation (EAFF): Represents the voices of farmers in the East Africa region, advocating for policies, sharing information, and providing capacity development.
   * West African Farmers' and Producers' Organizations (ROPPA): Networks farmers' organizations in the West Africa region, promoting policy dialogue and improved market access.
 * National-Level Agricultural Organizations: National farmer unions and agricultural cooperatives implement activities such as advocating for farmers' rights, providing technical guidance, supporting market access, and facilitating the joint purchase of inputs.
 * Thematic Agricultural Organizations: Organizations exist to support specific crops, livestock, organic farming, and women farmers, providing specialized knowledge and technology.

**Specific Examples of Initiatives:**
 * Sasakawa Africa Association (SAA): Supported by the Nippon Foundation, SAA has been providing agricultural assistance in 17 African countries for over 35 years. Currently focusing on Ethiopia, Mali, Nigeria, and Uganda, it supports smallholder farmers' food, nutrition, and income security through agricultural extension services, reduction of post-harvest losses, promotion of conservation agriculture, dissemination of high-nutrient crops, and agribusiness development.
 * One Acre Fund: An NGO operating in Kenya, Rwanda, and Burundi, providing smallholder farmers with access to financing, training, and high-quality seeds to increase yields and improve incomes.
 * African Development Foundation (ADF): Implemented a project in Ghana focused on improving cassava production, contributing to increased farmer income and local economic development.

**Collaboration and Coordination**

These institutions and organizations not only work independently but also collaborate and coordinate with each other to address agricultural development and food security issues more effectively. For example, the World Bank and FAO may jointly conduct research and analysis or implement joint projects. Furthermore, collaboration between international organizations and regional/national agricultural organizations is crucial for implementing activities at the grassroots level.
Agricultural development and food security measures in Africa are complex challenges that require the cooperation and continuous efforts of many stakeholders. By leveraging the strengths of each institution and organization and strengthening collaboration, greater results can be expected.

 * FAO's "Save and Grow" Approach: This method, which emphasizes the sustainable intensification of crop production, has resulted in farmers in countries like Brazil and India reporting increased yields and improved resilience to climate shocks.
 * One Acre Fund: In Kenya, participating farmers have reported an average income increase of over 50% within a few years of joining the program, thanks to a comprehensive support system including financing, training, and high-quality seeds.

#### 2. Economic Effects:
 * Importance of Agriculture-Led Development: The FAO emphasizes that agriculture-led development is fundamental to cutting hunger, reducing poverty, promoting economic growth, alleviating the burden of food imports, and paving the way for export expansion. Agriculture is the backbone of many African economies, making significant contributions to GDP, being a major source of foreign exchange, and the primary supplier of industrial raw materials.
 * Increased Rural Income: Improved agricultural performance has the potential to increase rural incomes and purchasing power for a large number of people. Agriculture has the capacity to uplift people on a mass scale more than any other sector.

#### 3. Technological Advancements:
 * Promotion of Digital Agriculture: The World Bank supports the introduction of data-driven digital agriculture, with the initial adoption of soil mapping and precision agriculture technologies.
 * Dissemination of Sustainable Agricultural Technologies: The FAO provides training and promotes the adoption of sustainable agricultural technologies, such as enhancing biodiversity, improving soil fertility, and reducing reliance on chemical inputs.
 * SAA (Sasakawa Africa Association) Activities: Approximately 10 million farmers have received agricultural extension services, and over 9,000 agronomists have acquired specialized knowledge.

#### 4. Development of Agricultural Businesses:
 * Promoting Investment in Agribusiness: The World Bank and FAO facilitate investment in agribusiness through public-private partnerships and other means, supporting the development of value chains.
 * Improving Market Access for Smallholder Farmers: The FAO's "Hand-in-Hand Initiative" aims to connect small-scale farmers with markets and resources, helping farmers in Ethiopia and Bangladesh, among others, to sell their products at better prices.
 * SHEP (Smallholder Horticultural Empowerment and Promotion) Approach: The SAA supports the strengthening of business skills and the fostering of entrepreneurship among smallholder horticultural farmers.

#### 5. Technical Improvement of African Agricultural Workers:
 * Implementation of Training Programs: Many agricultural organizations and international bodies conduct training programs on agricultural techniques, nutrition, and gender issues to enhance the knowledge and skills of farmers.
 * Training of Extension Workers: The SAA emphasizes capacity building by educating and training extension workers at agricultural universities.

#### 6. Increase in Income:
 * Income Growth through Increased Yields: As demonstrated by the One Acre Fund, increased yields resulting from appropriate support directly contribute to higher farmer incomes.
 * Income Growth through Improved Market Access: The FAO's initiatives and support for market linkages by NGOs enable farmers to sell under more favorable conditions, leading to income increases.

#### 7. Changes in Awareness of African Producers and Food Companies:
 * Growing Interest in Sustainable Agriculture: With increasing awareness of environmental issues and climate change, more producers and food companies are becoming interested in sustainable agriculture.
 * Focus on Nutrient-Rich Crops: Initiatives like the collaboration between SAA and HarvestPlus to promote biofortified crops are raising awareness about the importance of nutrition improvement.
 * Recognition of the Need for Fair Trade Models: Discussions aimed at reshaping partnerships between African and European food systems are leading to a growing recognition of the need for fairer global trade models.

#### 8. Impact on African Society:
 * Improved Food Security: Increased productivity and better market access are strengthening regional and national food security.
 * Poverty Reduction: Increased agricultural income significantly contributes to poverty reduction in rural areas.
 * Job Creation: The development of agriculture-related businesses is creating new employment opportunities, especially for young people.
 * Improved Nutritional Status: The promotion of nutrient-rich crops and nutrition education is improving people's nutritional status.
 * Strengthening of Communities: Support for the establishment of agricultural cooperatives and other initiatives strengthens collaboration among farmers and enhances community resilience.

#### Important Considerations:
 * These outcomes vary by region and project, and progress is not uniform across all areas.
 * The availability of data and differences in measurement methods can make it challenging to provide specific numerical figures in all cases.
 * The policies of national governments and the efforts of farmers themselves, in addition to external support, significantly influence these outcomes.
