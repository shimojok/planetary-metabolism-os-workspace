英語：

- African agriculture is vulnerable to the impacts of climate change, such as droughts, floods, pests and diseases, which reduce yields and food security⁴⁵. Agricultural aid needs to include adaptation measures and insurance schemes to cope with these risks.
- African agriculture relies on small-scale and traditional production methods, which result in low productivity and income, and trap farmers in poverty and hunger⁴⁶. Agricultural aid needs to promote sustainable development through capacity building, technological innovation, market access improvement and other means.
- African agriculture faces environmental problems such as land and water depletion and pollution, deforestation and soil degradation, which undermine ecosystem functions and biodiversity⁴⁵. Agricultural aid needs to disseminate and raise awareness of environmentally friendly agricultural technologies and practices.
- African agriculture cannot meet the growing food demand due to population growth and urbanization⁴⁶. Agricultural aid needs to aim at food security and nutrition improvement by establishing diverse and balanced food production systems and educating consumers.
- African agriculture is hindered by political, economic and social instability and conflicts⁴⁵. Agricultural aid needs to include peace building, conflict prevention, security restoration and other aspects.
- African agriculture loses labor force and human resources due to infectious diseases such as HIV/AIDS and malaria⁴⁵. Agricultural aid needs to include disease control and health care provision.
- African agriculture has low competitiveness in the international market and faces unfavorable trade conditions and tariff barriers⁴⁶. Agricultural aid needs to facilitate market entry by improving quality, creating added value, integrating regions and other means.
- African agriculture lacks policy, institutional and legal support, and suffers from insufficient protection of land rights, water rights and other aspects⁴⁶. Agricultural aid needs to cooperate with governments and local authorities to reform and establish agricultural policies and legal systems.
- African agriculture lacks data and information to evaluate the effects and impacts of agricultural aid⁴⁶. Agricultural aid needs to strengthen and share statistics and monitoring and evaluation systems.
