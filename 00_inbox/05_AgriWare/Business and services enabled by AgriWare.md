#### 2. Is it possible to establish a new insurance business linked with carbon credits? 

• Feasibility and Effectiveness: Carbon credits are emission rights obtained by reducing or absorbing greenhouse gas emissions. Carbon credits are traded in emission trading systems and voluntary offset markets. There are various risks associated with carbon credits. For example, the following risks can be considered:

• Policy Risk: The risk of fluctuations in the demand and price of carbon credits due to changes in international frameworks and domestic regulations related to climate change.

• Technical Risk: The risk of a decrease in the quantity and quality of carbon credits due to inaccurate or unstable technology used for measuring and verifying carbon reduction or absorption.

• Market Risk: The risk of price fluctuations in carbon credits due to changes in supply, demand, and liquidity.

• Environmental Risk: The risk of a decrease in the effect of carbon reduction or absorption due to climate change or natural disasters.

To address these risks, new insurance businesses linked with carbon credits are being developed. For example, the following insurance businesses can be considered:

• Hedge Insurance for Price Fluctuations in Carbon Credits: Insurance that compensates for losses due to price fluctuations in the trading of carbon credits.

• Guarantee Insurance for Non-performance of Carbon Credits: Insurance that compensates the buyer when the seller cannot fulfill the promised quantity or quality of carbon credits in the trading of carbon credits.

• Insurance for Environmental Risks of Carbon Credits: Insurance that compensates the creator when the effect of carbon reduction or absorption decreases due to climate change or natural disasters in the creation of carbon credits.

The AgriCloud platform can create carbon credits through carbon storage by MBT compost and ferrihydrite/humic substances, and carbon reduction by AgriWare®. Also, the quantity and quality of carbon credits can be ensured by quality control and production management by SafetyChain. When providing these services, collaborating with the above insurance businesses can reduce the risk of carbon credits and enhance their reliability and value.

• Business Overview: The AgriCloud platform conducts the following businesses to develop a new insurance business linked with carbon credits:

• Consulting Services for the Creation and Trading of Carbon Credits: Services that provide information, analysis, advice, training, and networking related to the creation and trading of carbon credits.

• Insurance Services for Risk Management and Compensation of Carbon Credits: Services that provide hedge insurance, guarantee insurance, and environmental risk insurance for price fluctuations, non-performance, and environmental risks of carbon credits.

• Services for Quality Control and Production Management of Carbon Credits: Services that ensure the quantity and quality of carbon credits using quality control and production management by SafetyChain.

• Overseas Examples: The following are examples of new insurance businesses linked with carbon credits overseas:

#### 5. Financial product development based on trade-off theory  

Feasibility and effectiveness:  MBT compost is a biotechnology that decomposes organic matter and fixes carbon simultaneously, and has multiple effects such as improving agricultural productivity, quality, reducing food loss and medical expenses, storing carbon and creating carbon credits. MBT compost has higher water retention, nutrients, microbial activity, pH adjustment ability, and soil improvement effect than conventional compost, and improves crop yield and quality. In addition, MBT compost contributes to reducing food loss and medical expenses by increasing the freshness of food. Furthermore, MBT compost suppresses greenhouse gas emissions and leads to the acquisition of carbon credits by stably storing carbon in the soil. These effects vary depending on the amount and duration of use of MBT compost, but in the long term, they are expected to be future benefits for MBT compost users.

MBT compost users can evaluate the value of MBT compost by converting future benefits into present value. However, the value of MBT compost may not be uniquely determined because its effects are accompanied by uncertainty and time lag. Therefore, it is possible to find the optimal balance between the value of MBT compost and the price of financial products based on trade-off theory. Trade-off theory is a theory that finds the optimal balance between the benefits and costs associated with the use of a resource. For example, MBT compost users can enjoy the effects of MBT compost by paying the price of financial products for the value of MBT compost. However, if the price of financial products is too high, MBT compost users will not be able to enjoy the effects of MBT compost. Conversely, if the price of financial products is too low, MBT compost providers will not be able to obtain revenue commensurate with the value of MBT compost. Therefore, it is assumed that there is an optimal balance between the value of MBT compost and the price of financial products. By finding this optimal balance, it is possible to develop financial products that satisfy both MBT compost users and providers. This is the idea of financial product development based on trade-off theory.

Business overview:  The business overview of financial product development based on trade-off theory is as follows.

• Business purpose: To develop financial products that satisfy both MBT compost users and providers, and to promote the diffusion and maximization of the effects of MBT compost.

• Business content: To find the optimal balance between the value of MBT compost and the price of financial products, follow the steps below.

• Step 1: Collect and analyze data on the effects of MBT compost to evaluate the value of MBT compost. The effects of MBT compost include improving agricultural productivity, quality, reducing food loss and medical expenses, storing carbon and creating carbon credits. Platform services such as AgriWare® are used to collect and analyze data.

• Step 2: To convert the value of MBT compost into present value, consider the uncertainty and time lag associated with the effects of MBT compost. Uncertainty and time lag include climate change, crop demand and supply, carbon market trends, etc. Methods to consider uncertainty and time lag include scenario analysis, Monte Carlo simulation, real option analysis, etc.

• Step 3: To find the optimal balance between the value of MBT compost and the price of financial products, investigate and analyze the needs and costs of both MBT compost users and providers. The needs and costs of MBT compost users include enjoying the effects of MBT compost, paying the price of financial products, adjusting the amount and duration of use of MBT compost, etc. The needs and costs of MBT compost providers include obtaining revenue commensurate with the value of MBT compost, managing the production and supply of MBT compost, ensuring the quality and safety of MBT compost, etc.

• Step 4: Once the optimal balance between the value of MBT compost and the price of financial products is found, design and develop financial products that reflect that balance. Types of financial products include bonds, stocks, options, futures, swaps, etc. Financial product design and development consider factors such as financial product features and conditions, risk and return, liquidity and price formation, legal, accounting, and tax aspects, etc.

• Business target: Both MBT compost users and providers are targeted. MBT compost users include farmers, food processors, food retailers, etc. MBT compost providers include MBT compost producers, sellers, etc.
