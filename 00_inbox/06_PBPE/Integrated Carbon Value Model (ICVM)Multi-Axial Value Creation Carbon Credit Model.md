🌿 Integrated Carbon Value Model (ICVM)
Multi-Axial Value Creation Carbon Credit Model

---

1. 🎯 Purpose of the Model

Difference from Conventional Models:  
- Conventional carbon credits: Based solely on CO₂ sequestration volume  
- ICVM: Integrates carbon sequestration with social cost reduction, resource recovery, quality enhancement, and health improvement

Objective:  
To treat carbon not as a pollutant to be reduced, but as a resource for generating value—realizing simultaneous regeneration of agriculture, environment, economy, and public health.

---

2. 🧬 Evaluation Axes

| Axis | Description | Example Indicators |
|------|-------------|--------------------|
| Carbon Sequestration | CO₂ fixation via soil, biomass, composting | tCO₂/ha/year (MBT55-based) |
| Soil Regeneration | Restoration of degraded soils | Soil Humus Index, MBT activity |
| Food Quality | Nutritional value, polyphenols, freshness retention | Polyphenol Index, Freshness Score |
| Food Loss Reduction | Reduction in waste due to freshness retention | kg/year, CO₂ equivalent |
| Healthcare Cost Reduction | Lower disease risk through improved nutrition | Health Score, medical cost savings |
| Resource Monetization | Conversion of waste into compost, fertilizer, energy | BioProduct Value ($/ton) |

---

3. 💰 Credit Architecture

✅ Carbon+ Credit (C⁺ Credit)

- Not just CO₂ sequestration, but a credit integrating multiple value axes
- Example:  
  1 C⁺ = 1tCO₂ + 10kg compost + 20% quality improvement + 5kg food loss reduction + $10 healthcare savings

✅ Use Cases

- Connection to ESG investment models (via ACAIN)
- Local currency, agricultural subsidies, insurance discounts
- Proposal for new international carbon credit frameworks

---

4. 🔗 Implementation Platform

AGRIX → ANE → ACAIN Integration

| Layer | Function | Credit Linkage |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| AGRIX | Data integration, SafetyChain | Verification of quality, freshness, nutrition |
| ANE | MBT circular agriculture, resource recovery | Composting, microbial activity, social issue linkage |
| ACAIN | Carbon management, financial integration | Evaluation, distribution, and financial design of C⁺ credits |

---

5. 🌍 Societal Impact

- Reduction of waste processing costs (food, livestock, forest residues)  
- Restoration of degraded soils and increased agricultural productivity  
- Reduction of food loss and healthcare costs via freshness retention  
- Revitalization of local economies and attraction of international investment

---

✳️ Strategic Message

> Carbon is not a pollutant to be offset—it is the starting point of regeneration and value creation.  
> ICVM goes beyond sequestration to enable simultaneous value creation across society, environment, economy, and health.  
> It is a new carbon model for a regenerative future.
