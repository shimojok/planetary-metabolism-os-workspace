We are now facing an unprecedented global challenge—climate change. Its impacts are severe, causing extreme weather events such as floods, droughts, and wildfires, which threaten food production, water resources, energy supply, economic activity, and our daily lives.

Why Must We Act Now?

One of the main causes of climate change is the loss of carbon from soil and the resulting destruction of ecosystems. For years, agricultural expansion, deforestation, and urbanization have depleted organic matter in our soil, disrupting the carbon cycle. However, if we restore our soil and harness the power of microorganisms, we can store carbon again and help mitigate climate change.

The Innovation Brought by AGRIX Project

Through MBT55 (Microbial Biotechnology) and MBT Sustainable Cycle (Organic Matter Decomposition System), we have demonstrated that degraded soil can be regenerated, the carbon cycle can be reactivated, and food production can become more sustainable. This technology enables:
✅ Soil restoration and enhanced agricultural productivity
✅ Carbon sequestration and climate change mitigation
✅ Cost reduction through domestic fertilizer production
✅ Stabilization of food prices
✅ Recycling and repurposing of food waste

This technology has the potential to fundamentally transform agriculture in Africa.
