**"AquaTower"** is a city-based high-rise agricultural facility unaffected by climate change disasters, ensuring stable production. The basic structure includes 1ha per floor, featuring vertical cultivation, artificial photosynthesis, **MBT** functional liquid fertilizer circulation, "SeaGenesis Water" utilization, and innovative agricultural water use via the **AquaMake** system, all without soil, ensuring efficient production through film farming technology. It also enables aquaculture of both seawater and freshwater fish, with MBT materials playing a crucial role.

Lower floors function as food storage facilities, integrated with logistics systems, functioning as part of the international food system managed by the **AGRIX** Platform alongside **BioValley**, facilitating market transactions. Primary processing facilities are also provided, with all food residues converted into MBT materials through the **MBT Sustainable Cycle**, realizing a circular production model.

Cultivation using MBT liquid fertilizer achieves increased yield, improved quality, and enhanced freshness, ensuring productivity and quality of MBT compost. Additionally, vertical cultivation leads to increased yields of fruit-bearing vegetables such as tomatoes, melons, watermelons, and pumpkins, extending yields to approximately eight times compared to traditional agriculture, contributing to food security enhancement.

The AquaTower Model allows for farm installations in urban areas, reducing transportation distances and environmental footprint. Utilizing idle facilities improves operational efficiency, making AquaTower a promising sustainable investment opportunity. BioValley and AquaTower projects contribute to addressing future food issues, soil restoration, and climate change mitigation, thereby contributing to environmental conservation, societal stability, and climate change adaptation.

#### Characteristics and Novelty of the Next-Generation Urban High-rise Agriculture Facility "AquaTower Model":

1. Fusion of Vertical Hydroponics and Aquaculture

Combining hydroponic cultivation and aquaculture systems on each floor to establish a cyclical system that utilizes the excrement of aquatic organisms as fertilizer.
Significantly reduces water usage compared to conventional agriculture, thus reducing environmental impact.
Suitable for installation in urban areas, contributing to increased food self-sufficiency.

2. Hybrid Lighting with Solar and Artificial Photosynthesis

Achieving stable production unaffected by weather conditions through the maximal utilization of solar energy alongside artificial photosynthesis.
Expanding the variety of cultivated crops and enabling the production of high-value agricultural products.
Improved production efficiency through 24-hour operation.

3. Automation and Optimization through AI

Automating and optimizing cultivation environments, irrigation, fertilization, etc., through AI to reduce labor costs and achieve high-quality crop production.
Enabling more precise agricultural management through data analysis-based variety selection and harvest time prediction, contributing to addressing the aging workforce in agriculture.

4. Multi-functionality as an Urban Farm

Enhancing multifunctionality as an urban farm with rooftop greening, educational facilities, tourist attractions, etc., contributing to local revitalization.
Generating various benefits such as promoting local consumption, providing food education programs, and improving the urban environment.
Increasing urban residents' interest in food and contributing to the realization of a sustainable society.

5. Food Supply Hub during Disasters

Featuring a disaster-resistant structure to ensure stable food supply during earthquakes, floods, tsunamis, etc.
Operates with solar power generation and batteries during power outages, contributing to business continuity planning (BCP).
Can be utilized as evacuation shelters during disasters.

6. Utilization as an International Food System

Installing AquaTower globally to establish an international food network.
Contributing to support for food-deficient areas, reduction of food loss, and strengthening international food security.
Contributes to regional economic development.

7. Potential of Agriculture in Space

Applying AquaTower technology to realize agriculture in space.
Addressing future population growth on Earth, contributing to space exploration and Mars colonization.
Expanding the habitat for humanity and pioneering new frontiers.

**These innovative and novel ideas hold the potential to evolve AquaTower into a more innovative and sustainable food production system, contributing to the resolution of global food issues.**
