**Compost is a** [fertilizer](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%82%A5%E6%96%99 "fertilizer") or soil conditioner in which easily decomposable [organic matter](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%89%E6%A9%9F%E7%89%A9 "organic matter") is completely decomposed by microorganisms . It may be used synonymously with [organic materials](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%89%E6%A9%9F%E8%B3%87%E6%9D%90 "Organic materials") ( [organic fertilizers ), but organic materials include easily decomposable organic matter including undecomposed organic matter residues, while compost refers to those in which easily decomposable organic matter is completely decomposed.](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%89%E6%A9%9F%E8%B3%AA%E8%82%A5%E6%96%99 "Organic fertilizer")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%82%A5%E6%96%99 "fertilizer")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%89%E6%A9%9F%E8%B3%87%E6%9D%90 "Organic materials")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%89%E6%A9%9F%E8%B3%AA%E8%82%A5%E6%96%99 "Organic fertilizer")

In English, it is called **compost** , and in this section, [compost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/compost "en: compost") and **compost are** **treated** as synonymous. In some cases, the compost, which is the product of the swill composting container, turns and the swill composting container [is](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%B3%E3%83%B3%E3%83%9D%E3%82%B9%E3%82%BF%E3%83%BC#%E7%94%9F%E3%81%94%E3%81%BF%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5%E5%8C%96%E5%AE%B9%E5%99%A8 "Compost heap") called **compost .**

## 

Effect of compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=1 "Edit Section: The effect of compost")

Compost has the effect of improving the chemical, physical and biological properties of the soil [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) .

Improving soil chemistry

[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%AA%92%E7%B4%A0 "nitrogen")Supply of [nitrogen](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%AA%92%E7%B4%A0 "nitrogen") , [phosphoric acid](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%AA%E3%83%B3%E9%85%B8 "phosphoric acid") and [potassium](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%AB%E3%83%AA%E3%82%A6%E3%83%A0 "potassium") ( [three elements of fertilizer )](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%82%A5%E6%96%99%E3%81%AE%E4%B8%89%E8%A6%81%E7%B4%A0 "Three elements of fertilizer") [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) . In addition, it contains a large amount of elements such as lime and [magnesium](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%9E%E3%82%B0%E3%83%8D%E3%82%B7%E3%82%A6%E3%83%A0 "magnesium") and trace elements such as boron and iron, and is a direct source of fertilizer [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) [[BioNexus Concept 0607 2]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-%E4%B8%96%E7%95%8C%E5%8E%9F%E8%89%B2%E7%99%BE%E7%A7%91%E4%BA%8B%E5%85%B8%E3%83%BB%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5-2) . In addition, soil organic matter produced in the process of compost decomposition is also effective in improving fertility [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) .

Improving soil physics

The softening of the soil improves breathability, water retention, drainage, and improves root growth and nutrient absorption of plants [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) . It promotes soil aggregate formation, improves soil physics, and improves the environment of the rhizosphere of crops [[BioNexus Concept 0607 2]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-%E4%B8%96%E7%95%8C%E5%8E%9F%E8%89%B2%E7%99%BE%E7%A7%91%E4%BA%8B%E5%85%B8%E3%83%BB%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5-2) .

Improving the biological properties of the soil

Increasing the number of compost decomposers (soil animals, fungi, etc.) enriches the biota and suppresses the growth of pests and harmful pathogens [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) .

The above are interrelated, and compost improves the physical properties such as air permeability and water retention of the soil, which also has the effect of improving the microbial activity environment [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) . It also has the effect of adsorbing and retaining nutrients and binding to alumina and heavy metals that are harmful to crops to protect the roots of plants [[BioNexus Concept 0607 2]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-%E4%B8%96%E7%95%8C%E5%8E%9F%E8%89%B2%E7%99%BE%E7%A7%91%E4%BA%8B%E5%85%B8%E3%83%BB%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5-2) .

In terms of production, it also has the effect of being able to meet consumer needs for environmentally friendly, safe and secure agricultural products [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) . It is said that it also has the effect of improving the taste, color, and storability of the product because the vitality of the plant is increased [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) .

## 

Composting process[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=2 "Edit Section: Composting Process")

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/CompostBinTube_wb.jpg/220px-CompostBinTube_wb.jpg)](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%95%E3%82%A1%E3%82%A4%E3%83%AB:CompostBinTube_wb.jpg)

Commercial compost container

See " [composting "](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5%E5%8C%96 "Composting")

Composting is the production of compost, which is defined as "to a state where biological waste can be safely returned to soil under certain controlled conditions, with ease of handling, good storability and no harm to the environment. Microbial decomposition ”(Goluke, 1977). Certain controlled conditions mean artificially creating a significant environment for composting microorganisms. In addition, there are various problems as fertilizer when the decomposition of organic matter is incomplete. Composting is the process of artificially decomposing until these problems no longer occur.

In order to activate the activity of composting microorganisms, it is necessary to prepare the following conditions. [Carbon](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%82%AD%E7%B4%A0 "carbon") and [nitrogen](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%AA%92%E7%B4%A0 "nitrogen") balance ( [C / N ratio](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/C/N%E6%AF%94 "C / N ratio") ), [water content](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%90%AB%E6%B0%B4%E7%8E%87 "Moisture content") , [pH](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%B0%B4%E7%B4%A0%E3%82%A4%E3%82%AA%E3%83%B3%E6%8C%87%E6%95%B0 "Hydrogen ion index") , [temperature](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%B8%A9%E5%BA%A6 "temperature") and [oxygen](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%85%B8%E7%B4%A0 "oxygen") . If the above conditions are not optimal, the rate of decomposition will slow down, the quality of the product will deteriorate, and [nitrogen starvation](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%AA%92%E7%B4%A0%E9%A3%A2%E9%A4%93 "Nitrogen hunger") of the crop will occur.

### Fast compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=3 "Edit Section: Fast Compost")

Compost made from chemical fertilizers reaffirmed the need for compost in the 1960s to maintain soil fertility, so non-stock farmers use surplus straw and commercially available nitrogen fertilizers to ripen faster than usual. It is a compost that has been made to.  
The method of making is to add 1.5 kg (weight ratio 250: 1) of nitrogen fertilizer (especially lime nitrogen) that promotes ripening to 375 kg of straw that has been sufficiently moistened by the day before stacking to prevent heat retention and drying. It is covered and transshipped once every 2-3 weeks, and it becomes medium-ripened compost in about 6 weeks [[4]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-4) .

### Compost maturity[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=4 "Edit Section: Compost Ripeness")

The required compost maturity varies from crop to crop [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) . As the maturity of the compost progresses, the shape of the raw material almost disappears and the water content becomes around 50% [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) .

Compost quality standards[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=5 "Edit Section: Compost Quality Standards")

Since the raw materials for compost such as livestock manure compost are various, the fertilizer components are also diverse, and the purpose of use of the compost and the required conditions are also different, which compost is better is comprehensively judged according to the crop and soil conditions. Must be [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) . From the standpoint of making compost, the ease of handling during distribution and spraying is important, so it is better to have less moisture, smoother texture, and less odor as fermentation progresses [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) . In addition, from the standpoint of using compost, the fertilizer component of compost, soil improvement effect, safety, price and acquisition cost are emphasized, and the expected effect of compost is supply of fertilizer component or soil improvement (improvement of soil physicality). ) Also depends on [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

organic matter

The recommended standard value in "Recommended Standards for Livestock Manure Compost" ( [National Federation of Agricultural Cooperatives](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%85%A8%E5%9B%BD%E8%BE%B2%E6%A5%AD%E5%8D%94%E5%90%8C%E7%B5%84%E5%90%88%E9%80%A3%E5%90%88%E4%BC%9A "National Federation of Agricultural Cooperatives") , 1994) is 60% or more [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

C / N ratio

Shows the ratio of carbon and nitrogen in compost. Carbon in compost is derived from [carbohydrates](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%82%AD%E6%B0%B4%E5%8C%96%E7%89%A9 "carbohydrate") , and compost made mainly of plant matter such as straw, fallen leaves, sawdust, and bark contains a large amount of carbon, so the C / N ratio is large [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) . On the other hand, nitrogen in compost is derived from [protein , and compost whose main raw material is animal matter such as livestock manure, meat and fish residue has a large amount of nitrogen, so the C / N ratio is small](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%81%9F%E3%82%93%E3%81%B1%E3%81%8F%E8%B3%AA "protein") [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) .

Compost with high nitrogen content and low C / N ratio is considered to be "fertilizer effect type" compost [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) . However, if the C / N ratio is too small, it may cause concentration disorders during germination, poor root development, and soil diseases [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) .

Compost with low nitrogen content and high C / N ratio is considered to be a "root-building effect type" compost that is effective in improving air permeability and water retention by agglomeration of soil [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) . However, if the C / N ratio is too large, nitrogen will be consumed due to the decomposition of organic matter, which may lead to poor growth of crops [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) . The recommended standard value in "Recommended Standards for Livestock Manure Compost" (National Federation of Agricultural Cooperatives, 1994) is 30 or less [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

Heavy metal concentration

Copper and zinc concentrations. These are trace elements necessary for crops, but too much can harm the crop. The recommended standard values ​​in the "Recommended Standards for Livestock Manure Compost" (National Federation of Agricultural Cooperatives, 1994) are copper 600 ppm or less and zinc 1,800 ppm or less [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

[Electrical conductivity](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%9B%BB%E6%B0%97%E4%BC%9D%E5%B0%8E%E7%8E%87 "Electrical conductivity") (EC)

The amount of ions contained in the compost. The recommended standard value in the "Recommended Standards for Livestock Manure Compost" (National Federation of Agricultural Cooperatives, 1994) is 5.0 dS / m or less [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

## 

Types of compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=6 "Edit Section: Compost Type")

### Livestock manure compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=7 "Edit Section: Livestock Manure Compost")

The most composted is the composting of livestock manure. Mainly used are [cattle manure compost](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%89%9B%E7%B3%9E "Cow dung") , pig manure compost, and chicken manure compost.

-   Cow manure compost has a high water content and a low fast-acting fertilizer component, while it has a high content of persistent organic matter and a high slow-release fertilizer component [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) . In cattle manure compost, when the feed for cattle is high in forage, potassium is high, while nitrogen, phosphoric acid, and lime are low, and decomposition in soil is slowed down [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) . Generally, it has more lignin and carbohydrates than pig manure compost and chicken manure compost, and the fertilizer efficiency of nitrogen is low [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) .
-   Pig manure compost tends to have less nitrogen than chicken manure, higher than cattle manure, lime higher than cattle manure, and potassium lower than cattle manure [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) . In addition, pig manure compost generally has a higher copper and zinc content than compost from other livestock species [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) .
-   Chicken manure compost has a low C / N ratio and a higher lime content than compost from other livestock species [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) . Some chicken manure compost is mixed with wood-based materials and some is not, and the fertilizing effect is different . [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) In Japan, compost is classified as a special fertilizer under the law regarding ensuring the quality of fertilizer, but some chicken manure whose content of fertilizer components is guaranteed is a normal fertilizer, "Processed house fertilizer". Registered and sold as [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

Pig manure compost and chicken manure compost are low in water content, especially in the fertilizer component of phosphoric acid, but the content of persistent organic matter is low (carbohydrates are similar in pig manure compost and chicken manure compost) [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) . Pig manure compost and chicken manure compost are used as fertilizer-based materials, and cow manure compost is used as soil-making material . [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6)

### Rice straw compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Rice straw compost")

Rice straw is easy to compost, but the C / N ratio is about 60 to 70, so it is necessary to add a nitrogen source to reduce the C / N ratio to about 30 to 40 to promote ripening [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) .

### Rice husk compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: Rice husk compost")

Rice husks are less likely to be composted than rice straw, and it is necessary to use livestock manure as a nitrogen source for C / N ratio adjustment to promote putrefaction [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) .

### Wood compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=10 "Edit Section: Wood Compost")

#### Pruning branch compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Pruning branch compost")

Pruned branches of fruit trees such as apples are crushed with a chipper and mixed with chicken manure and lime nitrogen as a nitrogen source to compost [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) .

#### Bark compost[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=12 "Edit Section: Bark Compost")

It is a compost made from coniferous bark, which is difficult to ripen, but it is used because it has a high effect of improving physical soil such as water retention and fertility [[6]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-tuti301-6) . Some are fermented only with bark, while others are fermented with livestock manure added to the bark [[AWS Health Suite と変動生存給付型生命保険の連携]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-taihigaido2-1) .

### Swill-like fertilizer[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: Swill-like fertilizer")

A facility for composting swill discharged from companies is being constructed for the purpose of reducing the amount of garbage. In addition, composting of swill is also carried out in ordinary households using composters [.](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%B3%E3%83%B3%E3%83%9D%E3%82%B9%E3%82%BF%E3%83%BC "Compost heap") The content of nitrogen, phosphoric acid and potassium is rather low [[BioNexus Concept 0607]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-BOOKLET-3) .

### Each organic fertilizer[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=15 "Edit Section: Each Organic Fertilizer")

Compost is a type of organic fertilizer made from animal and vegetable organic matter [[7]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-mlit-7) . Other organic fertilizers include animal and vegetable fertilizers (fish lees powder, rapeseed oil lees, bone meal, etc.) and organic by-product fertilizers (sludge fertilizer, etc.), but these are distinguished [[7]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-mlit-7) .

#### Manure[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=17 "Edit Section: Manure")

[Manure](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%8E%A9%E8%82%A5 "Manure") is a fertilizer made from manure from livestock and straw.

[In medieval](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E4%B8%AD%E4%B8%96 "middle Ages") Japan, horse manure excreted from horses raised by [samurai for military use was used as fertilizer for the fields of self and the territories under its control.](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%AD%A6%E5%A3%AB "samurai")

#### Humanure[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=18 "Edit Section: Humanure")

Humanure is a new term for human waste that is composted and reused for agricultural or other purposes. The term became known by Joseph Jenkins in the 1999 book "Humanure Handbook", which explains the use of this [organic soil conditioner .](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%9C%89%E6%A9%9F%E8%B3%87%E6%9D%90 "Organic materials")

Humanure differs from classic sewage treated in waste treatment facilities (sewage also contains waste from industrial and other sources), manure, paper, and substances containing additional carbon (sawdust, etc.). ).

Humanure is safe for humans to use in crops as long as human waste is properly composted. This is because the waste heats up sufficiently until the thermophilic decomposition removes the harmful pathogens, and / or after the addition of new fertilizer, sufficient time has passed for microbiological activity to kill most pathogens. Means you have to. For the purpose of being safe for use in crops, a second step of medium temperature process is often required to remove phytotoxins.

Humanure is separate from manure (raw human waste that is applied to crops).

Compost challenges[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=20 "Edit Section: Compost Challenge")

detail

**This section has no or inadequate verifiable [references](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:%E6%A4%9C%E8%A8%BC%E5%8F%AF%E8%83%BD%E6%80%A7 "Wikipedia: Testability") [or sources .](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:%E4%BF%A1%E9%A0%BC%E3%81%A7%E3%81%8D%E3%82%8B%E6%83%85%E5%A0%B1%E6%BA%90 "Wikipedia: Reliable source of information")**

Problems of weeds, pests and pathogens derived from compost

The safety and quality required for livestock manure compost include the absence of problems with weeds, pests, and pathogens derived from compost [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) . If the material is the weed itself, weed seeds may survive during the composting process. Also, if livestock manure is the material, weed seeds mixed in the [feed](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E9%A3%BC%E6%96%99 "feed") may be alive. When weed seeds are applied with live compost, it causes weeds to grow in the field. As a point of livestock manure composting, it is a guideline that the fermentation temperature was maintained at 60 ° C or higher for 2 days or more at the time of composting [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

Odor problem when spraying

To prevent bad odors when spraying, it is preferable to avoid immature compost or use compost that is not too dry and has less dust [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

Obstacles due to immature compost

Applying immature compost can cause various problems.

-   Nitrogen deficiency-The components of compost are assessed by the ratio of carbon to nitrogen ( [C / N ratio ).](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/C/N%E6%AF%94 "C / N ratio") Immature compost can cause nitrogen starvation in the soil due to the incomplete decomposition of carbon components.
-   Oxygen damage-When fertilizing compost in which easily decomposable organic matter is not completely decomposed, organic matter is decomposed in the soil, and oxygen damage causes great damage to crop roots and soil ecosystems.

Heavy metal problem

Use heavy metals with a low concentration [[５．Amazonのネット・ゼロ達成を加速する 修正版]](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5#cite_note-okayama-5) .

[Potassium](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%AB%E3%83%AA%E3%82%A6%E3%83%A0 "potassium") release

Potassium is present in [cells](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%B4%B0%E8%83%9E "cell") in the form of [ions .](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%A4%E3%82%AA%E3%83%B3 "ion") Therefore, when an organism dies, potassium is easily eluted from the cells. This is a big difference from nitrogen incorporated in various organic substances. Focusing solely on nitrogen, compost is a slow-acting fertilizer, while focusing solely on potassium, compost is a fast-acting fertilizer. Therefore, the amount of compost applied is limited to the amount of potassium contained in the compost. However, it is not usually done to use only compost as fertilizer (because if only compost is used as fertilizer, the nitrogen component is insufficient). Therefore, excess potassium is likely to occur.

By leaving the compost in the rain for several years, potassium dissolves in the rain and runs away. Applying such compost does not result in excess potassium. However, potassium flowing from compost may lead to [groundwater pollution.](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%9C%B0%E4%B8%8B%E6%B0%B4 "Groundwater") In Japan, it is prohibited by the [Livestock Excretion Law](https://www.maff.go.jp/j/chikusan/kankyo/taisaku/t_mondai/03_about/index.html) for livestock farmers of a certain size to pile up compost in a rainy state .

[Antibiotics](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%8A%97%E7%94%9F%E7%89%A9%E8%B3%AA "Antibiotics")

The excrement when veterinary drugs are administered to livestock contains antibiotics. The safety of compost made from this antibiotic-containing excrement has not yet been clarified.

Sawdust compost

Sawdust contains phenolic acids that have an adverse effect on crops, and some people consider this a problem. However, there are reports that if composting is sufficient, no problems will occur.

Salt problem

Swill and livestock manure contain salt. Therefore, it has been pointed out that crops may have a salt concentration disorder.

Compost ecosystem[edit](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=%E5%A0%86%E8%82%A5&action=edit&section=21 "Edit Section: Compost Ecosystem")

detail

**This section has no or inadequate verifiable [references](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:%E6%A4%9C%E8%A8%BC%E5%8F%AF%E8%83%BD%E6%80%A7 "Wikipedia: Testability") [or sources .](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:%E4%BF%A1%E9%A0%BC%E3%81%A7%E3%81%8D%E3%82%8B%E6%83%85%E5%A0%B1%E6%BA%90 "Wikipedia: Reliable source of information")**

Compost production also means creating an appropriate [ecosystem for the decomposition of organic matter.](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%94%9F%E6%85%8B%E7%B3%BB "Ecosystem") For efficient composting, an appropriate environment for the activity of degrading biota must be maintained. The raw materials for compost provide not only microorganisms that directly decompose organic matter, but also organisms that prey on the [decomposers and housing.](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%88%86%E8%A7%A3%E8%80%85 "Decomposer") Their emissions are also part of the process of decomposition.

Among the degrading organisms, the most directly acting are [microorganisms](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%BE%AE%E7%94%9F%E7%89%A9 "Microorganisms") such as [bacteria](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%90%E3%82%AF%E3%83%86%E3%83%AA%E3%82%A2 "Bacteria") . Among them, [fungi](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%8F%8C%E9%A1%9E "Fungi") , [filamentous fungi](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%B3%B8%E7%8A%B6%E8%8F%8C "Filamentous fungus") , [protists](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%8E%9F%E7%94%9F%E7%94%9F%E7%89%A9 "Protists") , [actinomycetes](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%94%BE%E7%B7%9A%E8%8F%8C "Actinomycetes") (bacteria that often appear as white fibers in decomposed organic matter) and the like are important. [Earthworms](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%9F%E3%83%9F%E3%82%BA "Earthworm") , [ants](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%A2%E3%83%AA "Ants") , [snails](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%AB%E3%82%BF%E3%83%84%E3%83%A0%E3%83%AA "snails") , [slugs](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%8A%E3%83%A1%E3%82%AF%E3%82%B8 "Slugs") , [millipedes](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%A4%E3%82%B9%E3%83%87 "Millipede") , [woodlouse, collembolan](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%88%E3%83%93%E3%83%A0%E3%82%B7 "Collembola") , [etc.](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%AF%E3%83%A9%E3%82%B8%E3%83%A0%E3%82%B7 "Woodlouse") also contribute to the consumption and decomposition of organic matter. [Centipedes](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%A0%E3%82%AB%E3%83%87 "centipede") and other predators feed on these degrading organisms.[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%BE%AE%E7%94%9F%E7%89%A9 "Microorganisms")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%8F%8C%E9%A1%9E "Fungi")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%B3%B8%E7%8A%B6%E8%8F%8C "Filamentous fungus")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E5%8E%9F%E7%94%9F%E7%94%9F%E7%89%A9 "Protists")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%94%BE%E7%B7%9A%E8%8F%8C "Actinomycetes")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%9F%E3%83%9F%E3%82%BA "Earthworm")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%A2%E3%83%AA "Ants")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%82%AB%E3%82%BF%E3%83%84%E3%83%A0%E3%83%AA "snails")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%8A%E3%83%A1%E3%82%AF%E3%82%B8 "Slugs")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%A4%E3%82%B9%E3%83%87 "Millipede")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%AF%E3%83%A9%E3%82%B8%E3%83%A0%E3%82%B7 "Woodlouse")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%88%E3%83%93%E3%83%A0%E3%82%B7 "Collembola")[](https://ja.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E3%83%A0%E3%82%AB%E3%83%87 "centipede")
