
### MBT Herbal Probioticsプロジェクトの目的

MBT Probioticsプロジェクトは、分子栄養学の視点により代謝阻害要因を解析し、必要とされるアミノ酸やファイトケミカルや食物繊維を利用したプロバイオティクスを開発し、代謝改善を目指す"バイオトランスフォーメーション"です。栄養と疾病の関係を長くリサーチしてきた私達は、ヒトの代謝経路と腸内細菌が生成する代謝物質、そして、植物が生成するファイトケミカルに注目しました。

私達は同様の手法で、漢方薬の薬効成分にもチャレンジします。アルカノイドやファイトケミカルなどの薬効成分が、どのように人体に作用し効果が得られるのか、その作用機序は完全には解明されていません。MBT Herbal Probioticsプロジェクトは、MBT55微生物群を用いて、生薬成分の代謝（分解・発酵）を研究することで、漢方薬の作用機序の解明にも期待がかかります。また、MBT55は生薬成分を分解し、様々な代謝物を生成することが分かっています。これらの代謝物は、生薬成分よりも高い有効性を持つ可能性があります。

### Objectives of the MBT Herbal Probiotics Project

The MBT Probiotics aims to analyze metabolic inhibitors from the perspective of molecular nutrition and develop probiotics utilizing essential amino acids, phytochemicals, and dietary fiber to improve metabolism, representing a "biotransformation" approach. Having conducted extensive research on the relationship between nutrition and disease, we have focused on human metabolic pathways, metabolites produced by gut microbiota, and phytochemicals generated by plants.

We will also apply similar methods to explore the active components of traditional Chinese medicines (TCM). The mechanisms by which alkaloids and phytochemicals exert their effects on the human body are not yet fully understood. The MBT Herbal Probiotics seeks to clarify these mechanisms by studying the metabolism (degradation and fermentation) of herbal medicine components using MBT55 microorganisms. Additionally, MBT55 has been shown to break down herbal components and produce various metabolites, which may possess higher efficacy than the original herbal ingredients.

**Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) and Gut Microbiome: Unveiling the Mechanisms of TCM's Efficacy**

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) has a long and rich history, dating back to the Han dynasty and the seminal work of Zhang Zhongjing's "Treatise on Cold Febrile Diseases" (Shang Han Lun). Despite its widespread use and clinical effectiveness, the mechanisms of action of many TCM herbal remedies remain largely unknown, with only a few isolated bioactive compounds having been identified. This complexity, coupled with individual variability and seasonal fluctuations in efficacy, has hindered the development of a more comprehensive understanding of TCM's therapeutic effects.

The advent of microbiome research has shed new light on the potential role of gut bacteria in mediating TCM's efficacy. Emerging evidence suggests that the therapeutic properties of TCM herbs may be attributed, at least in part, to their ability to modulate the composition and function of the gut microbiome. This intricate interplay between TCM and the gut microbiome highlights the importance of considering the gut microbial ecosystem as an integral component of TCM's therapeutic strategy.

**Harnessing the Power of Phytonutrients and Alkaloids for Metabolic Improvement**

Humans have long utilized plant-derived phytonutrients and alkaloid-rich medicinal herbs as both dietary supplements and therapeutic agents. Recent advances in understanding the secondary metabolites of plants, their metabolic pathways, their interactions with gut bacteria, and the utilization of their metabolites by the human body have opened up new avenues for developing more efficient and effective metabolic-enhancing compounds.

**A Comprehensive Approach to Unraveling TCM's Mechanisms**

To fully elucidate the mechanisms underlying TCM's efficacy, a multi-pronged approach is necessary:

1. **Establishment of Phytonutrient and Alkaloid Libraries:** Create comprehensive libraries of phytonutrients and alkaloids, including those derived from medicinal herbs, to facilitate the identification and characterization of bioactive compounds.
2. **Data Integration and Analysis:** Integrate and analyze existing knowledge from classical TCM texts, such as the "Treatise on Cold Febrile Diseases," to extract information on herbal efficacies and treatment protocols.
3. **Modernization of TCM Knowledge:** Convert traditional TCM knowledge, including acupuncture, moxibustion, and Tui Na massage techniques, into modern, data-driven formats for further analysis and application.
4. **MBT55 Microbiome-Mediated Metabolic Investigations:** Investigate the metabolic pathways and metabolites generated by the MBT55 microbial consortium in response to phytonutrients and alkaloids, and correlate these findings with their therapeutic effects.
5. **Development of MBT Probiotics for Metabolic Improvement:** Utilize the insights gained from microbiome research to develop novel MBT Probiotics that enhance metabolic processes and contribute to healthcare cost reduction.

By pursuing these research avenues, we can unravel the complex mechanisms underlying TCM's efficacy and pave the way for the development of next-generation metabolic-enhancing therapies that integrate the wisdom of traditional medicine with the power of modern microbiome science.
