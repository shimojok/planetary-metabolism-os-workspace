### **Malaria Control with MBT55: A Next-Generation, Integrated Approach from Ecosystem to Host**

The fight against malaria faces a triple challenge: the emergence of insecticide-resistant mosquitoes, the spread of drug-resistant parasites, and fragile healthcare infrastructures. In response to these challenges, MBT55 offers not a single drug or technology, but a **sustainable biological control strategy** that harmonizes with the ecosystem, acting as a trinity on the **"Environment," "Vector," and "Human Host."**

#### **Strategy 1: Ecological Vector Control**

MBT55 targets the entire lifecycle of the mosquito, comprehensively inhibiting its development from breeding to human contact.

| Phase | Mechanism of Action of MBT55 | Expected Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **① Source Control (Larvicide)** | **Water Purification & Organic Matter Decomposition**: The diverse decomposer microbes in MBT55 (e.g., cellulose-degrading bacteria, nitrification-producing bacteria) rapidly break down and purify organic matter in stagnant water and wetlands, which serve as breeding grounds for mosquito larvae. | **Fundamental Destruction of Breeding Habitats**: This alters the microbial flora that larvae feed on, creating an aquatic environment unsuitable for their survival. It allows for the permanent suppression of mosquito sources without the use of chemical insecticides. |
| **② Adult Mosquito Control (Repellent/Insecticide)** | **Production of Bio-derived Repellent & Insecticidal Substances**: As demonstrated in mite control for honeybees, the metabolites of MBT55, such as **sesquiterpenoids**, may contain substances that act on the insect nervous system. | **Development of Next-Generation Bio-insecticides & Repellents**: Utilizing these metabolites, we can develop new spatial repellents and insecticides with low environmental impact and a low probability of resistance development, complementing or replacing existing mosquito nets and insecticides. |
| **③ Enhancement of Physical Defense** | **Contact Inhibition by Biopolymers**: Applying the experience of forming a protective layer on honeybees, the biopolymers produced by MBT55 can be used to coat mosquito nets and the walls of houses. | **A Sustainable Physical Barrier**: This makes it physically difficult for mosquitoes to rest on walls or nets, reducing opportunities for blood-feeding. As it does not rely on the evaporation of chemicals, the effect is long-lasting. |

#### **Strategy 2: Host Resilience Enhancement**

MBT55 builds an "internal defense wall" to prevent the onset and progression to severe disease even if the malaria parasite enters the body.

| Phase | Mechanism of Action of MBT55 | Expected Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **① Strengthening the Immune Foundation** | **Dramatic Improvement of the Gut Environment**: MBT55-derived probiotics (such as reCLA) optimize the gut microbiota, strengthen the barrier function of the intestinal tract where 70% of immune cells are concentrated, and promote the production of **Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs)**. | **Enhancement of Systemic Immune Response**: A strengthened gut immunity activates the entire immune system, enabling a rapid and effective initial immune response against malaria parasites. This significantly reduces the risk of infection and severe illness, especially in **children under five** with underdeveloped immunity. |
| **② Creation of Anti-malarial Active Substances** | **Application of Kampo Probiotics Technology**: Using MBT55's powerful fermentation capabilities, locally available medicinal plants (e.g., *Artemisia* species containing artemisinin) can be fermented to increase the bioavailability of their active compounds or to create new metabolites with anti-malarial activity. | **Development of Low-Cost Adjuvant Therapies & Prophylactics**: This maximizes the efficacy of medicinal plants, providing them as affordable and safe nutritional supplements or preventive agents. It offers a new alternative to the problem of resistance to existing drugs. |
| **③ Improvement of Nutritional Status** | **Improved Nutrient Absorption**: MBT55 aids in the digestion of food, promoting the absorption of essential amino acids and vitamins. | **Fundamental Improvement in Resistance to Infectious Diseases**: Malnutrition is a primary cause of immunodeficiency. By maximizing nutrient absorption from meals, MBT55 improves the basic physical strength of individuals, especially pregnant women and children, thereby increasing their resistance to all infectious diseases, including malaria. |

MBT55 is a technology that could become an extremely promising "game-changer" in the Gates Foundation's quest for innovation to eradicate malaria.
