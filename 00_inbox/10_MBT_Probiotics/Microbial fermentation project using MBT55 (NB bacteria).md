### Fermentation of Organic Matter and Expected Secondary Products

Fermentation of organic matter is the process by which microorganisms break down organic matter to obtain energy. During this process, microorganisms produce various secondary products.

##### Types of fermentation and generated secondary products

There are two main types of fermentation: aerobic fermentation and anaerobic fermentation.

##### Aerobic Fermentation
- Fermentation that requires oxygen.
- Generated secondary products: ethanol, organic acids, amino acids, vitamins, etc.

##### Anaerobic Fermentation
- Fermentation that does not require oxygen.
- Generated secondary products: methane, lactic acid, butanediol, acetone, etc.

##### Examples of specific organic matter and secondary products

- **Sugars**
    Ethanol, organic acids, amino acids, vitamins, etc.
- **Cellulose**
    Methane, lactic acid, butanediol, acetone, etc.
- **Proteins**
    Amino acids, ammonia, organic acids, etc.
- **Lipids**
    Fatty acids, glycerol, organic acids, etc.

##### Utilization of Secondary Products

Secondary products are used in various fields such as food, pharmaceuticals, and chemicals.

- **Food**
    Ethanol: beverages, fuel.
    Organic acids: food additives, seasonings.
    Amino acids: nutritional supplements, seasonings.
    Vitamins: health foods, pharmaceuticals.
- **Pharmaceuticals**
    Antibiotics: treatment of infections.
    Antioxidants: anti-aging, prevention of lifestyle diseases.
    Enzymes: digestive aids, therapeutic agents.
**Chemicals**
    Biofuels: fuel.
    Bioplastics: plastics.
    Biomass: energy source.

##### Development of Fermentation Technology

In recent years, fermentation technology has advanced. By using techniques such as genetic engineering and enzyme engineering, it is possible to produce microorganisms that efficiently produce desired secondary products.

##### Future Outlook

The development of fermentation technology may make it possible to produce secondary products that were not obtainable through conventional chemical synthesis. In the future, fermentation technology is expected to be further utilized in various fields.
