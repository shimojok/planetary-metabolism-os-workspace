# A Strategic Partnership for a Healthier, Nourished, and Climate-Resilient World_BioNexus Technologies for the Gates Foundation, Microsoft, and Yara

### **1.0 Executive Summary: Co-Creating the Future of Food Systems and Global Health**

We share the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation’s (BMGF) vision of “a world where every person has the opportunity to live a healthy, productive life” and are confident that we can be a decisive partner in its realization. Today’s world faces interconnected challenges—malnutrition, poverty among smallholder farmers, the adverse impacts of climate change, and preventable diseases. Traditional approaches that attempt to solve these issues in isolation have reached their limits.

At BioNexus, we provide the world’s first technological framework that integrates agricultural production and climate action (AGRIX Platform, MBT55), nutrition and health solutions (MBT Food & Herbal Probiotics), and healthcare through metabolic improvement (HealthBook) into an “ecological hypercycle.” This framework comprehensively supports BMGF’s strategic goals, accelerates global deployment through Microsoft’s AI and cloud capabilities, and delivers impact on the ground via Yara’s agricultural input network. This proposal outlines a concrete pathway for collaboration toward our shared goals of building sustainable food systems and significantly improving global health.

### **2.0 Introduction: Addressing the Interlinked Challenges of Our Time**

BMGF has achieved unparalleled results in supporting smallholder farmers in Africa, improving nutrition, combating infectious diseases such as malaria, and advancing maternal and child health. Yet several fundamental challenges remain:

- **Soil degradation**: Continued reliance on chemical fertilizers has led to a decline in soil organic matter, creating a ceiling on productivity and increasing vulnerability to climate change.
- **Nutrition quality and access**: The paradox of sufficient calories but micronutrient deficiencies (“hidden hunger”). Malnutrition in early childhood has long-term negative effects on growth and health.
- **Fragmented solutions**: Programs for agricultural productivity, nutrition improvement, and disease prevention often operate in silos, without unleashing the full potential of synergies.
    
Our proposal redefines these challenges as a continuum—**from soil health → to crop health → to human health**—and introduces a new paradigm for solving them simultaneously, grounded in data and microbial science.

### **3.0 The BioNexus Advantage: Core Technologies for Systemic Change**

#### **3.1 The AGRIX Platform: Democratizing Data-Driven Agriculture**

AGRIX provides unprecedented opportunities for all agricultural producers to leverage data, enhancing productivity and income, reducing food loss, lowering food prices, strengthening food security, transforming food systems, and promoting financial inclusion (data-driven lending).

- **AgriWare®**: Collects agricultural data and analyzes it through AI and edge computing. By leveraging phenotyping algorithms, real-time monitoring of soil and weather, and MBT Sustainable Cycle data, it enables optimal field management.
- **SafetyChain®**: Analyzes soil and crop quality, freshness retention, and related factors, contributing to food loss reduction and management of carbon footprints.
- **AgriChain® / World Foods Open Market**: Provides a distribution and trading platform that connects local production with global markets, stimulating regional economies.
- **ANE/ACAIN (Carbon Credits, ESG Investment)**: At the higher level, ANE (Agricultural Net Ecosystem) and ACAIN (Agro-Circular AI Network) interconnect, leveraging AGRIX data to build a service layer for the circular economy. This includes sustainable subscription investment models for MBT, carbon sequestration monitoring and credit issuance, and production risk assessment indices for insurance and financial sectors. Value is further expanded through API linkages with external stakeholders.
    
#### **3.2 The MBT55 Microbial Consortium & MBT Sustainable Cycle: The Engine of Regeneration**

“MBT55” is a microbial consortium of more than 120 species, composed of 55% aerobic and 45% anaerobic microbes, structured into four groups: starch-degraders, protein-degraders, lipid-degraders, and cellulose-degraders. It is a **“Hypercycle Accelerator”** that dramatically enhances natural biogeochemical cycles.

- **M3-BioEnergy System**: Unlike conventional methane fermentation, it transforms organic waste into high-quality compost and feed within 24 hours through the synergy of aerobic and anaerobic microbes, reducing waste management costs and establishing localized circular economies.
- **Formation of Black Humic Acid**: MBT55’s unique enzymatic functions (e.g., polyphenol oxidase) generate highly stable humic acids at unprecedented speed and scale. These sequester carbon in soil for long durations, enhance water retention and nutrient capacity, and support healthy crop growth.
- **Significant GHG reduction**: Methanotrophs suppress methane emissions from livestock waste, while balanced nitrifying/denitrifying bacteria reduce nitrous oxide (N₂O) release.
    
#### **3.3 MBT Food & Herbal Probiotics: Unlocking the Power of Upcycled Nutrition**

MBT55 fermentation technology fundamentally transforms the value of food by-products.

- By-products such as rice bran, okara (soy pulp), and coffee grounds are converted into “MBT Probiotics” rich in functional proteins, polyphenol metabolites, vitamins, and short-chain fatty acids.
- These are highly effective as nutritional supplements for infants and children, improving gut health and reducing risks of diarrheal and infectious diseases. They also contribute to maternal nutrition.
- Since they utilize locally available unused resources, costs remain low and sustainability is high.
    
#### **3.4 HealthBook Project: From Treatment to Prevention**

HealthBook integrates personal health data (lifestyle, body measurements, basic biometric data) and uses AI to predict health risks, functioning as a preventive healthcare platform.

- Its digitization of maternal and child health handbooks enables continuous monitoring of growth, vaccination management, and nutrition guidance, greatly enhancing maternal-child health programs.
- Analysis of anonymized regional data allows for prediction of nutritional deficiencies and infectious disease risks, supporting efficient allocation of public health resources.
    
### **4.0 Synergy with the Gates Foundation & Gates Ag One: Solving the Unsolvable**

#### **4.1 Transforming Agricultural Development in Africa: The Next Step Beyond the Green Revolution**

The climate-resilient crops developed by Gates Ag One realize their true potential only in healthy soils. MBT55 and the MBT Sustainable Cycle increase soil organic matter and restore biodiversity without relying on chemical fertilizers. This enhances crop yield and nutritional value, while improving resilience against droughts and diseases. **“BioValley”** packages these technologies into localized agricultural hubs, providing the foundation for food security and farmer livelihoods.

**Case of the Great East Japan Earthquake:**  
In farmland covered with 4–10 cm of tsunami sludge, MBT55 materials were lightly applied and hydrated. While MBT55 achieves complete organic decomposition within 24 hours in fermentation systems at 80–100°C, even in disaster-hit fields, MBT55 (formerly NB bacteria) was applied on April 29, and by May 20 (rice planting), nutrient mineralization was complete—sludge decomposition took less than a week. By June 18, MBT55-treated fields had growing rice, while untreated fields still had undecomposed sludge, showing a lack of microbial activity. Moreover, MBT55 decomposed and detoxified heavy metals and chemicals in the sludge.

#### **4.2 Revolutionizing Global Health and Nutrition: Breaking the Vicious Cycle**

Malnutrition and infectious diseases reinforce each other (malnutrition → weakened immunity → infection → impaired nutrient absorption → further malnutrition). MBT Probiotics break this vicious cycle:

- Improving gut health and nutrient absorption alleviates hidden hunger.
- Healthy microbiota strengthen immunity, preventing diarrheal diseases and pneumonia.
- HealthBook visualizes and verifies the impact of interventions with data, enabling program refinement and efficiency.
    
#### **4.3 Climate Change Mitigation and Adaptation: Measurable Impact for Smallholders**

Smallholder farmers are among the most vulnerable to climate change, yet with proper technology, they can also be crucial agents of mitigation. Farmers adopting the MBT Sustainable Cycle become practitioners of “climate-smart agriculture,” reducing greenhouse gas emissions and sequestering carbon in soils. These outcomes can be verified and monetized as carbon credits, providing farmers with new revenue streams.

### **5.0 Strategic Partnership with Microsoft: The Digital Enablement Layer**

The vast data generated by BioNexus technologies (soil, crops, microbes, health) reaches its full potential through Microsoft’s Azure cloud and AI services.

- **Azure FarmBeats** and **Microsoft Cloud for Sustainability** integrate with AGRIX to unify agricultural and environmental data management.
- **Azure AI** enables ultra-parallel metabolomic analysis, uncovering correlations between MBT55 metabolites and human health outcomes.
- **Blockchain** enhances the credibility and transparency of carbon credits generated in BioValleys, facilitating their global trading.
- **Azure**, through phenotyping methods in AGRIX/HealthBook and complete metabolic pathway analysis, becomes a platform surpassing AWS.
- Azure also supports R&D platforms for **MBT55 Food & Herbal Probiotics**, screening pathways from “bioactive compounds → functional microbes (MBT55) → metabolites → expected effects → target diseases.” This opens licensing opportunities with food companies, pharmaceutical firms, and insurers.
    
### **6.0 Strategic Partnership with Yara: Redefining “Farming Solutions”**

Yara possesses unparalleled expertise in mineral nutrition and a global distribution network. MBT55 can become the core of its **biological solutions division**.

- By combining Yara’s chemical fertilizers with MBT functional compost, we can co-develop “hybrid nutrient management” solutions that maximize fertilizer efficiency, optimize (reduce) inputs, and simultaneously improve yield and quality.
- Through acquiring the MBT Sustainable Cycle business, Yara can market fermentation-based fertilizers that surpass chemical fertilizers, capturing the regenerative agriculture market and generating immense profits.
    
### **7.0 The Proposal: A Phased Approach to Global Implementation**

- **Phase 1: Pilot Projects (Year 1–2):** Select pilot sites in regions where BMGF already operates (e.g., Kenya, Tanzania). Introduce AGRIX, BioValley, and HealthBook, and rigorously measure impacts on agricultural productivity, nutrition, and health indicators.
- **Phase 2: Regional Scale-Up (Year 3–5):** Upon successful pilots, strengthen platforms on Microsoft’s cloud infrastructure and expand across regions in partnership with Yara’s supply chains.
- **Phase 3: Global Integration (Year 5+):** Position this model as a core BMGF approach, and work with other donor nations and international institutions to establish it as a global standard.
    
### **8.0 Measurable Impact: Aligning with SDGs and Foundation Goals**

We commit to achieving the following KPIs:

- **Agriculture**: ≥30% yield increase among adopting farmers, ≥20% reduction in chemical fertilizer use, ≥0.5% annual increase in soil organic carbon.
- **Nutrition & Health**: ≥15% reduction in stunting among children under five, ≥25% reduction in diarrheal disease incidence.
- **Climate**: ≥40% reduction in farm-level GHG emissions, ≥90% recycling rate of waste.
