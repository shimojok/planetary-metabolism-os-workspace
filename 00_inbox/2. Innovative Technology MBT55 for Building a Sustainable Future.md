## 2. Innovative Technology MBT55 for Building a Sustainable Future

### A Circular Approach Utilizing the Earth System

Climate change is a response of the Earth caused by the disruption of the carbon cycle. Therefore, we must understand the **"fundamental ecological processes"** within the Earth's system.

1. **Ecological Hypercycle** – The utilization of waste products from one organism by another.
2. **Integration of Biological and Ecophysiological Processes** – In all global phenomena, biological and abiotic (non-living) processes interact so strongly that they cannot be separated.
3. **Photosynthesis**
4. **Carbon Sequestration**

These fundamental processes lead to the emergence of nutrient cycles. The integration of **Earth System Science and Ecology** is crucial for successfully addressing large-scale issues and future challenges related to global change.

The energy sources for crops come from **photosynthesis powered by sunlight** and **metabolites generated by soil microorganisms breaking down organic matter.** The **existence of soil** itself is based on its role as a **hub for microorganisms that decompose organic matter and regulate plant metabolism through the hypercycle, as well as a reservoir of essential minerals. MBT55, which meets these requirements, plays a key role in **realizing a sustainable food system.**

### Biocontrol Functions of MBT55

The **MBT55 microbial consortium** demonstrates a wide range of **biocontrol functions** due to its diverse microbial composition and unique properties. Based on these characteristics, the following key biocontrol functions of MBT55 can be categorized and outlined.

##### 1. Disease Control Functions

- **Prevention of Chalkbrood Disease in Honeybees**: Competitive exclusion and direct suppression of pathogens.
- **Prevention of Green Mold Disease in Shiitake Mushrooms**: Decomposition of green mold by specific microorganisms.
- **Prevention of Avian Influenza, Swine Fever, and Livestock Influenza**: Microbial activity that degrades pathogens and enhances immune functions. Avian influenza causes severe economic losses, and **MBT Animal Probiotics** can help prevent outbreaks, mitigating economic damage and supporting sustainable food production.
- **Plant Disease Control**: Lignin-degrading and cellulose-degrading bacteria prevent root disease infections.
- **Antibacterial Activity**: Actinomycetes and lactic acid bacteria produce antimicrobial substances that inhibit pathogen growth.

##### 2. Soil Health Improvement

- **Complete Decomposition of Organic Matter**: Starch-degrading, protein-degrading, and lipid-degrading bacteria efficiently break down organic residues in the soil, generating essential nutrients.
- **Nitrogen Fixation**: Aerobic and anaerobic nitrogen-fixing bacteria increase nitrogen availability in the soil.
- **Decomposition and Detoxification of Heavy Metals**: Manganese-reducing and iron-reducing bacteria neutralize heavy metals in the soil.
- **Sulfur Cycle Enhancement**: Sulfur bacteria and sulfate-reducing bacteria utilize hydrogen sulfide, improving sulfur circulation in the soil.
- **Regulation of Redox Balance**: Iron-oxidizing bacteria, methane-oxidizing bacteria, and sulfate-reducing bacteria adjust soil redox reactions to maintain a stable environment.

##### 3. Environmental Pollution Remediation

- **Decomposition and Composting of Marine and Sewage Sludge**: Cellulose actinomycetes and filamentous fungi break down sludge, converting it into useful compost.
- **Decomposition of Aromatic Compounds**: Aromatic compound-degrading bacteria break down persistent organic pollutants.
- **Methane Decomposition**: Methane-oxidizing bacteria contribute to greenhouse gas reduction.
- **Pesticide and Chemical Degradation**: Actinomycetes and yeast bacteria neutralize harmful chemicals.

##### 4. Animal and Human Health Promotion

- **Improvement of Gut Microbiota**: Consuming MBT55-based powder promotes beneficial gut bacteria, enhancing intestinal health.
- **Prevention of Bad Breath and Cavities**: Balances oral microbiota, reducing bad breath and the risk of cavities.
- **Digestive Health Improvement**: Decomposition of dietary fiber and organic matter alleviates constipation, diarrhea, and prevents intestinal polyps.
- **Hemorrhoid Relief**: Microbial regulation of gut health helps alleviate hemorrhoid symptoms.

##### 5. Crop Growth Promotion

- **Soil Fertility Enhancement through Composting**: Cellulose-degrading and lipid-degrading bacteria convert agricultural waste into compost, enriching soil nutrients.
- **Increased Plant Disease Resistance**: Microbial-derived antibacterial compounds strengthen plants' natural defenses against disease.
- **Production of Growth-Promoting Hormones**: Certain microbes produce plant hormones (auxins and gibberellins), enhancing crop growth.

##### 6. Ecosystem Stabilization

- **Preservation of Microbial Diversity**: The balance of aerobic and anaerobic microbes helps maintain microbial biodiversity in soil and water environments.
- **Pest Suppression**: Microbial activity suppresses the reproduction of harmful insects that affect crops.

The **MBT55 microbial consortium** offers a wide range of functions, from **disease prevention and environmental remediation to promoting animal and human health**. Its **multifunctionality** makes it an invaluable **biocontrol technology** for **sustainable agriculture and environmental conservation**.

### MBT55's contribution to the control of global greenhouse gases

MBT55 microbial groups contribute significantly to the reduction of greenhouse gases (GHGs) responsible for global warming through various functions. Below is a detailed summary of the mechanisms involved.

**1. Reduction of Methane (CH₄)**

Activity of Methanotrophic Bacteria
Methanotrophic bacteria decompose methane present in soil and water into carbon dioxide (CO₂) and water. This reduces methane emissions, which have a global warming potential 25 times higher than CO₂.

Relevant Applications:

Livestock manure management (reduction of methane emissions from livestock waste).
Reduction of methane emissions from rice paddies.

**2. Absorption and Fixation of Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)**

Composting Function of Cellulose-Decomposing Bacteria
These bacteria decompose organic waste efficiently, stabilizing carbon in the soil through composting. This enhances carbon cycling and reduces CO₂ emissions.

Contribution of Iron-Oxidizing Bacteria
Iron-oxidizing bacteria facilitate oxidation reactions in the soil, aiding in carbon fixation.

**3. Reduction of Nitrous Oxide (N₂O)**

Activity of Nitrogen-Fixing Bacteria
Both aerobic and anaerobic nitrogen-fixing bacteria stabilize nitrogen compounds, reducing N₂O emissions. This is particularly effective in agricultural fields where excessive fertilizer application leads to N₂O emissions.

Balance Regulation by Nitrifying and Sulfate-Reducing Bacteria
These microbes control the nitrification and denitrification processes, minimizing N₂O emissions from the soil.

**4. Suppression of Hydrogen Sulfide (H₂S)**

Activity of Sulfur Bacteria and Sulfate-Reducing Bacteria
Sulfur bacteria and sulfate-reducing bacteria decompose hydrogen sulfide, using it as an electron donor. This prevents H₂S from being released into the atmosphere during organic matter decomposition.

**5. Reduction of GHGs from Livestock and Agriculture**

Decomposition of Livestock Manure
Protein- and lipid-decomposing bacteria rapidly decompose livestock manure, converting it into compost and significantly reducing methane and N₂O emissions.

Reduction of GHGs in Paddy Fields
The application of MBT55 promotes methane oxidation in rice paddies, reducing GHG emissions from rice cultivation.

**6. Reduction of GHGs from Waste**

Rapid Decomposition of Organic Waste
Cellulose- and lipid-decomposing bacteria efficiently break down food waste, agricultural residues, and sewage sludge, reducing methane and CO₂ emissions from waste.

Optimization of Composting Processes
MBT55 enhances the efficiency of organic matter decomposition, suppressing the formation of anaerobic conditions that lead to GHG emissions.

**7. Effects of Long-Term Carbon Sequestration**

- Cellulose-degrading bacteria and lignin-degrading bacteria decompose plant residues, producing organic acids (such as fulvic acid and citric acid) and humus. Humus stabilizes and sequesters carbon, remaining in the soil for decades, and in some cases, over 1,000 years, significantly contributing to carbon sequestration.
- Actinomycetes break down complex organic matter and assist in humus formation.
- Fulvic acid and citric acid dissolve minerals in the soil, aiding silicate weathering and facilitating the long-term fixation of CO₂ as carbonates.
- Organic acids produced by lignin-degrading bacteria and yeast promote rock weathering, advancing processes that fix atmospheric CO₂.
- Iron-reducing bacteria and manganese-oxidizing bacteria generate metal ions, which bind to humus and organic carbon, promoting carbon stabilization.
- Biomass increase through the promotion of plant growth.
- Nitrogen-fixing bacteria and nutrient-producing bacteria enhance plant growth and increase CO₂ absorption.

**Conclusion**

MBT55, through its diverse microbial groups such as methanotrophic and nitrogen-fixing bacteria, provides multi-faceted benefits in reducing greenhouse gases. Utilizing these functions in agriculture and waste management can serve as a vital component of sustainable GHG reduction strategies.

### Production and accumulation of black humic acid by MBT55 and the role of polyphenols and charcoal

#### 1. Mechanism of Humic Acid (Black Humic Acid) Formation by MBT55

(1) Oxidation and Polymerization of Polyphenols

- MBT55 microbial groups contain polyphenol oxidase and laccase-producing bacteria, which oxidize polyphenols into quinones.
- These quinones react with organic matter in the soil, forming precursors of high-molecular-weight black humic acid.

(2) Stabilization of Carbonized Materials (Biochar and Lignin)

- MBT55 microbial groups include lignin- and cellulose-degrading bacteria, allowing them to efficiently decompose carbonized materials (such as biochar and recalcitrant organic matter) and convert them into forms that readily bind with humic substances.
- As a result, the stability of black humic acid improves, enabling its long-term accumulation in the soil.

(3) Interaction with Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Minerals

- The black humic acid produced by MBT55 fermentation binds with nitrogen and phosphorus, functioning as a slow-release fertilizer.
- This ensures that nutrient supply to the soil is sustained and efficient, creating an optimal environment for plant growth.

#### 2. Changes in Effects Due to MBT55

(1) Increased Production of Black Humic Acid

- Compared to conventional compost, MBT55 generates high concentrations of black humic acid in a short time.
- The humification process occurs rapidly, with soil improvement effects appearing within a month.

(2) Long-Term Carbon Sequestration and Soil Improvement

- The accumulation of black humic acid enhances soil carbon storage and enables long-term carbon sequestration.
- Soil aggregation structure improves, leading to better water retention, aeration, and microbial activity.

(3) Suppression of Pathogens and Promotion of Beneficial Microorganisms

- MBT55 microbial groups suppress harmful pathogens while promoting the growth of beneficial microbes, thereby improving soil health.
- As a result, the use of chemical pesticides can be reduced, lowering environmental impact.

(4) Immobilization of Soil Contaminants

- The interaction between black humic acid and polyphenols immobilizes heavy metals and pesticide residues, preventing their absorption by crops.

#### 3. Conclusion

The humic acid (black humic acid) formation process using MBT55 differs from conventional composting in the following ways:

- Accelerates polyphenol oxidation and polymerization, increasing black humic acid production.
- Controls the decomposition of carbonized materials, enhancing the stability of black humic acid.
- Fixes nitrogen and phosphorus, enabling long-term nutrient supply.
- Improves soil health while suppressing pathogens and immobilizing heavy metals.

Thus, utilizing MBT55 significantly enhances the formation and functionality of black humic acid, providing long-term soil improvement effects.
