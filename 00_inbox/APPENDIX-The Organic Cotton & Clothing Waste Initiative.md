#### APPENDIX-The Organic Cotton & Clothing Waste Initiative

The **Organic Cotton & Clothing Waste Initiative** is a circular economy model based on the following three pillars:

1. **Production and Processing of Organic Cotton**: Promoting the cultivation and processing of organic cotton in economically disadvantaged regions of Africa, providing apparel companies with affordable and high-quality materials.
2. **Carbonization and Reuse of Clothing Waste**: Converting the large amounts of clothing waste generated by the apparel industry into carbonized materials and processing them into construction boards for temporary housing in disaster-stricken areas.
3. **Establishing a Circular Model**: Closing the loop of production, consumption, and disposal to reduce environmental impact and achieve a sustainable economy.

- **Environmental Goals**: Reducing CO₂ emissions from the incineration and landfill disposal of clothing waste, contributing to Amazon’s **"Net-Zero"** targets.
- **Social Goals**: Supporting economic development and job creation in African regions while also contributing to disaster recovery through the supply of temporary housing materials.
- **Economic Goals**: Creating a new business model through the stable supply of organic cotton and the reutilization of clothing waste.

### Consideration of Greenhouse Gas Emission Reduction by the Organic Cotton & Clothing Waste Initiative

#### 1. Current Status of Apparel Waste (Apparel Loss) 
- **Annual Waste Volume**:  
  - Approximately **92 million tons** of clothing are discarded globally each year (as of 2023, per the Ellen MacArthur Foundation).  
  - About **85%** of this waste is incinerated or landfilled, with a recycling rate below 15%.  

- **Breakdown of Waste**:  
  - Unsold/returned items (excess inventory): ~30%  
  - Used clothing: ~70%  
#### 2. Calculation of Greenhouse Gas Emissions from Incineration and Landfilling 
##### Methodology:  
1. **CO₂ Emissions from Incineration**:  
   - Burning 1 ton of clothing generates ~**1.5 tons of CO₂** (varies by fiber type and chemical treatments).  
   - Polyester (petroleum-based) is a major contributor to CO₂ emissions.  

2. **Methane Emissions from Landfilling**:  
   - Natural fibers (cotton, wool) decompose in landfills, releasing methane (CH₄).  
   - Methane’s global warming potential (GWP) is **28x CO₂** over 100 years.  
   - Landfilling 1 ton of cotton generates ~**0.05 tons of CO₂-equivalent methane** (EPA estimate).  
##### Annual Emission Estimates:  
- Assumed disposal ratios: **60% incineration** (e.g., Japan/EU), **40% landfilling** (e.g., U.S./developing countries).  
  - CO₂ from incineration:  
    \( 92M tons \times 60\% \times 1.5 = 82.8M tons \, CO₂ \)  
  - Methane (CO₂e) from landfilling:  
    \( 92M tons \times 40\% \times 0.05 = 1.84M tons \, CO₂e \)  
  - **Total Emissions**:  
    \( 82.8M + 1.84M = **84.64M tons CO₂e/year** \)  
#### 3. Projected GHG Reduction from the Initiative
##### Reduction Mechanisms:  
1. **Repurposing Waste into Construction Boards**:  
   - Avoids incineration/landfilling by carbonizing waste for building materials.  
   - Producing 1 ton of boards reduces ~**1.45 tons CO₂** (vs. incineration).  

2. **Promoting Organic Cotton**:  
   - Organic cotton generates **30% less CO₂** than conventional cotton (no synthetic fertilizers).  
##### Annual Reduction Estimates:  
- **Assumptions**:  
  - 10% of apparel waste diverted to boards within 5 years (realistic initial target).  
  - Scaling organic cotton production to **1M tons** (applying CO₂ differential).  

- **Calculations**:  
  1. Reduction from boards:  
    \( 92M tons \times 10\% \times 1.45 = **13.34M tons CO₂/year** \)  
  2. Reduction from organic cotton:  
    \( 1M tons \times 0.3 = **0.3M tons CO₂/year** \)  
  - **Total Reduction**:  
    \( 13.34M + 0.3M = **13.64M tons CO₂/year** \)  
##### Reduction Rate:  
- Against current emissions (84.64M tons):  
  \( \frac{13.64}{84.64} \times 100 = **16.1\%** \)  
#### 4. Additional Impacts
- **Carbon Sequestration**:  
  - Carbon locked in boards creates a carbon-negative effect.  
- **Disaster Resilience**:  
  - Supplying boards for temporary housing reduces post-disaster emissions (e.g., emergency logistics).  
#### Conclusion  
"The Organic Cotton & Clothing Waste Initiative" could reduce apparel industry emissions by **13.64M tons CO₂/year** (over **16%** of current emissions). Amazon’s support would accelerate progress toward its **net-zero goals** while solidifying its **ESG leadership** globally.
