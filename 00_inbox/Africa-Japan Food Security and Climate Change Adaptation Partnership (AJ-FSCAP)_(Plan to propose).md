
[[☪️アフリカ-日本 食料安全保障・気候変動適応パートナーシップ（AJ-FSCAP）]]

---

### **Policy Name**  
**"Africa-Japan Food Security and Climate Change Adaptation Partnership (AJ-FSCAP)"**

---

### **Policy Objectives**  
1. **Strengthening Japan’s Food Security**: Establish stable supply routes for African agricultural products and diversify import sources.  
2. **Promoting Sustainable Agricultural Development in Africa**: Enhance local agricultural productivity through Japanese technology support and fair trade.  
3. **Climate Change Mitigation**: Promote carbon offsetting through J-Credit and expand climate-resilient agricultural practices.  
4. **Building a Win-Win Economic Relationship**: Establish a mutually beneficial trade and investment model between Japan and Africa.  

---

### **Specific Policy Measures**  

#### **1. Expanding Fair Trade with Africa**  
- **AGRIX-Based Trade Platform**:  
  - Develop a digital trading platform connecting African farmers directly with Japanese companies.  
  - Reduce intermediary costs and maximize farmer profits (30% increase compared to traditional models).  
- **"African Fair Trade Certification" System**:  
  - Certify African farmers practicing sustainable agriculture.  
  - Prioritize certified products in the Japanese market (e.g., supermarkets, school lunches).  

#### **2. Leveraging J-Credit**  
- **Issuing J-Credits to African Farmers**:  
  - Provide credits to farmers adopting climate-resilient practices (e.g., agroforestry, water-saving agriculture).  
  - Automatically measure CO2 reduction using AGRIX’s sensor network.  
- **J-Credit Purchases by Japanese Companies**:  
  - Japanese companies purchase African J-Credits to achieve ESG goals.  
  - Reinvest proceeds into African agricultural infrastructure.  

#### **3. Japanese Technology Support**  
- **Establishing the "AGRIX Africa Agricultural Innovation Center"**:  
  - Provide Japanese agricultural technologies (e.g., AI-based cultivation, drone monitoring) to local farmers.  
  - Train local technicians to promote sustainable agriculture.  
- **Technician Dispatch Program**:  
  - Send Japanese agricultural experts to Africa (1,000 experts annually).  
  - Support productivity improvement and quality control for local farmers.  

---

### **Contributions to Japan’s Food Security**  

#### **1. Risk Diversification through Import Diversification**  
- Position Africa as a new supply source, reducing dependence on specific countries.  
- Ensure stable supply of key products (e.g., coffee, cocoa, corn).  

#### **2. Stabilizing Food Prices**  
- Introduce African agricultural products to the Japanese market to mitigate price volatility risks.  
- Use AGRIX for demand-supply adjustments to stabilize the market.  

#### **3. Enhancing Domestic Agricultural Competitiveness**  
- Advance Japanese agricultural technologies through exports to Africa.  
- Apply African market experience to accelerate domestic agricultural digital transformation (DX).  

---

### **Ministry of Finance Policy Tools**  

#### **1. Financial Support**  
- **Establishing the "Africa-Japan Food Security Fund"**:  
  - Initial government contribution of ¥200 billion to attract private investment.  
  - Provide low-interest loans to Africa (annual interest rate below 1%).  
- **Subsidy Programs**:  
  - Subsidize initial investments for AGRIX-participating farmers (up to 50%).  
  - Partially cover technician dispatch costs (¥100,000 per person per month).  

#### **2. Tax Incentives**  
- **Introducing the "Africa Agricultural Investment Promotion Tax System"**:  
  - Allow 20% tax deduction for agricultural investments in Africa.  
  - Provide tax credits for J-Credit purchases (up to 10%).  

#### **3. International Coordination**  
- **Collaboration with TICAD (Tokyo International Conference on African Development)**:  
  - Propose the "Africa Agricultural DX Initiative" based on AGRIX.  
  - Promote public-private partnerships between Japan and Africa.  
- **Partnerships with International Financial Institutions**:  
  - Collaborate with the World Bank and African Development Bank (AfDB) to secure funding for agricultural infrastructure.  

---

### **Expected Outcomes**  
1. **Japan**:  
   - Increase food self-sufficiency rate (achieve 45% by 2030).  
   - Expand imports of African agricultural products to ¥100 billion annually.  
2. **Africa**:  
   - Double incomes for small-scale farmers.  
   - Improve agricultural productivity by 30%.  
3. **Global Impact**:  
   - Reduce CO2 emissions in agriculture by 50 million tons annually.  
   - Establish a win-win trade model between Japan and Africa.  

---

### **Key Features of the Policy**  
- **Balancing Sustainability and Economic Efficiency**:  
  - Combine fair trade and J-Credit to ensure economic incentives.  
- **Strengthening Public-Private Partnerships**:  
  - The government establishes the framework, while private companies lead project implementation.  
- **Establishing International Leadership**:  
  - Create new global standards based on Japanese technology and systems.  

---

This policy package not only strengthens Japan’s food security but also contributes to sustainable development and climate change mitigation in Africa. By integrating AGRIX’s digital technology with J-Credit, it creates a new model that maximizes mutual benefits for Japan and Africa.
