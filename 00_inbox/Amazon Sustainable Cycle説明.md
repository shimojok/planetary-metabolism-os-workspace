MBT Sustainable Cycle 投資モデル

1. サブスクリプションモデル

概要:
MBT発酵機とMBT55菌体をサブスクリプション方式で提供。利用料を定期的に徴収し、現地のニーズに応じたサービスを柔軟に展開。

利点:
初期導入コストを削減し、中小規模農家でも利用可能にする。長期的な収益基盤を構築。

収益源:

サブスクリプション料金

炭素クレジット収益の一部管理

機器のメンテナンスサービス費用



2. パートナーシップモデル

概要:
各国政府、世界銀行、FAO、地域のNGOと連携し、初期投資を国際機関や公共資金で負担。運用後の収益は投資元へ部分還元。

利点:
初期費用の障壁を下げ、迅速な普及を可能にする。現地政府や国際機関との協力を通じて政策的支援を得られる。

収益源:

生産性向上による収益の一部分配

廃棄物処理コスト削減分を再分配

食料生産物の売上からの収益



3. ESGファンド/Climate Financeモデル

概要:
ESG投資や気候変動対応のためのファンドから資金調達し、プロジェクトに投資。炭素クレジット収益や農業生産の成果を投資家に還元。

利点:
投資効果の透明性を示しながら、途上国の食料安全保障や環境改善に直接貢献。リスク分散も容易。

収益源:

炭素クレジット収益

堆肥・飼料の販売益

堆肥化による処理コスト削減分



4. 収益分配型農業共同体モデル

概要:
BioValleyを中心に農業共同体を設立し、地域の農業従事者や投資者と収益を分配。現地の雇用創出と所得向上を促進。

利点:
現地の経済的自立を支援しつつ、地域全体の農業生産性を向上。社会的インパクトが大きい。

収益源:

農産物の輸出益

地域市場での販売益

廃棄物処理コスト削減分の再投資

MBT Sustainable Cycle Investment Model and Business Structure Proposals

MBT Sustainable Cycle Investment Model

1. Subscription Model

Overview:
Provide MBT fermentation machines and MBT55 microbial cultures through a subscription-based service. Users pay regular fees, allowing for flexible service deployment tailored to local needs.

Advantages:
Reduces initial setup costs, making the system accessible to small-scale farmers. Establishes a long-term revenue stream.

Revenue Sources:

Subscription fees

A share of carbon credit revenue

Maintenance and support service fees



2. Partnership Model

Overview:
Collaborate with national governments, the World Bank, FAO, and local NGOs to cover initial investment costs with public or international funding. Operational profits are partially returned to the investors.

Advantages:
Lowers the barrier to initial investment, enabling faster adoption. Gains policy support through collaboration with local governments and international institutions.

Revenue Sources:

Share of increased agricultural revenues

Redistribution of savings from reduced waste management costs

Sales revenue from agricultural products



3. ESG Fund/Climate Finance Model

Overview:
Secure funding through ESG investment or climate change-focused finance, investing in the project with returns provided through carbon credits and agricultural production outcomes.

Advantages:
Demonstrates transparency in investment outcomes while directly contributing to food security and environmental improvement in developing countries. Diversifies risk.

Revenue Sources:

Carbon credit revenue

Sales of compost and feed

Savings from waste management



4. Revenue-Sharing Agricultural Community Model

Overview:
Establish agricultural communities centered around BioValley, sharing revenue among local agricultural workers and investors. Promotes employment and income growth in local areas.

Advantages:
Supports economic independence in local areas while improving agricultural productivity regionally. Delivers significant social impact.

Revenue Sources:

Export profits from agricultural products

Local market sales revenue

Reintegration of waste management cost savings into investment

Proposed Business Structure

1. Initial Deployment and Scaling Plan

Stage 1: Pilot Implementation in Model Regions
Select high-density urban areas like Nairobi for initial deployment. Demonstrate cost reductions in waste management and increased agricultural revenues.

Stage 2: Expansion to Other Regions
Use proven outcomes to scale deployment across Africa, with parallel training and education programs for local stakeholders.

Stage 3: Global Expansion
Explore technology export opportunities to emerging markets beyond Africa, such as South Asia and Latin America.


2. Strengthening Digital Integration

Integration with AGRIX Platform:
Digitize production management and distribution data, leveraging AWS cloud services for efficient data management and real-time agricultural monitoring.

Alexa Functionality:
Develop voice-assisted tools that enable producers to monitor and manage crop conditions and composting processes easily.


3. Approach to Carbon Credit Markets

Project Certification:
Obtain formal certification for carbon credits and sell them on international trading platforms, strengthening the project’s revenue base.

Regional Redistribution:
Allocate part of the carbon credit revenue to local infrastructure development and as rewards for agricultural workers.


4. International Recognition and Advocacy

Actively present the project’s impact at international conferences such as COP29 to promote its success as a model for developing country support.

Expected Outcomes

1. Strengthening Agricultural Economies in Developing Countries:

Improved food self-sufficiency, reduced reliance on imported fertilizers, and increased agricultural incomes.



2. Environmental Impact:

Waste reduction, soil restoration, and climate change mitigation through carbon sequestration.



3. Social Impact:

Job creation, nutrition improvement, and the establishment of food security.



4. Investor Returns:

Revenue from carbon credits, branding of agricultural products, and returns on ESG investments.
