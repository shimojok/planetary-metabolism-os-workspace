The functions and roles of the microorganisms contained in NB bacterial group are as follows.

- Aerobic bacteria (yeast, cellulose-degrading bacteria, nitrogen-fixing bacteria) are bacteria that decompose organic matter using oxygen and obtain energy. Yeast ferment sugars to produce alcohol and carbon dioxide, and are used for the production of food such as bread and sake. Cellulose-degrading bacteria have enzymes that decompose cellulose, and can convert cellulose-based biomass such as wood and paper into sugars and other compounds. Nitrogen-fixing bacteria reduce nitrogen in the air to ammonia, and synthesize organic nitrogen compounds. This provides nitrogen to nitrogen-deficient soils and promotes plant growth.

- Anaerobic bacteria (cellulose-degrading bacteria, nitrogen-fixing bacteria) are bacteria that decompose organic matter in the absence of oxygen and obtain energy. Cellulose-degrading bacteria can decompose cellulose as mentioned above. Under anaerobic conditions, they perform reactions such as fermentation and methane production. Nitrogen-fixing bacteria can fix nitrogen as mentioned above. Under anaerobic conditions, they perform reactions that reduce nitrate and nitrite to nitrogen gas. This regulates the nitrogen concentration of soils that have excess nitrate and nitrite.

- Lactic acid bacteria group is a general term for bacteria that ferment sugars to produce lactic acid. Lactic acid bacteria are used for the fermentation of food such as dairy products and pickles. Lactic acid bacteria also reside in the intestine and vagina, and play a role in maintaining the homeostasis of the intestinal and vaginal environments by symbiosis or antagonism with other microorganisms. Lactic acid bacteria are also expected to have health effects such as immune system regulation, allergy prevention, constipation improvement, blood pressure reduction, cholesterol reduction, etc.

- Filamentous bacteria (aromatic compound-degrading bacteria) are bacteria that have filamentous bodies. Filamentous bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in organic matter decomposition and soil formation. Some filamentous bacteria have the ability to degrade aromatic compounds. Aromatic compounds are organic compounds that are contained in fossil fuels such as petroleum and coal, and petrochemical products such as synthetic resins, and cause environmental pollution. Aromatic compound-degrading bacteria have enzymes that degrade aromatic compounds, and contribute to environmental purification.

- Actinomycetes (chitin-degrading bacteria) are bacteria that have filamentous bodies. Actinomycetes are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in organic matter decomposition and soil formation. Some actinomycetes have the ability to degrade chitin. Chitin is a polysaccharide that is contained in the exoskeleton of insects and crustaceans, and the cell wall of fungi, and is abundant in the environment. Chitin-degrading bacteria have enzymes that degrade chitin, and contribute to the carbon and nitrogen cycles.

- Manganese-reducing bacteria (black mold group - primitive basidiomycetes) are bacteria that have the ability to reduce manganese. Manganese-reducing bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the manganese cycle. Manganese-reducing bacteria reduce manganese oxide to manganese ions, and obtain energy. Some manganese-reducing bacteria belong to the black mold group, which are primitive basidiomycetes. The black mold group have filamentous bodies, and also contribute to organic matter decomposition and soil formation.

- Manganese-oxidizing bacteria (organotrophic bacteria) are bacteria that have the ability to oxidize manganese. Manganese-oxidizing bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the manganese cycle. Manganese-oxidizing bacteria oxidize manganese ions to manganese oxide, and obtain energy. Some manganese-oxidizing bacteria are called organotrophic bacteria, which are bacteria that use organic matter as a carbon source for growth.

- Ammonia-oxidizing bacteria (nitrite bacteria) are bacteria that have the ability to oxidize ammonia. Ammonia-oxidizing bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the nitrogen cycle. Ammonia-oxidizing bacteria oxidize ammonia to nitrite, and obtain energy. Ammonia-oxidizing bacteria (nitrite bacteria) are bacteria that perform the first stage of nitrification, and cooperate with nitrification-producing bacteria, which are bacteria that oxidize nitrite, to convert ammonia to nitrate. This regulates the nitrogen concentration of soil and water, and promotes plant nitrogen absorption.

- Sulfur bacteria (bacteria that use hydrogen sulfide as a hydrogen donor, green sulfur bacteria group) are bacteria that have the ability to oxidize sulfur. Sulfur bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the sulfur cycle. Sulfur bacteria oxidize hydrogen sulfide and sulfur to produce sulfuric acid and sulfates, and obtain energy. Some sulfur bacteria belong to the green sulfur bacteria group, which are bacteria that can perform photosynthesis. Green sulfur bacteria group can synthesize organic matter using light and hydrogen sulfide.

- Sulfate bacteria are bacteria that have the ability to reduce sulfate. Sulfate bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the sulfur cycle. Sulfate bacteria reduce sulfate and sulfates to produce hydrogen sulfide and sulfur, and obtain energy. Sulfate bacteria are bacteria that operate under anaerobic conditions, and perform a metabolism called sulfate respiration.

- Cellulose actinomycetes are actinomycetes that have the ability to decompose cellulose. Cellulose actinomycetes are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in organic matter decomposition and soil formation. Cellulose actinomycetes have enzymes that decompose cellulose, and can convert cellulose-based biomass such as wood and paper into sugars and other compounds.

- Iron-oxidizing bacteria are bacteria that have the ability to oxidize iron. Iron-oxidizing bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the iron cycle. Iron-oxidizing bacteria oxidize iron ions to iron oxide, and obtain energy. Iron-oxidizing bacteria are bacteria that operate under aerobic conditions, and perform a metabolism called iron respiration.

- Nitrification-producing bacteria are bacteria that have the ability to oxidize nitrite. Nitrification-producing bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the nitrogen cycle. Nitrification-producing bacteria oxidize nitrite to nitrate, and obtain energy. Nitrification-producing bacteria are bacteria that perform the second stage of nitrification, and cooperate with ammonia-oxidizing bacteria, to convert ammonia to nitrate. This regulates the nitrogen concentration of soil and water, and promotes plant nitrogen absorption.

- Cellulose filamentous bacteria are filamentous bacteria that have the ability to decompose cellulose. Cellulose filamentous bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in organic matter decomposition and soil formation. Cellulose filamentous bacteria have enzymes that decompose cellulose, and can convert cellulose-based biomass such as wood and paper into sugars and other compounds.

- Iron-reducing bacteria are bacteria that have the ability to reduce iron. Iron-reducing bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the iron cycle. Iron-reducing bacteria reduce iron oxide to iron ions, and obtain energy. Iron-reducing bacteria are bacteria that operate under anaerobic conditions, and perform a metabolism called iron respiration.

- Methane-oxidizing bacteria are bacteria that have the ability to oxidize methane. Methane-oxidizing bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in the methane cycle. Methane-oxidizing bacteria oxidize methane to carbon monoxide and carbon dioxide, and obtain energy. Methane-oxidizing bacteria are bacteria that operate under aerobic conditions, and perform a metabolism called methane respiration.

- Lignin-degrading bacteria are bacteria that have the ability to degrade lignin. Lignin is a polymer that is contained in plant cell walls such as wood and paper, and is abundant in the environment. Lignin-degrading bacteria have enzymes that degrade lignin, and can convert lignin-based biomass into aromatic compounds and other compounds. Lignin-degrading bacteria are widely distributed in soil and water, and play an important role in organic matter decomposition and soil formation.
