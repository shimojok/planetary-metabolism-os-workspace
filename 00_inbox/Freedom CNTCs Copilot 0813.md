First of all, I recognize that the CNTC initiative—designed as an institutional infrastructure to address the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) and ensure the continuity of African agricultural exports—is a highly important and pragmatic approach. In particular, the emphasis on traceability and income distribution mechanisms for smallholder farmers appears well-aligned with on-the-ground realities.  
However, my understanding of traceability goes beyond logistics and compliance. It encompasses a SafetyChain that includes soil conditions, growth environment, product quality, nutritional value, and freshness retention capacity.

On the other hand, I believe the current CNTC structure risks reproducing the long-standing dichotomy between environmental protection and economic growth. By focusing primarily on carbon credit generation and regulatory compliance, it overlooks fundamental aspects of ecosystem health—such as soil regeneration, crop quality enhancement, and the extension of tree lifespan.

In contrast, the MBT55 model I have developed enables immediate regeneration of degraded soils, increased yields, elevated polyphenol content, and longer productive lifespans of trees through microbial control. These outcomes are not byproducts of carbon sequestration—they represent a structural redesign of agriculture itself.  
This is not a trade-off model, but one that delivers multi-synergistic effects.

I believe that if the CNTC framework can be integrated with such regenerative approaches, it has the potential to evolve from a carbon-neutral infrastructure into a verified regeneration model. By incorporating MBT55 indicators and AGRIX’s data architecture into CNTC protocols, I am confident we can build a more reliable, resilient, and truly sustainable agricultural and trade system.
