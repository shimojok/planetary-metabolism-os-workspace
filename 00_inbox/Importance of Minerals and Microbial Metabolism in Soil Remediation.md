## Importance of Minerals and Microbial Metabolism in Soil Remediation

**1. Requirements for Soil Remediation**

Traditional soil remediation methods focus on basic measures such as adding organic matter, cultivating cover crops, applying soil amendments, and improving drainage. However, these methods may only address the imbalance of soil conditions and not provide a fundamental solution.

**2. Importance of Minerals and Microbial Metabolism**

Soil chemistry is influenced by the presence of trace elements and microorganisms. Microorganisms decompose organic matter and convert it into nutrients that plants can utilize. Microorganisms also play a crucial role in soil structure formation.

Minerals are essential for microbial metabolic activity and improve the physical, chemical, and biological properties of soil. Soil rich in minerals:

* Provides essential nutrients for plant growth
* Improves drainage and aeration
* Adjusts soil acidity
* Promotes microbial activity

thereby improving soil health.
