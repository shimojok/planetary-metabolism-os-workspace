It is widely recognized that the total amount of soil carbon in the world is approximately twice that of atmospheric CO2 and three times that of terrestrial vegetation biomass. Given this immense quantity of carbon stored in soils, even slight changes in soil carbon levels can significantly impact the global carbon cycle.

  

Over the years, I have engaged with more than 10 soil improvement technologies, conducting experimental verifications through agricultural production. As a result, I have discovered the following correlations: soil organic carbon (SOC) levels are directly proportional to crop yields, inversely proportional to production costs, and the required input of organic carbon can be reduced. The increase in microbial biomass itself also seems to contribute to these effects.

  

The diverse microbial group MBT55, with its ability to decompose organic matter and generate humic substances, not only enhances the carbon cycle but also facilitates the nutrient cycle, thereby promoting crop growth.

  

The potential of soil carbon and nutrient cycling is immense. For the early realization of carbon neutrality, I believe the following evaluations and recognition are essential, and I am currently working on these tasks.

  

1. Functional Demonstrations of MBT55

  

・Short-term carbon sequestration in soil

・Increase in biomass

・Generation of humic substances

・Reduction of methane gas emissions

・Contribution to carbon cycling in marine ecosystems

・Long-term carbon cycling and sequestration (enhancing silicate weathering)

  

2. MBT Sustainable Cycle

  

・MBT55 and MBT Reactor (fermentation device):

・Decomposes most organic matter within 24 hours and produces functional compost

・Targeted organic materials include processed food waste, food residues, plant residues, livestock manure, marine residues, woody biomass, sewage sludge, etc.

・Produces functional feed within 48 hours
