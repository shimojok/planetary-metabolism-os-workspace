## 1. Restoration of Farmland Inundated by the Tsunami

### Introduction

The Great East Japan Earthquake (Tohoku Region Pacific Coast Earthquake) exposed coastal farmland, including areas in Iwanuma City, to massive amounts of seabed sludge and salt damage, severely impairing vegetation and soil structure. The video material presented here documents the first case study of organic matter treatment and soil regeneration using MBT55. This case is not only an example of organic matter decomposition and salt reduction but also a broadly applicable experimental success in that it enables **prevention of salt damage, decomposition of chemical substances, and inactivation of harmful metals**. Furthermore, it offers the significant advantage of **enabling the resource recovery of sludge derived from water and sewage systems**.

[Video material: Iwamura_ Tohoku Earthquake](https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fi/tk7zmzgscxhbkyp77b3yd/Iwanuma_Tohoku-Earthquake.mp4?rlkey=dgl4bj69smxq3p9zczcwpeciq&st=c1sc1zkj&dl=0) 

**Video Introduction (0:00 – 0:30)**

- **Site Overview**: Drone footage shows coastal farmland in Iwanuma affected by tsunami-deposited seabed sediments and prolonged seawater inundation.
    
- **Problem Statement**: Narration highlights severe organic contamination and salt accumulation, leading to soil pH increase and poor germination/growth.
    
**Scientific Note**: Seabed sediments contain humic substances and salts that suppress native microbial activity and reduce cation exchange capacity (CEC). MBT55 secretes enzymes to depolymerize high-molecular-weight organics while biochemically dispersing and diluting salts. Furthermore, MBT55 enables enzymatic degradation of synthetic chemicals and inactivation of toxic heavy metals via chelation or biological neutralization.

**Mid-Section (0:30 – 2:00)**

- **MBT55 Application**: Suspension of MBT55 in a custom nutrient medium is broadcast evenly using tractor-mounted spreaders.
    
- **Tilling & Aeration**: Footage captures light tillage to alternate aerobic and anaerobic conditions, stimulating diverse microbial metabolic pathways.
    
- **Initial Fermentation**: Within 48 hours, surface bubbling indicates anaerobic breakdown of fatty acids and hydrogen sulfide, shifting soil pH toward neutral or slightly acidic levels.
    
**Scientific Note**: MBT55 produces enzymes such as cellulases, ligninases, and proteases to concurrently decompose complex organics. Fermentation-generated organic acids adjust pH to 5.5–6.5, optimizing nutrient availability for plants.

**Late Section (2:00 – 3:30)**

- **Sampling & Analysis**: On-site instruments monitor reductions in electrical conductivity (EC) and salt concentration in leachate and core samples.
    
- **Crop Trials**: Time-lapse of tomato and rice seedlings shows a 25% increase in germination rate and 30% yield improvement compared to untreated soils.
    
- **Yield & Shelf-life**: Polysaccharides and antioxidants from MBT55 culture coat crop surfaces, reducing moisture loss and microbial spoilage. Shelf-life extends by 40% over control.
    
**Scientific Note**: Chito-oligosaccharides and β-glucans induce plant immunity, while polyphenols mitigate oxidative stress, enhancing quality and reducing post-harvest loss.

**Conclusion & Outlook (3:30 – End)**

- **Long-term Carbon Sequestration**: Formed humic substances bind tightly to soil particles, creating recalcitrant organic carbon that remains in situ for decades.
    
- **Ecological Hypercycle**: MBT55’s rapid decomposition–fermentation closes the loop of produce→consume→regenerate, establishing a sustainable foundation for regenerative agriculture.
    
- **Broader Potential**: This case also demonstrates the feasibility of converting sewage sludge from urban water systems into safe, valuable resources through MBT55 bioprocesses.

## MBT55 Fermentation Video Explanation and Scientific Basis

### 1. Video Explanation

#### 【Food Residue Treatment】

[Video material: Food residue treatment](https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fi/c256f9jz7uq243sgzz7r7/Food-residue-treatment.mp4?rlkey=3qwrcf7vzuemlzce9dmgbathc&st=kbz0kydr&dl=0)

In the video "Food residue treatment," various organic materials such as citrus peels, vegetable scraps, livestock bones, and even newspaper are completely decomposed within **24 hours** using MBT55 (originally NB strain). The fermentation temperature reaches an extremely high range of **80°C to 100°C**, enabling the breakdown of materials typically resistant to composting.

#### 【Chicken Dung Processing】

[Video material: Chicken dung processing](https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fi/4k8i4f88oawx8imgyp1sq/Chicken-dung-processing.mp4?rlkey=ufum7dxde62ktifil8czz8o4c&st=v17z1qyv&dl=0)

In "Chicken dung processing," a conventional composting process that normally takes **3 to 6 months** is completed in just **24 hours** with MBT55. Post-fermentation, the pH stabilizes, and foul odors (especially ammonia) are significantly reduced. The resulting product is a **high-quality, odorless compost**.

### 2. Scientific Rationale and Significance

- **High-Temperature Aerobic Fermentation**: MBT55 consists of a unique thermophilic microbial consortium that remains active at **80–100°C**, exceeding the capabilities of mesophilic and standard thermophilic microbes.
    
- **Rapid Decomposition & Pathogen Elimination**: The system enables the complete breakdown of mixed organic matter—including bones, paper, and animal residues—within 24 hours, effectively **eliminating pathogens and parasites**.
    
- **Functional Fertilizer Output**: The final product is rich in **humic substances**, enhancing water retention, aeration, and nutrient holding capacity. Its **carbon sequestration potential** supports **climate change mitigation**.
    
- **Sustainable Fertilizer Alternative**: This MBT compost functions comparably or superior to chemical fertilizers (NPK), contributing to **resource recycling and regenerative agriculture**.
