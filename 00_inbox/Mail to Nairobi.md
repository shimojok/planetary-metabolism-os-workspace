Economic Impact of Composting Wastewater Sludge in Nairobi: A Case Study
1. Introduction

Nairobi, the capital of Kenya, has a population of approximately 4.4 million people. The wastewater treatment facilities in the city are unable to cope with the increasing population, resulting in a significant amount of untreated wastewater sludge being discharged into rivers and lakes. This poses serious environmental concerns, including water pollution and unpleasant odors.

MBT55 technology offers a viable solution to address this issue by transforming wastewater sludge into valuable compost. This approach not only eliminates the environmental hazards associated with improper sludge disposal but also generates significant economic benefits.

2. Calculation Assumptions

Population: 4.4 million
Daily wastewater generation per person: 200 liters
Sludge moisture content: 80%
Compost moisture content after MBT55 treatment: 50%
Compost selling price: 1,000 Kenyan shillings per ton
MBT treatment system installation cost: 1 billion Kenyan shillings
Annual operating days of the MBT treatment system: 365
MBT treatment system lifespan: 10 years
3. Calculation Results

3.1 Daily Wastewater Sludge Generation

4.4 million people × 200 liters/person/day = 88,000 tons

3.2 Daily Treatable Wastewater Sludge Quantity

88,000 tons × (1 - 80%) = 17,600 tons

3.3 Daily Compost Production

17,600 tons × (1 - 50%) = 8,800 tons

3.4 Daily Compost Sales Revenue

8,800 tons × 1,000 Kenyan shillings/ton = 8,800,000 Kenyan shillings

3.5 Annual Compost Sales Revenue

8,800,000 Kenyan shillings/day × 365 days = 3,204,000,000 Kenyan shillings

3.6 Total Compost Sales Revenue over 10 Years

3,204,000,000 Kenyan shillings/year × 10 years = 32,040,000,000 Kenyan shillings

3.7 Profit over 10 Years

32,040,000,000 Kenyan shillings - 1 billion Kenyan shillings = 31,040,000,000 Kenyan shillings

4. Discussion

Implementing MBT55 technology to compost all wastewater sludge in Nairobi has the potential to generate substantial economic benefits, amounting to approximately 310 billion Kenyan shillings over a decade. This figure represents about 10% of Nairobi's annual budget.

Moreover, the MBT55 treatment system contributes to environmental sustainability by reducing greenhouse gas emissions and providing valuable agricultural fertilizer in the form of composted sludge.

5. Conclusion

Composting wastewater sludge using MBT55 technology in Nairobi presents a compelling opportunity to achieve both economic and environmental gains.
