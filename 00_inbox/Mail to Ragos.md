##### Economic Impact of Composting Wastewater Sludge in Lagos: A Case Study

1. Introduction

Lagos, the most populous city in Nigeria, is home to approximately 22 million people. The city's wastewater treatment facilities are overwhelmed by the rapid population growth, resulting in a significant amount of untreated wastewater sludge being discharged into rivers and lakes. This poses severe environmental threats, including water pollution and unpleasant odors.

MBT55 technology offers a viable solution to address this issue by transforming wastewater sludge into valuable compost. This approach not only eliminates the environmental hazards associated with improper sludge disposal but also generates substantial economic benefits.

2. Calculation Assumptions

Population: 22 million
Daily wastewater generation per person: 200 liters
Sludge moisture content: 80%
Compost moisture content after MBT55 treatment: 50%
Compost selling price: 2,000 Nigerian Naira per ton
MBT treatment system installation cost: 2 billion Nigerian Naira
Annual operating days of the MBT treatment system: 365
MBT treatment system lifespan: 10 years
3. Calculation Results

3.1 Daily Wastewater Sludge Generation

22 million people × 200 liters/person/day = 440,000 tons

3.2 Daily Treatable Wastewater Sludge Quantity

440,000 tons × (1 - 80%) = 88,000 tons

3.3 Daily Compost Production

88,000 tons × (1 - 50%) = 44,000 tons

3.4 Daily Compost Sales Revenue

44,000 tons × 2,000 Nigerian Naira/ton = 88,000,000 Nigerian Naira

3.5 Annual Compost Sales Revenue

88,000,000 Nigerian Naira/day × 365 days = 32,240,000,000 Nigerian Naira

3.6 Total Compost Sales Revenue over 10 Years

32,240,000,000 Nigerian Naira/year × 10 years = 322,400,000,000 Nigerian Naira

3.7 Profit over 10 Years

322,400,000,000 Nigerian Naira - 2 billion Nigerian Naira = 302,400,000,000 Nigerian Naira

4. Discussion

Implementing MBT55 technology to compost all wastewater sludge in Lagos has the potential to generate substantial economic benefits, amounting to approximately 322.4 trillion Nigerian Naira over a decade. This figure represents about 20% of the Lagos State's annual budget.

Moreover, the MBT55 treatment system contributes to environmental sustainability by reducing greenhouse gas emissions and providing valuable agricultural fertilizer in the form of composted sludge.

5. Conclusion

Composting wastewater sludge using MBT55 technology in Lagos presents a compelling opportunity to achieve both economic and environmental gains.
