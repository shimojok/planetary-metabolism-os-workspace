### **For HealthBook**

#### 1. **Metabolic Network Diversity Score**
- **What it assesses**: The diversity of pathways for producing key metabolites like acetate, butyrate, and propionate.
- **Example Application**: "Your gut metabolic network diversity is 85/100, indicating high resilience to environmental changes."

#### 2. **Mucosal Barrier Interaction Score**
- **What it assesses**: The functionality of the microbial community involved in the chain of acetate production → mucus production → barrier function enhancement.
- **Example Application**: "The activity of your gut flora supporting the intestinal barrier is moderate. We recommend increasing your dietary fiber intake."

#### 3. **Inflammation Regulation Balance Score**
- **What it assesses**: The balance between the production of anti-inflammatory metabolites (e.g., butyrate) and pro-inflammatory metabolites.
- **Example Application**: "Activity of anti-inflammatory pathways is decreased. Increasing Omega-3 fatty acid intake may be beneficial."

---

### **For MBT Probiotics**

#### 1. **Pathway Complementarity Optimization Score**
- **What it assesses**: How well a probiotic formulation can compensate for an individual's deficient metabolic pathways.
- **Example Application**: "This product covers 78% of your deficient acetate production pathways."

#### 2. **Ecological Integration Compatibility Score**
- **What it assesses**: The likelihood that administered bacterial strains will successfully colonize and coexist with the existing gut ecosystem.
- **Example Application**: "This bacterial strain shows high affinity (92% compatibility) for your gut environment."

#### 3. **Functional Expression Probability Score**
- **What it assesses**: The predicted increase in the production of target metabolites (e.g., acetate) after consumption.
- **Example Application**: "This probiotic is predicted to increase acetate production by an estimated 35%."

---

### **For AGRIX Project**

#### 1. **Soil Metabolic Cascade Score**
- **What it assesses**: The efficiency and stability of the metabolic cascade: organic matter decomposition → nutrient generation → plant uptake.
- **Example Application**: "This soil has high organic matter decomposition capacity but challenges in nutrient retention."

#### 2. **Crop-Microbe Co-evolution Score**
- **What it assesses**: The functional match between specific crops and the soil microbiome.
- **Example Application**: "Chickpeas are microbiologically more suitable for this field than soybeans."

#### 3. **Carbon Cycle Efficiency Score**
- **What it assesses**: The efficiency of the carbon flow from photosynthetic products → rhizosphere carbon → soil organic matter.
- **Example Application**: "High carbon cycle efficiency indicates superior CO2 sequestration capacity from the atmosphere."

---

### **Cross-Project Scores**

#### 1. **Ecosystem Resilience Index**
- **What it assesses**: The functional stability of each ecosystem (gut, soil, probiotic) in maintaining its functions against environmental fluctuations.
- **Example Application**: "Both your gut environment and the soil of your food crops show high resilience."

#### 2. **Nutritional Function Connectivity Score**
- **What it assesses**: The optimal flow of nutrients and metabolites through the chain of soil → crop → gut.
- **Example Application**: "Your current diet is deficient in acetate precursors. We recommend adding the vegetables suggested by AGRIX."
