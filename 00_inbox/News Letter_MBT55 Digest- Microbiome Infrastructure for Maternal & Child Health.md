# **MBT55 Digest: Microbiome Infrastructure for Maternal & Child Health**

### **Why Microbial Metabolites Are the Missing Link in Global Health Innovation**


## **Introduction: A New Frontier for Maternal & Child Health**

Across the global health community, a clear shift is underway.  
Leading organizations and international development programs are increasingly focused on:

- **nutritional equity**, and
- **the gut microbiome as a foundation for early‑life health**

Yet most scientific and policy discussions still emphasize the _composition_ of the microbiome—  
while overlooking a far more important question:

**What metabolites do microbes produce, and how do these metabolites shape human health?**

This is where the **MBT Food & Herbal Probiotics** platform and the  
**MBT55 Microbial Consortium** introduce a fundamentally new paradigm.

## **1 — Core Insight: Humans Benefit From Microbial Metabolites, Not Microbes**

The value of the gut microbiome lies not in the microbes themselves,  
but in the **metabolites they generate**, including:

- short‑chain fatty acids
- vitamin B complex and vitamin K
- tryptophan metabolites (indoles)
- bile acid derivatives
- polyphenol metabolites
- anti‑inflammatory compounds
- vascular‑protective molecules
- immune‑modulating factors

These metabolites directly influence:

- maternal immunity
- fetal development
- infant growth
- infection resistance
- chronic inflammation
- metabolic health

However, today’s probiotic industry relies almost entirely on **single‑strain lactobacilli**,  
which can produce only a narrow range of metabolites.

## **2 — Why MBT55 Represents a Breakthrough**

MBT55 is a **multi‑species, multi‑pathway microbial consortium**  
containing over 120 selectively cultured microorganisms, including:

- cellulose‑degrading bacteria
- lignin‑degrading fungi
- sulfur bacteria
- actinomycetes
- lactobacilli
- yeasts
- aromatic‑compound degraders
- methane oxidizers
- manganese and iron oxidizers

This diversity enables MBT55 to activate **multiple metabolic pathways simultaneously**,  
making it possible to design **purpose‑specific metabolite profiles**.

## **3 — The Two Levers of Metabolite Design**

### **Lever 1 — Selecting the microbial energy source (substrate)**

Dietary fibers, polyphenols, flavonoids, minerals, and plant polysaccharides  
determine the **metabolites produced**.

### **Lever 2 — Selecting the microbial groups that generate the target metabolites**

Because MBT55 contains many functional species,  
specific metabolic pathways can be **selectively activated**.

This enables targeted designs for:

- anti‑inflammatory effects
- immune modulation
- vascular protection
- neuroactive metabolites
- metabolic improvement

A capability impossible with single‑strain probiotics.

## **4 — Why This Matters for Global Health**

Women and children in low‑resource settings face a dual burden:

- nutritional deficiencies
- high infectious disease exposure

MBT55‑based probiotics can address both:

- improved nutrient absorption
- strengthened immune function
- restored gut barrier integrity
- reduced infection risk
- improved anemia and inflammation
- reduced chronic diarrhea
- enhanced infant growth

This aligns with the priorities of many global health initiatives  
focused on **preventable mortality** and **nutritional equity**.

## **5 — HealthBook × MBT55 × AGRIX: A New Health & Nutrition Infrastructure**

This is not merely a probiotic innovation.  
It is a **data‑driven health infrastructure** integrating:

### **HealthBook Platform**

A secure system linking:

- microbiome data
- metabolic profiles
- nutritional status
- phenotyping

to enable predictive, preventive healthcare.

### **MBT Food & Herbal Probiotics**

A platform for designing targeted microbial metabolites  
through substrate selection and microbial pathway activation.

### **AGRIX Platform**

A global agricultural intelligence system connecting:

- soil health
- crop nutrition
- food quality
- supply chains
- food security

Together, these platforms form a unified ecosystem  
for health, nutrition, and sustainable development.

## **Closing Reflection**

**Are we ready to move beyond “microbes” and begin designing “microbial metabolites” as a foundation for maternal and child health?**

Upcoming issues will explore:

- metabolite screening
- microbial pathway selection
- substrate design
- implementation models for low‑resource settings
- policy and investment implications

The future of global health will be shaped not only by medicine,  
but by **microbial metabolite engineering**.

---

## **1 — 核心：人間が恩恵を受けるのは“微生物そのもの”ではなく“微生物代謝物”である**

腸内細菌の価値は、  
人間が自力では作れない代謝物を生成する点にあります。

- 短鎖脂肪酸
- ビタミンB群・K
- トリプトファン代謝物（インドール）
- 胆汁酸代謝物
- ポリフェノール代謝物
- 抗炎症性メタボライト
- 血管保護因子
- 免疫調整因子

これらは、母体の免疫、胎児の発達、乳幼児の成長に直結します。

しかし現在のプロバイオティクスは  
**単一菌種（主に乳酸菌）** に依存しており、  
生成できる代謝物は限定的です。

---

## **2 — MBT55 がブレークスルーとなる理由**

MBT55 は、120種類以上の微生物から構成される  
**多菌種・多代謝経路型の微生物コンソーシアム** です。

- セルロース分解菌
- リグニン分解菌
- 硫黄細菌
- 放線菌
- 乳酸菌群
- 酵母菌
- 芳香族化合物分解菌
- メタン酸化菌
- マンガン・鉄酸化菌

この多様性により、  
**目的別の代謝物デザイン** が可能になります。

---

## **3 — 代謝物デザインを可能にする2つのレバー**

### **レバー1：微生物のエネルギー源（基質）を選ぶ**

食物繊維、ポリフェノール、フラボノイドなどが  
**生成される代謝物を決定** します。

### **レバー2：目的代謝物を生成する微生物群を選択する**

MBT55 の多様性により、  
特定の代謝経路を持つ菌群を **選択的に活性化** できます。

---

低所得地域の女性と子供は、

- 栄養吸収の改善
