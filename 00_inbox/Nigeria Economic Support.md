
Providing economic assistance to Nigeria through Official Development Assistance (ODA) by constructing facilities for processing agricultural and marine products, and instructing in manufacturing technology, distribution, and operational know-how, is beneficial for both countries. The repayment will be covered by exporting surplus production to Japan, based on a long-term perspective and the future bilateral food security. This will enable Nigeria to secure production technology, distribution technology, and employment in various regions, and solve nutritional issues such as calcium deficiency. For Japan, it can expect a stable food supply source in the future and a relationship as a cost-effective manufacturing base. Nigeria, where many marine products should be available, can also develop new products.

### Benefits for Nigeria

1. **Improvement of Production Technology**:
   - Introduction of the latest agricultural and marine product processing technologies will increase production efficiency and enable the production of high-quality products.

2. **Enhancement of Distribution Technology**:
   - Establishment of an efficient distribution system will ensure that products reach the market quickly and safely.

3. **Creation of Employment**:
   - Construction and operation of processing facilities will create local employment opportunities and contribute to revitalizing the regional economy.

4. **Solving Nutritional Issues**:
   - Increased production of nutritious foods such as marine products can help address nutritional problems like calcium deficiency.

### Benefits for Japan

1. **Future Food Security**:
   - Importing surplus production from Nigeria will stabilize Japan's food procurement, strengthening food security.

2. **Securing a Cost-effective Manufacturing Base**:
   - Due to the relatively low production costs in Nigeria, a relationship can be established with Nigeria as a cost-effective manufacturing base.

### Implementation Methods

1. **Construction of Facilities**:
   - Utilizing ODA to construct processing facilities for agricultural and marine products in suitable regions of Nigeria.

2. **Technical Instruction**:
   - Dispatching experts from Japan to provide local technicians with manufacturing technology, distribution systems, and facility operation know-how.

3. **Long-term Trade Agreements**:
   - Establishing long-term trade agreements with Nigeria to repay ODA through the export of surplus production to Japan.

4. **Monitoring and Evaluation**:
   - Regularly monitoring the project's progress and adjusting the support content and methods as needed.

### Development of New Products

New product development can also be expected by utilizing the abundant marine products available in Nigeria. For example, developing processed foods using local seafood or new marine products for the Japanese market can create high-value-added products.

### Conclusion

This economic assistance plan contributes to Nigeria's economic development and resolution of social issues while simultaneously achieving Japan's food security and economic benefits. By cooperating and aiming for sustainable development, both countries can build a mutually beneficial relationship.
