
1. **Preparation of the Financial Budget**

    
    **A. Overall Project Overview**
    
    **Purpose:**  
    To deploy MBT Sustainable Cycle (utilizing MBT55, etc.) across various regions in Africa, thereby achieving degraded soil remediation, promotion of carbon cycling, improvement in food security through composting and feed production from food waste (which in turn reduces medical expenses), and measures against global warming (carbon sequestration).
    
    **Main Priorities:**
    
    - **Soil Remediation:**  
        Establishment of an organic decomposition/composting process through the introduction of MBT fermenters (5 million yen per unit)  
        Promotion of the regeneration of organic carbon and microorganisms in degraded soils
        
    - **Farmer and Local Technology Dissemination & Education:**  
        Technical training for farmers and local communities, and development of operational manuals
        
    - **Technology Adoption and Infrastructure Development:**  
        Construction of the AGRIX Platform (implementation of real-time monitoring, data analysis, and various integrated systems)  
        Development of BioValley (establishment of agricultural and aggregation centers, compost and feed production facilities)
        
    - **Supply Chain and Policy Coordination:**  
        Development of food distribution, storage, and transportation infrastructure, and establishment of international and multilateral cooperation frameworks
        
    
    **B. Budget Breakdown (Scenario Example)**
    
    **[Phase 1: Pilot Program (Years 1–2)]**
    
    - **Target Area:**  
        Nairobi, Kenya (population approximately 4.4–4.5 million)
        
    - **Major Investment Items:**
        
        - Installation of MBT fermenters (local funding)  
            Example: 50 units × 5 million yen = 250 million yen  
            _Note: The local government or agricultural corporations will primarily contribute the funding._
            
        - Initial development and implementation of the AGRIX Platform:  
            System development, IoT sensors, monitoring, and data analysis infrastructure = approximately 500 million to 1 billion yen
            
        - BioValley infrastructure development:  
            Compost and feed production equipment, logistics infrastructure, and establishment of coordination centers = approximately 500 million to 1 billion yen
            
        - Farmer education and training programs:  
            Dispatch of experts, establishment of training facilities, development of educational materials, etc. = approximately 200 million to 500 million yen
            
        - Operation and management costs:  
            Project operation, local support, monitoring operations = approximately 300 million to 500 million yen
            
    - **Estimated Total Investment for Phase 1:**  
        Approximately 1.5 billion to 3 billion yen  
        _Note: Based on the outcomes at this stage, performance will be evaluated and data accumulated for effectiveness._
        
    
    **[Phase 2: Expansion Phase (Years 3–5)]**
    
    - **Purpose:**  
        Based on the achievements of the pilot, expand into multiple local regions, add additional MBT units, and enhance the AGRIX Platform.
        
    - **Major Investment Items:**
        
        - Expanded deployment of MBT fermenters:  
            An additional 100 to 200 units (locally funded or co-funded) = several hundred million to over a billion yen
            
        - Enhancement of AGRIX Platform functionalities:  
            Advanced AI analysis, remote operation, and enhanced regional coordination systems = approximately 1 to 2 billion yen
            
        - Expansion of the BioValley network:  
            Development of multiple centers and expansion of logistics and storage facilities = approximately 1 to 3 billion yen
            
        - Large-scale training and technology dissemination programs:  
            Nationwide educational programs and strengthening of local partnerships = approximately 500 million to 1 billion yen
            
        - Expansion of operation and monitoring systems:  
            Establishment of a continuous support system and regular evaluation systems = approximately 500 million to 1 billion yen
            
    - **Estimated Total Investment for Phase 2:**  
        Approximately 4 to 10 billion yen
        
    
    **[Phase 3: Regional and Global Expansion (From Year 5 Onwards)]**
    
    - **Purpose:**  
        Based on the successful Nairobi model, expand across Africa and through multilateral efforts, further establishing a sustainable agricultural infrastructure with the cooperation of funds from developed nations.
        
    - **Major Investment Items:**  
        Comprehensive investment for expansion (additional investment tailored to the characteristics of each country/region)  
        Investment amounts could expand to tens of billions to hundreds of billions of yen.
        
    - **Expected Economic Impact:**  
        Simulation of the Nairobi model suggests an annual economic ripple effect of approximately 132.16 billion yen to a maximum of 330 billion yen.
        
    
    **C. Risk Mitigation Measures**
    
    - **Technical and Operational Risks:**  
        Regular monitoring and third-party evaluations, along with establishing a rapid feedback system in cooperation with local partners.
        
    - **Funding Risks:**  
        Risk diversification through decentralized finance (multiple funding sources, co-funding, and public-private partnerships).
        
    - **Policy and Environmental Risks:**  
        Strengthening coordination with local governments, international organizations, and NGOs to flexibly respond to regulatory changes and environmental shifts.
        
2. **Construction of the MBT Sustainable Cycle/BioValley Investment Platform**
    
    **A. Basic Structure of the Platform**
    
    **Purpose:**  
    To systematically manage the introduction and operation of MBT Sustainable Cycle and BioValley, integrating mechanisms for funding, technology dissemination, local operations, and performance evaluation.
    
    **Main Components:**
    
    - **Fund Management Department:**  
        Integrates funding from various governments, private companies, and institutional investors to establish a highly transparent management system.
        
    - **Technical and Operational Support Department:**  
        Centers on the AGRIX Platform, providing local technical support, monitoring, and the operation of training programs.
        
    - **Network and Partner Coordination Department:**  
        Establishes a cooperative framework with universities, research institutions, local governments, NGOs, and private companies.
        
    - **Evaluation and Reporting System:**  
        Quantitative evaluation of KPIs, environmental and economic impacts, preparation of periodic reports, and public disclosure of information.
        
    - **Digital Platform:**  
        Implementation of a real-time data collection, analysis, and decision-support system under the AGRIX Platform.
        
    
    **B. Features of the Investment Platform**
    
    - **Integrated Finance:**  
        Formation of a long-term investment cycle by utilizing various financial instruments (green bonds, convertible bonds, fund contributions, etc.).
        
    - **Open Network:**  
        Enhances the liquidity of expertise, technology, and funds through coordination with multiple domestic and international partners.
        
    - **Outcome-Based Mechanism:**  
        Constructs a funding return model based on quantitative outcomes such as soil remediation, carbon sequestration, improved food productivity, and reduced medical expenses.
