Over the past few years, I have been researching and conducting interviews on topics such as the trends at the annual COP meetings, climate change countermeasures by international organizations like the World Bank and FAO, and the state of soil degradation and food productivity.

Despite the massive investments made by institutions such as the World Bank, soil degradation has worsened, and no improvement has been seen in Africa’s food issues. It is the deterioration of these conditions that has driven me.

The factors hindering improvement include high cost structures, low economic returns, a lack of sustainability, and a shortage of innovative technologies.

For the maintenance of Earth’s ecosystems—which is essential for humanity’s prosperity—the fundamental ecological processes (such as the “ecological hypercycle” and the cycles of materials like carbon, nitrogen, and water) are critical. These processes align perfectly with the functions of MBT55.

In other words, by harnessing the diversity of soil microorganisms, humanity should be able to overcome global warming; indeed, the circulation of materials achieved through sustainable agriculture based on soil remediation (i.e. the restoration of soil organic matter and microorganisms) is a key countermeasure against global warming.

The MBT55 microbial consortium is organized into four colonies (protein-degrading bacteria, lipid-degrading bacteria, starch-degrading bacteria, and cellulose-degrading bacteria). It maintains a balance between aerobic and anaerobic organisms, enabling the decomposition and detoxification of organic matter, chemical substances, and harmful metals.

Fourteen years ago, the tsunami triggered by the Tohoku earthquake inflicted devastating damage on farmlands, which are still plagued by salt damage today. However, our development team went on-site immediately after the earthquake and applied MBT55 to the farmlands covered in marine sludge. As in previous cases, MBT55 decomposed the sludge and generated nutrients, resulting in a harvest even more abundant than usual.

For us, restoring degraded soils and enhancing productivity are not insurmountable challenges. MBT55 also proves effective in converting sludge materials into nutrient salts, neutralizing harmful substances, decomposing marine sludge, sequestering carbon in soils and oceans, reducing methane gas production, and preventing infectious diseases in livestock.

Furthermore, it helps prevent mold on shiitake mushrooms and chalkbrood disease in bees, as well as colon polyps and colorectal cancer in humans.
