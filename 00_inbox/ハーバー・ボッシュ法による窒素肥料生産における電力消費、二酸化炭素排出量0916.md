The global production of ammonia, primarily for fertilizers, releases about 1.5 to 1.6 tons of CO₂ per ton of ammonia produced, accounting for roughly 1% of global CO₂ emissions.

Most of the CO₂ emissions come from using natural gas, coal, or oil to produce hydrogen, a key input for ammonia synthesis. This accounts for more than half of the total emissions. Ammonia production is responsible for emitting over 450 million metric tons of CO₂ annually.

Efforts to reduce the carbon footprint include switching to renewable energy sources and using green hydrogen (via water electrolysis) instead of fossil fuels. Such initiatives can potentially reduce emissions by up to 75%. However, progress toward large-scale adoption of these green alternatives has been slow due to economic and technological challenges.
