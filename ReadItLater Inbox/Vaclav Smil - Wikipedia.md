**Vaclav Smil** (Czech: [\[ˈvaːtslaf ˈsmɪl\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Czech "Help:IPA/Czech");<sup id="cite_ref-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> born December 9, 1943) is a Czech-Canadian scientist and policy analyst.<sup id="cite_ref-2"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He is [Distinguished Professor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distinguished_Professor "Distinguished Professor") Emeritus<sup id="cite_ref-3"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> in the Faculty of [Environment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_science "Environmental science") at the [University of Manitoba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Manitoba "University of Manitoba") in [Winnipeg, Manitoba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winnipeg,_Manitoba "Winnipeg, Manitoba"), Canada. His interdisciplinary research interests encompass energy, environmental, food, [population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population "Population"), economic, historical and [public policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_policy "Public policy") studies. He has also applied these approaches to energy, food and [environmental affairs of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_governance_in_China "Environmental governance in China").

## Early life and education

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vaclav_Smil&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life and education")\]

Smil was born during [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II") in [Plzeň](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plze%C5%88 "Plzeň"), at that time in the German [Protectorate of Bohemia and Moravia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protectorate_of_Bohemia_and_Moravia "Protectorate of Bohemia and Moravia") (present-day [Czech Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Czech_Republic "Czech Republic")).<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> His father was a police officer and his mother a bookkeeper.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Growing up in a remote mountain town in the [Plzeň Region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plze%C5%88_Region "Plzeň Region"), Smil cut wood daily to keep the home heated. This provided an early lesson in [energy efficiency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Efficient_energy_use "Efficient energy use") and [density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_density "Energy density").<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-2"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>

Smil completed his undergraduate studies and began his graduate work (culminating in the RNDr., an intermediate graduate degree similar to the Anglo-American [Master of Philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_of_Philosophy "Master of Philosophy") credential, in 1965)<sup id="cite_ref-ProCon_5-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-ProCon-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> at the Faculty of Natural Sciences of [Charles University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_University "Charles University") in Prague, where he took up to 35 hours of lectures a week, 10 months a year, for five years.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-3"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> "They taught me nature, from geology to clouds," Smil said.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-4"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> After graduation he refused to join the [Communist party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_party "Communist party"), undermining his job prospects, though he found employment at a regional planning office.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-5"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> He married Eva, who was studying to be a physician.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-6"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1969, following the [Soviet invasion of Czechoslovakia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_invasion_of_Czechoslovakia "Soviet invasion of Czechoslovakia") and Eva's graduation, the Smils emigrated to the United States, leaving the country months before a Soviet travel ban shut the borders.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-7"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> "That was not a minor sacrifice, you know?" Smil says.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-8"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Smil then received his [Ph.D.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ph.D. "Ph.D.") in [geography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography "Geography") from the College of Earth and Mineral Sciences of [Pennsylvania State University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pennsylvania_State_University "Pennsylvania State University") in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-ProCon_5-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-ProCon-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-9"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bryce_6-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-bryce-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup>

In 1972, Smil took his first job offer at the [University of Manitoba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Manitoba "University of Manitoba") where he remained for decades, until his retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-10"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> He taught introductory environmental science courses among other subjects dealing with energy, atmospheric change, China, population and economic development.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-11"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>

Smil is skeptical that there will be a rapid transition to clean energy, believing it will take much longer than many predict.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-12"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Smil said "I have never been wrong on these major energy and environmental issues because I have nothing to sell," unlike many energy companies and politicians.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-13"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>

Smil noted in 2018 that coal, oil, and natural gas continue to make up 90% of the [primary energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_energy "Primary energy") sources used in the world. Although renewable energy technologies have improved over time, the global share of energy produced from fossil fuels since 2000 has increased.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-14"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Smil emphasizes that replacing the use of fossil carbon in the production of [primary iron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pig_iron "Pig iron"), [cement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cement "Cement"), [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia"), and [plastics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plastic "Plastic") is a significant and ongoing challenge in the industrial sector. Together, these industries account for 15% of the world's total fossil fuel consumption.<sup id="cite_ref-7"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Smil stresses the need for energy prices to reflect their true costs, including greenhouse gas emissions, and promotes a decrease in the demand for fossil fuels through [energy-saving measures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_conservation "Energy conservation").<sup id="cite_ref-8"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup>

### Position on economic growth

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vaclav_Smil&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Position on economic growth")\]

Smil believes economic growth has to end, that all growth is [logistic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logistic_growth "Logistic growth") rather than [exponential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exponential_growth "Exponential growth"), and that humans could consume much lower levels of materials and energy.<sup id="cite_ref-9"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag.org_10-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag.org-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup>

Included among Smil's admirers is Microsoft co-founder [Bill Gates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_Gates "Bill Gates"),<sup id="cite_ref-12"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> who has said: "I wait for new Smil books the way some people wait for the next [Star Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Wars "Star Wars") movie."<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-15"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> "He's a slayer of bullshit," says [David Keith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Keith_(scientist) "David Keith (scientist)"), an energy and climate scientist at [Harvard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University").<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-16"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>

His wife Eva is a physician<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-17"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and his son David is an organic synthetic chemist.

He lives in a house with unusually thick insulation, grows some of his own food, and eats meat roughly once a week.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag.org_10-1"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag.org-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> He reads 60 to 110 non-technical books a year and keeps a list of all books he has read since 1969. He "does not intend to have a cell phone ever."<sup id="cite_ref-13"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup>

Smil is known for being "intensely private", shunning the press while letting his books speak for themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-18"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> At the University of Manitoba, he only ever showed up at one faculty meeting (since the 1980s). The school accepted his reclusiveness so long as he kept teaching and publishing highly rated books.<sup id="cite_ref-sciencemag_4-19"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-sciencemag-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup>

He is a Fellow of the [Royal Society of Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Canada "Royal Society of Canada") (Science Academy)<sup id="cite_ref-14"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> and the recipient of the [American Association for the Advancement of Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Association_for_the_Advancement_of_Science "American Association for the Advancement of Science") Award for Public Understanding of Science and Technology in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-15"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2010, he was named by _[Foreign Policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_Policy "Foreign Policy")_ magazine to its list of FP Top 100 Global Thinkers.<sup id="cite_ref-fp_16-0"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-fp-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2013, he was appointed by the [Governor General](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governor_General_of_Canada "Governor General of Canada") to the [Order of Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_Canada "Order of Canada").<sup id="cite_ref-17"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> In the fall of 2013, he was the EADS Distinguished Visitor at the [American Academy in Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Academy_in_Berlin "American Academy in Berlin").

He has been an invited speaker in more than 300 conferences and workshops in the US, Canada, Europe, Asia and Africa, has lectured at many universities in [North America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America "North America"), Europe and [East Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Asia "East Asia") and has worked as a consultant for many US, [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") and [international institutions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_institution "International institution").

His book _How the World Really Works: The Science Behind How We Got Here and Where We're Going_ was a nominee for the 2022 [Balsillie Prize for Public Policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balsillie_Prize_for_Public_Policy "Balsillie Prize for Public Policy").<sup id="cite_ref-18"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaclav_Smil#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup>
