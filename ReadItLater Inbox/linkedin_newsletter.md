## The Crisis Beneath Our Feet

The 21st century faces a convergence of crises that can no longer be addressed in silos. Soil degradation, atmospheric CO₂ accumulation, food insecurity, and rising healthcare costs are not separate problems — they are symptoms of a single, systemic failure: **the disruption of Earth's metabolic cycles**.

Since the Industrial Revolution, human activity has systematically severed the natural feedback loops that sustain life. Soil Organic Carbon (SOC) is declining globally. Nitrogen runoff is poisoning watersheds. Waste treatment alone accounts for approximately **10% of global GHG emissions**.

The question is no longer *whether* these systems are broken. The question is: **what can actually reconnect them?**

## Introducing MBT55: A Microbial Approach to Planetary Regeneration

**MBT55 (Microbial BioTransformation 55)** is not a single-process carbon capture technology. It is a living, self-organizing microbial consortium — composed of **120 species operating at a 55% aerobic / 45% anaerobic ratio** — designed to mimic and restore the redox balance of healthy soil ecosystems.

Where conventional approaches (biochar, chemical fertilizers, single-strain EM bacteria) address isolated variables, MBT55 reconstructs the **entire electron flow network** underlying the carbon, nitrogen, phosphorus, and water cycles simultaneously.

The core innovation is expressed mathematically as:

> **C_f = λ · E_d / (E_d + K_m)**

Where C_f is the carbon fixation ratio and E_d is the electron donor generation efficiency — a metric unique to MBT55's NADH/NAD⁺ recycling loops and Mn/Fe reduction pathways. In simulation, MBT55 treatment increases SOC accumulation **1.8 to 2.6 times** over conventional compost, under optimal conditions of C/N ratio 20–25 and soil pH 6.5–7.2.

This is not incremental improvement. This is a category redefinition.
