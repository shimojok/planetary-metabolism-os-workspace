Nature-based solutions (NbS), can help to deliver up to 37% of solutions to achieve the Paris climate goal, as well as the potential to help address other societal challenges while being good for business and people. Yet, to scale up investment in Nature-based Solutions, business requires clarity on its scope and requirements.

This report aims to help remove the hurdles to nature-based solutions by clarifying definitions, while supporting the alignment of natural climate solutions (NCS) with nature-based solutions in order to accelerate investments at scale. In addition, the report helps business to navigate the nature and climate agenda by mapping the key initiatives, platforms and conventions for collective action.

自然ベースのソリューション（NbS）は、パリの気候目標を達成するためのソリューションの最大37％を提供するのに役立つだけでなく、ビジネスや人々に役立つ一方で、他の社会的課題に対処するのに役立つ可能性もあります。 それでも、自然ベースのソリューションへの投資を拡大するには、ビジネスはその範囲と要件を明確にする必要があります。

 このレポートは、定義を明確にすることで自然ベースのソリューションのハードルを取り除き、大規模な投資を加速するために自然気候ソリューション（NCS）と自然ベースのソリューションの整合性をサポートすることを目的としています。 さらに、このレポートは、集団行動のための主要なイニシアチブ、プラットフォーム、および慣習をマッピングすることにより、企業が自然と気候のアジェンダをナビゲートするのに役立ちます。

Below we have compiled a list of 10 questions answered that summarize the content of this business guidance report:

**1. Why should business adopt nature-based solutions for climate and nature?** 

These solutions can provide 37% of cost-effective GHG mitigation needed by 2030 to stabilize global warming to below 2°C.

1.なぜ企業は、気候と自然に対して自然ベースのソリューションを採用する必要があるのですか？**

 これらのソリューションは、地球温暖化を2°C未満に安定させるために2030年までに必要な費用効果の高いGHG緩和の37％を提供できます。

**2. What are Nature-based Solutions (NbS)?**

NbS are actions based on functioning ecosystems that deliver value to society by addressing societal challenges, and benefit nature.

2. Nature-based Solutions（NbS）とは何ですか？**

 NbSは、機能する生態系に基づいた行動であり、社会の課題に取り組み、自然に利益をもたらすことで社会に価値をもたらします。

**3. What are natural climate solutions (NCS)?**

_NCS can be considered as Nature-based Solutions for climate._ They have been defined as conservation, restoration, and/or improved land management actions to increase carbon storage and/or avoid greenhouse gas emissions across global forests, wetlands, grasslands, and agricultural lands.

3。 自然気候ソリューション（NCS）とは何ですか？

 NCSは、気候のための自然ベースのソリューションと見なすことができます。NCSは、炭素貯蔵を増やし、および/または世界の森林、湿地、草地、および農業全体で温室効果ガスの排出を回避するための保全、修復、および/または改善された土地管理アクションとして定義されています。 土地。

**4. Do all NbS fall within the definition of NCS?**

NbS are likely to have an impact on climate, yet not all projects have an explicit impact on carbon emissions (e.g. coral reef protection that aims at reducing human activity degrading coral reefs).

4.すべてのNbSはNCSの定義に含まれますか？

 NbSは気候に影響を与える可能性がありますが、すべてのプロジェクトが炭素排出量に明確な影響を与えるわけではありません（たとえば、サンゴ礁を劣化させる人間の活動を減らすことを目的としたサンゴ礁保護）。

**5. How can we align and connect NbS and NCS?**

Projects should consider NCS as NbS for Climate by fulfilling all three elements for NbS, i.e. actions based on nature that incorporate safeguards, providing benefits for nature with no net loss of biodiversity, and delivering societal value.

5.NbSとNCSをどのように調整して接続できますか？**

 プロジェクトは、NCSを気候のNbSと見なす必要があります。これは、NbSの3つの要素すべて、つまり、セーフガードを組み込んだ自然に基づく行動、生物多様性の純損失なしに自然に利益を提供し、社会的価値を提供することによって実現します。

**6. Can NbS and NCS have negative impacts on nature, biodiversity and society?**

NbS and NCS could have negative impacts when designed or implemented poorly (e.g. an afforestation project introducing non-native species can sequester carbon emissions, but can also have adverse impacts on biodiversity, or increase the risk of wildfire).

**7. How can companies ensure they are investing in high quality NbS projects?**

All NbS projects should ensure, at a minimum, no net loss for nature and biodiversity, i.e. do no net harm. Any risk of adverse effect on these two dimensions should be correctly identified and mitigated through appropriate safeguards.

7.どのようにして、企業が高品質のNBSプロジェクトに投資していることを確認できますか。 これら2つの寸法に悪影響を及ぼす危険性は、適切な保護措置を通じて正しく識別され軽減されるべきである。

**8.***What kind of actions/projects are considered high quality NbS?**

Ideally, any solution (whether NbS or NCS) should be positive for nature, climate and society, but at minimum good for one of these societal challenges, and not negative for the others.

***高品質のNBSと見なされるのはどのような行動/プロジェクトが考えられていますか？**理想的には、どんな解決策（NBSまたはNCSかどうか）は、自然、気候と社会のために前向きであるべきですが、これらの社会的課題の1つに最低であり、他のものには否定的ではありません。

**9. What steps can a company take to achieve a nature positive future?**

-   (1) Assess business relation with nature (dependencies, risks and opportunities)
-   (2) Contribute to the development of science-based targets for nature
-   (3) Engage in policy discussions e.g. CBD Post-2020 Global Biodiversity Framework
-   (4) Invest in scalable high-quality NbS projects

9.ネイチャーポジティブな未来を実現するために、企業はどのようなステップを踏むことができますか？**

 -（1）自然とのビジネス関係（依存関係、リスク、機会）を評価する
 -（2）自然に対する科学に基づく目標の開発に貢献する
 -（3）ポリシーの議論に参加します。  CBDポスト2020グローバル生物多様性フレームワーク
 -（4）スケーラブルで高品質なNbSプロジェクトに投資する

**10. What role does nature play in the climate agenda?**

The twin crises of nature loss and climate change are inextricably linked. We will not achieve the Paris climate goals without Nature-based Solutions in the Food, land and oceans systems. Climate change has been identified (by IPBES) as one of the leading drivers of biodiversity loss. Addressing the climate emergency and nature loss need to be key parts of the future strategy to build forward better following the COVID-19 pandemic.

10.気候アジェンダにおいて、自然はどのような役割を果たしていますか？**

 自然喪失と気候変動の2つの危機は密接に関連しています。 食料、陸、海のシステムにおける自然ベースのソリューションなしでは、パリの気候目標を達成することはできません。 気候変動は（IPBESによって）生物多様性喪失の主要な推進力の1つとして特定されています。 気候の緊急事態と自然の喪失に対処することは、COVID-19のパンデミックに続いてより良く前進するための将来の戦略の重要な部分である必要があります。

### Accelerating business solutions for Climate and Nature Report I: Mapping nature-based solutions and natural climate solutions

#### Foreword

The COVID-19 pandemic and connected economic challenges have shown governments and business the necessity to transform to net-zero before 2050 and become nature-positive by 2030. Driving unemployment, malnutrition and extreme poverty to soar in many countries around the world, the pandemic has provided a stark warning of system risks, vulnerabilities and inequalities – and what’s at stake if we can’t mobilize radical action to reset systems for a carbonneutral, nature-positive world.
Business has a leading role to play in forging a clear, actionable and sciencebased path to resetting our systems. As we navigate our recovery from the pandemic, it’s becoming evident that returning to business as usual is not an option for people or planet. Businesses and governments need to harness a green recovery that builds long-lasting resilience to the largest, most interlinked threats we face: nature loss and climate change. We need radical, collective climate action that slashes greenhouse gas emissions and supports the growth of natural ecosystems; and we need to harness the potential of natural ecosystems, which offer a third of the emissions reductions needed to secure a net-zero, resilient world.

COVID-19のパンデミックと関連する経済的課題は、政府と企業が2050年までにネットゼロに転換し、2030年までに自然陽性になる必要性を示しています。 は、システムのリスク、脆弱性、不平等についての厳しい警告を提供しました。そして、カーボンニュートラルで自然にポジティブな世界のためにシステムをリセットするための急進的な行動を動員できない場合、何が問題になりますか。
ビジネスは、システムをリセットするための明確で実用的で科学に基づいた道を築く上で主導的な役割を果たします。 パンデミックからの回復を進めるにつれ、通常どおりビジネスに復帰することは、人や地球にとって選択肢ではないことが明らかになりつつあります。 企業や政府は、私たちが直面する最大の、最も相互に関連する脅威である自然の喪失や気候変動に対して、長期的な回復力を構築するグリーンリカバリーを活用する必要があります。 温室効果ガスの排出を削減し、天然ガスの成長をサポートする、急進的で集団的な気候変動対策が必要です。 そして、自然の生態系の可能性を活用する必要があります。これは、ネットゼロで回復力のある世界を確保するために必要な排出削減の3分の1を提供します。

Despite the evidence linking the climate and nature crises, up until now humanity has mostly addressed nature loss and climate change as distinct and unrelated challenges, with no coordinated plan to address them together.
To mobilize stronger collective action against the world’s most urgent challenges, WBCSD has raised its membership criteria – putting action to combat the climate emergency, reverse nature loss and fight inequality at the center of what it means to be a business leader.
To help business fulfil our role in achieving this ambition, we are working with technical partners and leading businesses across the climate and nature agendas to clarify the business role in taking science-based, collective action to drive a netzero, nature-positive economy. Our members are showing leadership by bridging the climate and nature agendas, applying lessons from the climate agenda to nature action. Business mobilization to bring a unified voice to the Climate COP (Conference of the Parties to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change – UNFCCC) process brought policy-makers the necessary economic confidence to adopt an ambitious agreement – the target of limiting the global temperature increase to 1.5°C – and the Task Force on Climaterelated Financial Disclosures (TCFD) has ensured alignment and transparency in reporting. This collective business leadership is also driving the nature agenda. Emulating this process for nature will be key ahead of the Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD) COP15 in 2021.
We would like to thank all our member companies and partners for their contributions to this report, a joint effort between the Nature Action and Natural Climate Solutions teams at WBCSD.

These reports will guide business leaders and multi-stakeholder partners to take collective action on the inextricably linked climate and nature crises, supporting both human and planetary health. The series will span three reports that focus on the following priorities:

WBCSDのFood＆Nature ProgramとClimate＆Energy Programは、一連のレポートを共同で開発しています。ClimateandNatureレポートのビジネスソリューションの加速。 これらのレポートは、ビジネスリーダーと複数の利害関係者のパートナーが、密接に関連する気候と自然の危機に対して集団行動を起こし、人間と惑星の両方の健康をサポートするように導きます。 このシリーズは、次の優先事項に焦点を当てた3つのレポートにまたがります。

The purpose of this first report in the series is to lay out clear definitions and clarify the scope for nature-based solutions (NbS) and natural climate solutions (NCS) – to accelerate consistent and credible actions that are “good enough” and of high quality – and to map the leading initiatives in this crowded, rapidly evolving nature space.

To unlock investments and ensure that credible, highquality projects receive funding, there must be a common understanding of what defines a nature-based solution and a natural climate solution, and how they are related. We have found that while many businesses are keen to accelerate their own participation in the developing investment class of natural climate solutions (NCS), there is still confusion and overlap over the use of the terms NCS and nature-based solutions (NbS). With a common framework, it is necessary to accelerate investments into high-quality nature-based solutions that deliver both climate solutions and nature benefits.

このシリーズの最初のレポートの目的は、明確な定義を提示し、自然ベースのソリューション（NbS）と自然気候ソリューション（NCS）の範囲を明確にすることです。 品質–そして、この混雑した、急速に進化する自然空間における主要なイニシアチブをマッピングします。

 投資のロックを解除し、信頼できる高品質のプロジェクトが資金を確実に受け取れるようにするには、自然ベースのソリューションと自然気候ソリューションを定義するものと、それらがどのように関連しているかについての共通の理解が必要です。 多くの企業が開発中の投資クラスの自然気候ソリューション（NCS）への参加を加速することに熱心ですが、NCSと自然ベースのソリューション（NbS）という用語の使用には依然として混乱と重複があることがわかりました。 共通の枠組みで、気候ソリューションと自然の利益の両方を提供する高品質の自然ベースのソリューションへの投資を加速する必要があります。

It is key to understand that while many nature-based solutions are likely to impact climate, not all actions have an explicit impact on carbon emissions – therefore not all of these solutions fall within the definition of natural climate solutions. On the other hand, NCS projects which are designed to deliver a reduction in carbon emissions through the power of biological sequestration can also deliver co-benefits and can help combat other societal challenges in addition to climate change.

However, when businesses design or implement NbS and NCS poorly, they can have negative impacts on nature, biodiversity and society.
The private sector plays a crucial role in adopting and scaling up these solutions. Business can address the interconnected crises of climate and nature together when they make the connection between NbS and NCS and consider NCS as nature-based solutions for climate. This is applicable if an NCS project fulfills all three elements for nature-based solutions, meaning actions that provide benefits for nature with no net loss of biodiversity, as well as delivering societal value and being based on nature. This is achievable by incorporating appropriate safeguards.
We strongly recommend that WBCSD members invest in high-quality NbS and NCS solutions that are net-positive for nature and biodiversity, and also support people and local communities. Developing and delivering high-quality NbS and NCS supports achievement of climate, nature and biodiversity goals, supporting people and local communities and provide opportunities for business to invest in positive asset class investments.

##### Nature-based solutions (NbS)

Nature-based solutions are actions based on functioning biological ecosystems, such as forests, grasslands, agricultural systems, rivers, lakes and oceans. Several leading institutions have developed their own definitions of NbS and associated interventions.
The European Commission defines NbS as “solutions that are inspired and supported by nature, which are costeffective, simultaneously provide environmental, social and economic benefits and help build resilience. Such solutions bring more, and more diverse, nature and natural features and processes into cities, landscapes and seascapes, through locally adapted, resource-efficient and systemic interventions.”

自然ベースのソリューションは、森林、草地、農業システム、河川、湖、海などの機能する生物学的生態系に基づく行動です。 いくつかの主要な機関は、NbSおよび関連する介入の独自の定義を開発しました。
 欧州委員会は、NbSを「自然に触発され、サポートされ、費用対効果が高く、同時に環境的、社会的、経済的利益を提供し、回復力の構築を支援するソリューション」と定義しています。 このようなソリューションは、地域に適応した、資源効率の高い体系的な介入を通じて、ますます多様化する自然と自然の特徴とプロセスを都市、景観、海景にもたらします。」

The Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) proposes that “NbS are measures that protect, sustainably manage or restore nature, with the goal of maintaining or enhancing ecosystem services to address a variety of social, environmental and economic challenges.”
In order to provide a common simple, workable, and sciencebased definition, the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) launched a two-year consultation process building on a number of existing models and approaches. More than 1,000 individuals participated, of which one-sixth represented the private sector, including WBCSD and the World Economic Forum. The resulting NbS definition, endorsed by WBCSD, is:

経済協力開発機構（OECD）は、「NbSは、さまざまな社会的、環境的、経済的課題に対処するための生態系サービスを維持または強化することを目的として、自然を保護、持続的に管理、または回復する手段です」と提案しています。
 共通のシンプルで実行可能な科学に基づく定義を提供するために、国際自然保護連合（IUCN）は、いくつかの既存のモデルとアプローチに基づいて2年間の協議プロセスを開始しました。  1,000人以上が参加し、そのうち6分の1がWBCSDや世界経済フォーラムなどの民間セクターを代表していました。  WBCSDによって承認された結果のNbS定義は、次のとおりです。

**“Actions to protect, sustainably manage and restore natural or modified ecosystems, that address societal challenges14 (e.g., climate change, food and water security or natural disasters) effectively and adaptively, simultaneously providing human well-being and biodiversity benefits.”**

「自然または改変された生態系を保護、持続的に管理、回復するための行動。社会的課題14（気候変動、食糧と水の安全保障、自然災害など）に効果的かつ適応的に対処し、同時に人間の幸福と生物多様性の利益をもたらします。」

All definitions share three common elements:
1.   The actions are based on nature – involving protection, sustainable management or restoration of natural resources.
2.   They deliver value to society by addressing societal challenges (e.g., biodiversity loss, climate change).
3.   They benefit nature by preserving and enhancing biodiversity and ecosystem services.

The IUCN Global Standard for NbS also clearly states that NbS simultaneously provide human well-being and biodiversity benefits.

すべての定義は、次の3つの共通要素を共有しています。
 1.行動は自然に基づいており、保護、持続可能な管理、または天然資源の回復が含まれます。
 2.社会的課題（生物多様性の喪失、気候変動など）に取り組むことで、社会に価値をもたらします。
 3.それらは、生物多様性と生態系サービスを維持および強化することによって自然に利益をもたらします。

 NbSのIUCNグローバルスタンダードはまた、NbSが人間の幸福と生物多様性の利益を同時に提供することを明確に述べています。

**Hence, WBCSD encourages its members to invest in NbS that ensure, at a minimum, no net loss of nature and biodiversity, as well as no net harm for people. Business should correctly identify and mitigate any risk of adverse effects on these two dimensions through appropriate safeguards. Highquality NbS projects and investments therefore provide credible human well-being and biodiversity benefits.**
したがって、WBCSDは、メンバーがNbSに投資することを奨励しています。これにより、少なくとも自然と生物多様性の正味の損失がなく、人々に正味の害がないことが保証されます。 ビジネスは、適切な保護手段を通じて、これら2つの側面への悪影響のリスクを正しく特定して軽減する必要があります。 したがって、高品質のNbSプロジェクトと投資は、信頼できる人間の幸福と生物多様性の利益をもたらします。

It is also often important to understand what a definition specifically excludes. To define the scope of nature-based solutions, IUCN has identified the following categories, which are excluded from the scope of NbS:
Nature-derived: Wind, wave and solar energy come from nature. In this case, they are solutions to help fulfil our low-carbon energy needs through production methods derived from natural sources. These energy sources come from the natural world but are not directly based on functioning ecosystems.
Nature-inspired: Nature inspires the innovative design and production of materials, structures, and systems modelled on biological processes. For example, biomimicry is a practice that learns from and mimics the strategies found in nature to solve challenges.
These designs take inspiration from nature – such as specially made sticky gloves that mimic the wall climbing adaptations of geckos. However hey are not based on functioning ecosystems.

定義が具体的に何を除外するかを理解することもしばしば重要です。 自然ベースのソリューションの範囲を定義するために、IUCNは、NbSの範囲から除外される次のカテゴリを特定しました。
自然由来：風、波、太陽エネルギーは自然から来ています。 この場合、それらは、天然資源から得られた生産方法を通じて、私たちの低炭素エネルギーのニーズを満たすのに役立つソリューションです。 これらのエネルギー源は自然界から来ていますが、機能している生態系に直接基づいているわけではありません。
自然に触発された：自然は、生物学的プロセスに基づいてモデル化された材料、構造、およびシステムの革新的な設計と生産に影響を与えます。 たとえば、生体模倣は、課題を解決するために自然界に見られる戦略から学び、模倣する実践です。
これらのデザインは、ヤモリのウォールクライミングの適応を模倣した特別に作られた粘着性のある手袋など、自然からインスピレーションを得ています。 しかし、ちょっと機能している生態系に基づいていません。

**Natural climate solutions (NCS)**

The article “Natural climate solutions” published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences(PNAS) in 2017 and led by The Nature Conservancy and 15 other institutions, defines NCS as: “Conservation, restoration, and/ or improved land management actions to increase carbon storage and/or avoid greenhouse gas emissions across global forests, wetlands, grasslands, and agricultural lands.”
Therefore, NCS fall under the umbrella of nature-based solutions but focus explicitly on addressing climate change. Given the focus on conservation, restoration and improved land management, natural climate solutions are likely to have adaptation as well as mitigation benefits.
This definition is based on a limited range of well-researched, proven types of NCS and therefore does not include other examples, such as in urban or marine environments. It may therefore evolve to also include additional emerging types of NCS.
The definition was part of an academic paper, rather than for practical guidance, and as such included no explicit requirement for NCS to benefit nature or biodiversity. However, given the focus on conservation, restoration and improved land management, nature and biodiversity benefits are likely to occur together with co-benefits impacting other societal challenges. High-quality NCS projects and investments therefore provide credible co-benefits for nature and communities, including through conservation, restoration and improved land management.

2017年にNationalAcademy of Sciences（PNAS）の議事録に掲載され、The Nature Conservancyと他の15の機関が主導する記事「自然気候ソリューション」は、NCSを次のように定義しています。世界の森林、湿地、草地、農地全体で炭素貯蔵量を増やしたり、温室効果ガスの排出を回避したりします。」
したがって、NCSは自然ベースのソリューションの傘下にありますが、気候変動への対処に明確に焦点を合わせています。保全、修復、土地管理の改善に重点が置かれていることを考えると、自然気候ソリューションには、適応と緩和のメリットがある可能性があります。
この定義は、限られた範囲の十分に研究され、証明されたタイプのNCSに基づいているため、都市環境や海洋環境などの他の例は含まれていません。したがって、追加の新しいタイプのNCSも含まれるように進化する可能性があります。
この定義は、実践的なガイダンスではなく、学術論文の一部であり、そのため、NCSが自然や生物多様性に利益をもたらすという明確な要件は含まれていませんでした。しかし、保全、修復、土地管理の改善に重点が置かれていることを考えると、自然と生物多様性の利益は、他の社会的課題に影響を与える共同利益とともに発生する可能性があります。したがって、質の高いNCSプロジェクトと投資は、保全、修復、土地管理の改善などを通じて、自然とコミュニティに信頼できる共同利益をもたらします。

**Alignment between NbS and NCS:**

•   While many NbS are likely to have an impact on climate, either directly or indirectly, not all of them have an explicit impact on carbon emissions (e.g., coral reef protection that aims to reduce human activities that degrade coral reefs, groundwater recharging, sustainable fishing). As a result, not all NbS fall within the definition of NCS.

•   NCS typically impact societal challenges other than climate change – these impacts beyond primary NCS goals are “co-benefits”. For instance, a mangrove conservation project would primarily address climate change, disaster risk prevention, environmental degradation and biodiversity loss, but may also have co-benefits for other dimensions, such as food security (by providing shelter to local fish species) and economic and social development (by supporting sustainable fisheries and/or ecotourism).

•   Certain NbS and NCS, when poorly designed or implemented, could have negative impacts on nature, biodiversity and society. For example, an afforestation project using monoculture or introducing exotic/non-native species can help sequester carbon emissions, but can also have adverse impacts on biodiversity, or increase the risk of natural disasters such as wildfire.

•多くのNbSは、直接的または間接的に気候に影響を与える可能性がありますが、すべてが炭素排出に明示的な影響を与えるわけではありません（たとえば、サンゴ礁を劣化させる人間の活動を減らすことを目的としたサンゴ礁保護、地下水再充電、持続可能な釣り）。その結果、すべてのNbSがNCSの定義に含まれるわけではありません。

•NCSは通常、気候変動以外の社会的課題に影響を与えます。これらの影響は、NCSの主要な目標を超えて「コベネフィット」です。たとえば、マングローブ保全プロジェクトは、主に気候変動、災害リスク防止、環境劣化、生物多様性の喪失に対処しますが、食料安全保障（地元の魚種に避難所を提供することによる）や経済的および経済的社会開発（持続可能な漁業および/またはエコツーリズムを支援することによる）。

•特定のNbSおよびNCSは、設計または実装が不十分な場合、自然、生物多様性、および社会に悪影響を与える可能性があります。たとえば、単一栽培を使用したり、外来種や外来種を導入したりする植林プロジェクトは、炭素排出を隔離するのに役立ちますが、生物多様性に悪影響を及ぼしたり、野火などの自然災害のリスクを高めたりする可能性もあります。

**Ideally, any solution (whether NbS or NCS) should be positive for nature, climate and society, but at a minimum good for one of these societal challenges and not negative for the others.**
理想的には、どの解決策（NbSまたはNCS）も、自然、気候、社会にプラスである必要がありますが、少なくともこれらの社会的課題の1つには有効であり、他の課題にはマイナスではありません。

•   One way to align and connect NbS and NCS is to consider NCS as naturebased solutions for climate. This would be applicable if NCS projects fulfilled all three elements for nature-based solutions, i.e., they provide benefits for nature with no net loss of biodiversity, as well as delivering societal value and being based on nature. It is possible to achieve this by incorporating the appropriate safeguards.

•NbSとNCSを連携させて接続する1つの方法は、NCSを気候の自然ベースのソリューションと見なすことです。 これは、NCSプロジェクトが自然ベースのソリューションの3つの要素すべてを満たしている場合、つまり、生物多様性の純損失なしに自然に利益をもたらし、社会的価値を提供し、自然に基づいている場合に適用されます。 適切な保護手段を組み込むことにより、これを達成することが可能です。

***Summary**
Business has a critical role to play in accelerating projects and investments that support climate, nature and people. While the risks of inaction are significant and well documented, the benefits to both business security and growth are very compelling.
To unlock investment and ensure credible, high-quality projects receive funding, there must be a common understanding of what defines a nature-based solution and a natural climate solution. Nature-based solutions (NbS) are actions based on functioning ecosystems that deliver value to society by addressing societal challenges and benefit nature by enhancing biodiversity and ecosystem services.
Natural climate solutions (NCS) are nature-based solutions for climate – meaning solutions specifically aimed at delivering climate benefits.
At a minimum, both naturebased solutions and natural climate solutions should ensure zero net loss for biodiversity. We strongly recommend that WBCSD members invest in high-quality NbS and NCS solutions that are net-positive for nature and biodiversity and that also support people and local communities.
Regulatory and investment criteria that promote or reward high-quality NbS and NCS over lower quality single benefit options are also necessary. Developing and delivering high-quality NbS and NCS will help drive a race to the top and support the achieving of nature and biodiversity goals. It will also provide opportunities for business to invest in positive asset class investments.*

****Targets for nature

Apex goals. As we work to develop frameworks, targets and tools to address nature loss, it is possible to learn from parallel experience in the climate change debate. One key asset in dealing with climate change has been alignment on an overarching “apex goal” of limiting the global temperature rise to 1.5°C. So far, the nature agenda has lacked an equivalent to the 1.5°C goal but several NGO and business organizations have recently proposed an apex goal of zero net loss from 2020, nature-positive by 2030 and full recovery by 2050 to provide the level of ambition needed to preserve biodiversity, limit land conversion and safeguard oceans.
Science-based targets. In the climate space, there has been an increase in companies announcing climate commitments and establishing ambitious targets in line with climate science across sectors. The Science Based Targets Initiative (SBTi), a joint initiative by the Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP), the UN Global Compact (UNGC), the World Resources Institute (WRI) and the World Wildlife Fund (WWF) has supported this progress with the intention of increasing corporate climate action ambitions by mobilizing companies to set greenhouse gas emissions reduction targets consistent with the goals of the Paris Agreement – to limit global warming to wellbelow 2°C above pre-industrial levels and pursue efforts to limit warming to 1.5°C.

Apexの目標。 自然の喪失に対処するためのフレームワーク、ターゲット、ツールの開発に取り組んでいるため、気候変動の議論における並行した経験から学ぶことができます。 気候変動に対処する上での重要な資産の1つは、地球の気温上昇を1.5°Cに制限するという包括的な「頂点目標」の調整です。 これまでのところ、自然の課題には1.5°Cの目標に相当するものがありませんでしたが、いくつかのNGOや企業組織は最近、2020年から純損失をゼロにし、2030年までに自然をポジティブにし、2050年までに完全に回復するという頂点の目標を提案しました。 生物多様性を保護し、土地の転換を制限し、海洋を保護するために必要な野心。
 科学ベースのターゲット。 気候分野では、気候変動への取り組みを発表し、セクター全体の気候科学に沿った野心的な目標を設定する企業が増えています。 カーボンディスクロージャープロジェクト（CDP）、国連グローバルコンパクト（UNGC）、世界資源研究所（WRI）、世界野生生物基金（WWF）による共同イニシアチブであるScience Based Targets Initiative（SBTi）は、 パリ協定の目標と一致する温室効果ガス排出削減目標を設定するために企業を動員することにより、企業の気候変動対策の野心を高める意図–地球温暖化を産業革命前のレベルより2°C未満に制限し、温暖化を1.5°Cに制限する取り組みを追求する 。

Similarly, the Science Based Targets Network (SBTN) is expanding this exercise to nature (including, on top of climate: land, water, biodiversity and oceans) and for cities in addition to companies. In many cases, companies are aware of their impact and dependencies on nature and the actions required; but as with SBTi, the targets for nature aim to determine “how much is enough” on nature action to reverse nature loss.

同様に、Science Based Targets Network（SBTN）は、この演習を自然（気候に加えて、土地、水、生物多様性、海洋を含む）や、企業だけでなく都市にも拡大しています。 この目的のために、SBTNは、コミットメントを推進し、自然に対する科学に基づく目標のテストを開始したい企業をサポートするための暫定ガイダンスを開発しました。 多くの場合、企業は自然への影響と依存関係、および必要なアクションを認識しています。 しかし、SBTiと同様に、自然の目標は、自然の喪失を逆転させるための自然の行動について「どれだけ十分か」を判断することを目的としています。

We are working with business, NGOs and policy-makers to provide a clear and actionable path to being nature-positive by 2030 and climate neutral by 2050. We do so by creating and hosting coalitions and projects specifically aimed at: •   Promoting policy recommendations by bringing a collective business voice to the table.
•   Developing guidance and contributing to sciencebased targets and frameworks.
•   Developing and aligning nature-based business solutions for collective action.
•   Mobilizing business leadership to push business action forward.
•   Creating awareness and enabling knowledge-sharing across sectors.
Because it is not possible to achieve climate commitments if companies do not address nature loss, we are also continuously encouraging our member companies to take further action. This is part of our new membership criteria, which now also asks companies to include nature in their commitments, in addition to climate, and:
“Set ambitious, scienceinformed, short- and midterm environmental goals that contribute to nature/ biodiversity recovery by 2050 and report progress annually in standard, external company communications.”
The nature and climate agendas are cross-cutting; for that reason, we are bringing together our members across sectors to advance the establishing of targets and to scale up the implementation of NbS and NCS via three key projects:
Nature Action
This project aims to establish a group of leading companies across sectors applying sciencebased targets for nature and NbS that have a demonstrable positive impact on nature and biodiversity in line with the proposed apex goal for nature.
This cross-cutting project brings together businesses from the three key systems required for transformation and builds on the key transitions identified by the New Nature Economy Report Series published by the World Economic Forum:
•   Food, land and ocean use •   Infrastructure and built environment
•   Energy and extractives 

Natural Climate Solutions
Research shows that these solutions could deliver 37% of the emissions reductions needed to limit global warming to 2°C. However, despite their massive potential, they only make up 1% of the climate conversation. We’re changing that by boosting awareness across the business community and helping the private sector invest in nature. #### 5 Key takeaways

Overcoming obstacles to financing nature-based solutions
It is estimated that major ecosystem conservation and restoration work could contribute up to 37% of the solutions needed by 2030 to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and stabilize global warming to below 2°C. However, the amount of public finance spent on NbS for climate, as a percentage of all climate finance, was only 3% in 2015-2016 and only 8% in 20172018, leading to claims that NbS is a forgotten solution. This NbS funding that has occurred to date has mainly been for climate, rather than the broader benefits such as biodiversity that NbS can deliver.
Nature protection and restoration have suffered from a chronic lack of financial resources: estimates show that there is currently an annual funding gap for nature of USD $711 billion between now and 2030 (less than 1% of global GDP).17
The current slow growth in NbS investment will not unlock the full potential of nature-based solutions. To attract more capital to NbS projects, investors need to better understand and internalize the material risks and opportunities associated with nature. We are contributing to the development of tools led by the Task Force on Nature-related Financial Disclosures that will help make these risks visible and make it possible to measure and report.

Business is stepping up its climate & nature ambitions

NbS and NCS are affordable, scalable and available right now. Business plays a key role in adopting and integrating these solutions into their core activities as a way to do good for the planet, as well as people and prosperity. Many WBCSD members have announced ambitious actions, using sciencebased targets, to contribute to global climate, biodiversity and development goals: •   Business for Nature has identified over 1,200 examples of companies taking action for nature, including many WBCSD members.
•   Walmart recently pledged to achieve zero emissions by 2040 without carbon offsets, while also committing to protect and restore at least 50 million acres of land and 1 million square miles of ocean by 2030.
•   Microsoft pledged to offset all its historical emissions and become net-negative by 2030.
•   Unilever is setting aside EUR €1 billion to fund nature regeneration projects, including achieving a deforestation-free supply chain, promoting regenerative agriculture, and transitioning to biodegradable ingredients by 2023.
For more commitments from our members, including Nestlé, GSK, LafargeHolcim and Shell, please refer to our website.

Scaling up NCS and NbS
Despite increasing momentum for NbS and NCS, they have yet to achieve their full potential. The Accelerating Business Solutions for Climate and Nature report series aims to support business in overcoming challenges by (1) identifying best practices for natural climate solutions to ensure aligned and collective business action and (2) developing sector guidance for key sectors to help companies deploy nature-based solutions that have a positive impact on nature while also being good for business.

**I. Examples of nature-based solutions18 (non-exhaustive) **

**Ecosystem restoration approaches**

Reforestation
Replanting forests and woodlands that have been depleted, usually through deforestation

Coastal wetland restoration, incl. mangroves
Restoring wetlands to replace lost vegetation and wildlife and/ or repair natural flow of water

Peat restoration 
Restoring peat soils to prevent further breakdown of stored plant material

Coral reef protection 
Reducing human activity that degrades coral reefs

Forest landscape restoration 
Conservation and restoration of forest landscapes to sequester carbon and protect and increase biodiversity.

**Issue-specific ecosystem-related approaches**

Improved plantations 
Extending the rotation harvest cycle of timber plantations to maximize carbon absorption and timber yields

Trees in cropland 
Planting trees for windbreaks, shelter for crops and erosion prevention while reducing carbon emissions

Afforestation 
Establishing a forest or stand of trees in an area where there was no previous tree cover

Deforestation avoidance 
Keeping trees instead of removing them

Regenerative agriculture 
Producing crops while maximizing environmental conservation: minimum soil disturbance, maintenance of permanent soil cover, diversification of plant species

Avoided fuelwood harvest
Avoiding removal of trees for fuelwood by replacing fuel sources

Grazing optimization 
Improving plant species diversity by managing grazing

Nitrogen removal 
Ensuring nitrogen applied to fields does not exceed the amount that can be absorbed and timing application to avoid unnecessary runoff and reapplication

Biochar 
Adding biochar to croplands to improve soil health and help trapped carbon decompose

Improved rice cultivation 
Alternately wetting and drying rice fields or draining flooded fields mid-season to reduce time decaying plant material is submerged to reduce methane emissions

Seaweed aquaculture 
Cultivating seaweed to extract dissolved nutrients to enhance environment for marine organisms

Sustainable fishing 
Harvesting fish at rates that do not transgress sustainable boundaries

**Infrastructure-related approaches**

Groundwater recharge
Adding water to surface water to become groundwater

Green infrastructure 
Using natural systems instead of conventional infrastructure to deliver services such as flood control, water purification, etc.

**Ecosystem-based management approaches**

Natural forest management
Managing forests in a way that is commercially viable but maximizes biodiversity, soil health and landscape value

Fire management
Planning and preventing destructive fires and using fire to attain forestry, wildlife and land-use objectives

**Ecosystem protection approaches**

Avoided peat impact
Avoiding emissions from above- and below-ground biomass and soil carbon due to avoided degradation and/or loss of freshwater wetlands

Avoided coastal impact
protecting coastal areas and ecosystems to avoid erosion, with associated carbon emissions and biodiversity loss, and protect ecosystem services

Avoided grassland conversion
Avoiding soil carbon emissions by avoiding the conversion of grasslands (including savannas and shrublands) to cropland
